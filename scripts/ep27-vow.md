# Episode 27: "Vow"

**One job:** THE title episode. Ash speaks the Vow of Smoke — freely, with full knowledge, on the roof where they can see the sky. The bond transforms. Rune stops dying. Ash becomes permanently human-touched. The series earns its name.
**The moment:** "I dreamed about you for a thousand years before you were born. And the reality is better." — Ash's memory revelation, the sentence the audience replays at 2 AM.

---

[SFX: the water off. Rune drying his face. His footsteps — slower than five weeks ago, careful, the walk of someone conserving what's left] [spot]

[SFX: Ash — at the table. Laminated pages before him. His lips moving without sound, shaping old words his mouth remembers] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — Ash's frequency when Rune enters. The shift immediate. Not the spike of alarm. Something softer. The frequency of attention] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's at the table. The vow texts. He's been reading them for hours and I've been watching him through the bond — the way the words settle into him like water into dry earth. He knows the sounds. Kaya was right about that. The god-eater's memory holds the old language even if the man who wakes up and makes coffee doesn't. [Beat — 800ms]

ASH: The words are mine. I've spoken them before.

RUNE: You remember?

ASH: Not the speaking. The shape of the sounds. My mouth knows them the way your hands know a pulse point. Before the mind gets there.

[SFX: Ash touching the laminated page — his finger tracing a line. Fingertip on plastic-sealed parchment, careful, reverent] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: The vow isn't long. Seven lines. Each one a choice.

RUNE: What do they mean? In our language.

[SFX: Ash — looking up from the text. Meeting Rune's eyes. The bond-hum settling into a frequency that is neither warm nor urgent — just present] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash recites the meaning without reading. His voice is quiet, precise. The modern register. No performance. Just the truth of the words, translated plainly]

ASH: I give back what I am. I take what you are. The fire answers to two. The dark answers to two. What consumed alone now carries together. What burned becomes warmth. I am the smoke. You are the air that holds it.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum — resonating. The words themselves vibrating through the frequency, as though the bond recognizes its own description and responds] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Seven lines. Fourteen seconds to speak. And the meaning underneath the meaning — I give back godhood. I accept mortality. The power that was mine alone now runs through both of us. The hunger that consumed becomes the warmth that sustains. I am the smoke. You are the air that holds it. The most devastating promise I've ever heard, delivered in a kitchen at 4 PM on a Thursday, in the voice of a man who burns toast.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: You're sure.

[SFX: Rune — reaching across the table. His hand covering Ash's. The bond flaring at the touch — not a spike, a widening] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the open configuration from this morning. Both systems exposed. Carrying] [under]

RUNE: Then we go up. Before the light goes.

{Scene 2 — The rooftop. Evening.}

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum — raw. Not the managed hum, not the warm variant, not the alert spike. The frequency with nothing over it. The bond open, no armor between them] [under]

[SFX: Ash — exhaling. Shadows at his feet stilling. The wind moving around him but not through him] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice drops into a register the audience hasn't heard. Not the archaic formal. Not the modern-with-undertow. Something older than both — the voice of a man who has been carrying fragments of a thousand-year dream and is finally setting them down]

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: Before the ritual. Before the god-eater. I was someone. I had a name I can almost hear when the bond is quiet and the city is asleep. And I — there was someone. Before you.

[SFX: bond-hum — a pulse. Slow. The frequency registering the sentence without flinching. Rune holding still] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Before you. Two words that should land like a fist and don't. Because I've felt it. Through the bond, in fragments — a grief that has no memory attached. The howling loss from the night at the shrine, before he knew my name. Someone he lost. Someone the ritual took from him along with everything else.

RUNE: Tell me.

[SFX: Ash — hands in his lap. Scars on his forearms catching the fading light] [spot]

ASH: There was a temple. Stone. Cedar beams. Fire in the altar pit — not the destructive kind, the kind you maintain. The kind that means someone is home. And a person. I can't see the face. [Beat — 800ms]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice thins. Not breaking — thinning. Like a wire being drawn. The vulnerability is in the precision, not the volume]

ASH: They took everything from me. My name. My body. My humanity. And I spent a thousand years in the dark, sealed under stone, dreaming of fire.

[SFX: bond-hum — carrying the sentence to Rune. The frequency of a man speaking about a millennium of sleep the way someone else would speak about a bad year] [under]

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE: What did you dream?

[SFX: Ash — turning. Facing Rune. The full weight of a thousand years of dreaming in the look, carried through the bond as a frequency that has no name] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the frequency widening. Not a spike. An opening. Ash choosing to let Rune feel what the words can't hold] [under]

ASH: A voice calling my name. A hand I couldn't hold. And the feeling that somewhere, across all that time, someone was waiting.

[DIRECTION: Ash's eyes on Rune. The bond fully open. What comes next is delivered not as a confession but as a fact about the universe — quiet, certain, the way gravity works]

ASH: It was you. I dreamed about you for a thousand years before you were born. And the reality is better.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum — the frequency after the sentence. Not a spike, not a surge. Something without precedent. The bond recognizing what it has always carried] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The reality is better. Four words. And I am — I am done. Like it was held up by a breath I've been holding for five weeks and the breath is gone and there's nothing between me and the fact that I love him and the fact that he dreamed me before I existed and the fact that the reality — this reality, the bad coffee and the shaking hands and the blood in the sink and the way he holds my wrist without knowing he's doing it — is better than a thousand years of fire.

[SFX: Rune — pulling Ash to him. Not gently. His hand on the back of Ash's neck. Total contact] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the frequencies colliding. Not stabilizing. Merging. Every managed distance collapsing into a single held body] [under]

[SFX: the kiss — slow. Deliberate. Not the desperate press of crisis. Breath shared. Mouths and closeness and wind on the roof and the city below, irrelevant] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He kisses me back and the bond goes quiet. Not silent. Peaceful. Like a held breath finally released. His heartbeat pulses through my palm on his neck — too slow, too warm, the heartbeat of something that used to be a god. And it's mine. And the city can fall and the cage can break and the solstice can burn and this — his mouth on mine, the warmth, the taste of bad coffee and a thousand years of dreaming — this is the only thing that matters.

[SFX: Ash pulling back — the separation slow. His hand on Rune's face. Thumb along his jaw. The intimacy of the touch audible in the bond-hum, which carries it as a physical frequency] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice when it comes is wrecked — not from emotion performed, but from the rawness of having said the truest thing and been met. The voice of a man on the other side of the wall]

{Scene 3 — The rooftop. The Vow of Smoke.}

[BGM: the cello tone, sustained. A second tone joining — minor third above. Not a melody. A harmonic. The series resonating with itself] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): We stand. The laminated pages are in his coat pocket but he doesn't need them. The words are his. And he's going to speak them in the open air on a rooftop in December because a man who was sealed underground for a millennium deserves to see the sky when he chooses to be human.
