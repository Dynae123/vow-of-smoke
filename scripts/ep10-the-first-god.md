# Episode 10: "The First God"

**One job:** The crisis forces Rune and Ash to work together for the first time. In the aftermath, Ash names what Rune is — not anchor, not leash, but the person who holds people together when they're falling apart. And Rune reaches out. Not through the bond. With his hand. Ash leans into it. The chain becomes a current.
**The moment:** Rune's hand on Ash's shoulder. Ash leaning in. The first touch that isn't crisis, isn't triage. It's a choice.

[AMBIENT: apartment, predawn — deep stillness, fridge hum, faint distant traffic] [bed]

[SFX: subsonic thrum — massive, louder than ever. Deep structural cracking. Bedrock splitting far below. Geological groan] [bed, fade-in]
[DIRECTION: Not felt in the teeth anymore — in the floor, the walls, the bones]

RUNE (inner monologue): I wake up because Ash is already standing. Not at the window this time. In the center of the room, both hands open at his sides, his head tilted like he's listening to something under the floor.

[SFX: ancient voice, vast and fading, reverberating through stone: "We were here first..."] [spot]

[SFX: all ambient sound stops — fridge, traffic, everything. Hard cut to total silence] [spot]
[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE: I have to go.

ASH: I am coming with you.

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it the way he says everything that matters. No room for argument. No question mark. Just the fact and the silence after.

[SFX: Rune grabbing his kit — jacket, keys, door opening and closing] [spot]

{Scene 2 — Central Park disaster site. Dawn. Chaos.}

[AMBIENT: disaster site — layered sirens (near and far), people shouting, ruptured water main hiss, concrete grinding, glass breaking, child crying, first responder radio chatter] [bed]

RUNE (inner monologue): The park looks like a wound. A sinkhole thirty feet across has opened where the main fountain used to be. The ground around it is cracked in concentric rings, like something pushed up from below and then collapsed back. Two buildings on the east perimeter are leaning — not fallen, not yet, but the foundations have shifted. Water is shooting from a ruptured main at the north entrance. And everywhere, people. Running, stumbling, standing in their pajamas staring at the hole in the ground where the park used to be.

[SFX: Mika's voice cutting through the noise — close, controlled] [spot]

MIKA: Rune. Over here. I've got three with injuries from the building tilt — broken glass, one possible fracture. Where's your kit?

RUNE: On me. What's the triage?

MIKA: Lots of walking wounded. Two stretcher cases from the apartments. Fire's handling the structure search. We're taking the perimeter injuries.

[SFX: two sets of footsteps moving quickly. Medical bags clinking] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): Paramedic mode. The switch flips and everything else — the bond, the god-eater, the dying Meridian — drops into a compartment I'll open later. Right now there are people bleeding. That is enough. That has always been enough.

[SFX: knees on ground. Gloves snapping on] [spot]

RUNE: Can you tell me your name?

PATIENT: Sandra. Sandra Mitchell. The window just — it just blew in. I was asleep.

RUNE: Sandra, I'm Rune. I'm going to look at your forehead. Can you follow my finger?

[SFX: triage sounds — gauze tearing, BP cuff inflating. Other paramedics in background] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): I work. Patient after patient. Cuts, bruises, a dislocated shoulder from a man who tried to run down his fire escape when it pulled away from the wall. The injuries are real and mundane and I can help with them. My hands are steady. My voice is calm. This is the version of me that works.

RUNE (inner monologue): Through the bond — a low burn. Ash. He's at the perimeter of the park, inside the police tape. The bond pulses low and warm — a low-grade fever. Present. Elevated. Watching.

{Scene 3 — The disaster site. Mid-morning. Ash acts.}

[AMBIENT: disaster site, controlled phase — less screaming, more radio chatter, generators running, rhythmic heavy equipment] [bed, crossfade from previous]

MIKA (calling across the scene): Rune. Your friend's here.

RUNE (inner monologue): Mika says "friend" the way she says "elevated white count" — technically accurate, flagged for follow-up.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir] [under]
[DIRECTION: Mika's voice should carry a note of disbelief she's suppressing professionally.]

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash is at the east building. The one that's leaning. A fire crew is evacuating the upper floors. A woman is screaming on the second-floor balcony — the fire escape has pulled away from the wall and she's holding a kid. Maybe four years old. The ladder truck is stuck in the sinkhole debris and can't get close enough.

[SFX: woman crying out — distant but clear. Child whimpering. Metal groaning — building shifting] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash is directly below the balcony. I can feel his intention through the bond before I see him move. The shadows around him tighten, darken, reach —

[SFX: heavy fabric-unfurling sound. Metal creaking — fire escape bending, bolts grinding back into place, metal groaning and settling] [spot]
[DIRECTION: SFX team: the metal groan should reverse — stress sounds becoming settling sounds. The fire escape returns to position.]

FIREFIGHTER (distant): What the — it's holding. The escape's holding. Go, go, go —

[SFX: boots on metal stairs. Child lifted, handed down. Woman guided to the ladder] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): He held the fire escape together with shadow and will. Didn't ask. Didn't announce it. Saw a child about to fall and the thing inside him that is supposed to consume gods instead held a rusted staircase together until human hands could do the rest.

[SFX: sharp crack — concrete pathway buckling. Water pipe bursting] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Then the pipe. The ruptured main at the north entrance is getting worse — water pressure surging, the spray arc widening. A crew is trying to get close enough to shut the valve but the water pressure is too high.

[SFX: water rushing — high pressure. Shouts from the crew] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash turns toward it. I feel the shift through the bond — his attention, his focus, something old and precise clicking into place. He raises one hand. Not dramatically. The way I raise a hand to signal Mika across a scene.

[SFX: water sound changing — spray arc narrowing, pressure redirecting, diverted into the sinkhole] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't know if he's controlling the water or the pipe or the earth underneath. I don't think it matters. The crew gets to the valve. The water stops.

RUNE (inner monologue): A firefighter looks at Ash. At the hand he's lowering. At the shadows pooled at his feet like spilled ink. The firefighter opens his mouth, closes it, and goes back to work.

RUNE (inner monologue): Nobody says anything. The crisis is too loud for questions. And Ash — Ash is already moving to the next problem. A gas line. A tilting streetlight. A car wedged into the edge of the sinkhole with someone inside.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps — steady, purposeful] [bridge]
[DIRECTION: Ash's footsteps are purposeful now — no hesitation, moving with direction for the first time.]

{Scene 4 — The disaster site. Near the sinkhole. The hunger.}

[AMBIENT: sinkhole proximity — dissipating divine energy hum (meridian-thrum, weaker), dust, distant rescue activity] [bed, crossfade]

RUNE (inner monologue): The rescue work pushes us closer to the sinkhole as the morning goes on. The buildings are stabilized. Most of the injured are transported or treated. Mika's running the secondary triage. I'm patching a laceration on a firefighter's hand when the bond hits me like a freight train.

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — massive, sudden, overwhelming] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Ash's breathing should go involuntary — deeper, faster, pulled toward something. Voice when he speaks is strained, fighting for control.]

RUNE (inner monologue): The dead god. The great temple god. Its power is still leaking out of the ground — a thousand times stronger than the grove. And Ash is standing twenty feet from the rim.

[SFX: Ash's breathing — labored, strained. Footsteps — one, two — moving toward the edge] [bridge]

RUNE: Ash.

[SFX: gauze dropped. Rune standing, moving fast] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): His hands are shaking. His eyes are dark — not black, not yet, but the color is draining from the irises like ink pulled by a current. The shadows around him are alive, reaching toward the sinkhole. Toward the power.

[SFX: bond roaring — furnace-like intensity, open-flame quality] [under]

RUNE: Ash. Stay with me.

[SFX: Rune's hand closing on Ash's arm — firm grip, fabric and skin] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond flares and I do something I don't understand. Not a decision. A reflex. The same instinct that makes me grab a patient's hand when they're coding — the thing that says *stay here, stay with me, you are not leaving on my watch.* Except this time what I push through the bond isn't medical. It's me. The feeling of being held. Of being seen. Of being chosen over the hunger, over the power, over the nature of what he is.

RUNE (inner monologue): It costs. I feel the drain — a pulling in my chest, a taste of copper, the edges of my vision going white for half a second. The bond taking what I'm offering and routing it through.

[SFX: Ash's breathing changing — evening out. Stance shifting, weight settling back into heels] [under]
[DIRECTION: Ash's breathing evens. The tension in his voice releases — he's back.]

ASH (rough): Rune.

RUNE: I'm here. I've got you.

[SFX: bond-hum-settled — furnace-roar dialing back to steady tone] [under]
[DIRECTION: Rune's grip on Ash's arm is audible — fabric tight, knuckles shifting. Both breathing hard, steadying together.]

ASH: I nearly —

RUNE: You didn't.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: You should not be able to do that. Push through the bond like that. Anchors do not —

RUNE: Yeah, well. I did.

[SFX: sinkhole ambient — dissipating power, distant cleanup. Rune's hand still on Ash's arm] [bed]

RUNE (inner monologue): The gratitude in his face. I've never seen it before. Not on him. He doesn't do gratitude — he does acknowledgment, assessment, the cold cataloguing of facts. But this. His face open, the armor stripped, looking at me like I just pulled him out of a fire.

RUNE (inner monologue): Maybe I did.

{Scene 5 — The disaster site. Afternoon. The ceasefire.}

[AMBIENT: disaster site, winding down — fewer sirens, cleanup beginning, generators, machinery, lower radio chatter] [bed, crossfade]

RUNE (inner monologue): Kaya arrives at two. He looks like he hasn't slept. His coat is buttoned wrong and his hands are shaking and the measured academic composure I've come to expect has been replaced by something I recognize better: fear.

KAYA: Three gods in ten days. The Meridian is destabilizing faster than I projected. This was the central node — the largest, the oldest. Without it, the entire southern grid is unsupported.

RUNE: What does that mean? In plain language, Doctor.

KAYA: It means the ground south of the park will begin to shift. Slowly at first. Then not slowly.

[SFX: vehicle arriving — no engine sound. Multiple footsteps, deliberate, in formation] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Then Thessa arrives. And this time she doesn't come to threaten.

[SFX: Thessa's footsteps — two acolytes flanking. Stopping at the sinkhole edge] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): She stares into the hole. Her face does something I haven't seen before — the cold authority fractures. Just for a second. Then it's back. But I saw it. And through the bond, I feel Ash see it too.

THESSA: Two weeks ago, four gods were still alive.

[Silence — 1500ms]

THESSA: This is not the god-eater's doing. Someone else is killing our gods.

[DIRECTION: Thessa's next line is slower — measured, recalibrating. The contempt is gone. What replaces it is careful.]

THESSA: What are you?

ASH: I am what happens when your gods needed saving and you were not enough.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Nobody breathes. Kaya's hand goes to his collar. Thessa's acolytes shift, hostile, waiting. And Thessa — Thessa looks at Ash the way I imagine she looks at sacred texts she's never seen before. Not hatred. Not yet respect. But the contempt has cracked and what's underneath is the realization that she doesn't know what she's looking at.

THESSA: The god-keepers have been monitoring the remaining sites. Two major nodes remain active. If the summoner targets either, the Meridian will be beyond recovery.

KAYA: Then we need to protect them. Together.

[SFX: wind through the destroyed park. Distant cleanup sounds] [under]

THESSA: The god-keepers do not ally with god-eaters.

ASH: Then your gods die.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: You presume —

ASH: I do not presume. I state. You cannot protect them alone. You could not protect the three who are already gone. Your traditions, your laws, your containment — none of it stopped this. I am the only one who can feel the Meridian's pattern. I am the only one who can predict where the summoner will strike. And I am the only one who stood at the edge of that hole and chose not to feed.

[Silence — 1500ms]
[DIRECTION: Hold the silence. No one fills it. Let the wind and cleanup noise carry the gap.]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't raise his voice. He doesn't need to. The god-keepers have fought the sacred war for centuries with prayer and preservation and it failed. Ash is the ugly truth standing in the ruins of their strategy.

THESSA (after a long pause): Ceasefire. Not alliance. We share information. We protect the remaining sites. The moment the god-eater threatens any god, the arrangement ends.

ASH: Agreed.

RUNE (inner monologue): It isn't trust. It isn't even cooperation. It's two sides of a wall agreeing the fire is worse than each other.

{Scene 6 — Rune's apartment. Night. The aftermath.}

[AMBIENT: apartment night — wounded city outside, quieter than usual, fridge hum, clock ticking] [bed]

[SFX: couch springs creaking. Slow, heavy breathing — a body cataloguing twelve hours of work] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I sit on the couch and everything hurts. Not the bond-drain — that's its own category, the deep pull behind my sternum that's become my new normal. This is the regular kind. Twelve hours of rescue work. Lifting, kneeling, running. My shoulders are locked. My hands are cramping. The bruise on my forearm has darkened another shade and I'm too tired to worry about it.

[SFX: Ash sitting down — on the floor beside the couch. Wood creaking under him] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Not on the couch. Not beside Rune. On the floor, his back against the base of the couch, his shoulder near Rune's knee. Close. Closer than before]

RUNE (inner monologue): He sits on the floor. Not on the couch. Not beside me. On the floor, his back against the base of the couch, his shoulder near my knee. Close. Closer than before. The bond between them hums — different now. Not the wire. Not the current. Something with a rhythm to it. A pulse that wasn't there yesterday.

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — rhythmic, pulse-like quality] [bed]

ASH: You held me together today. Through the bond. I felt you.

RUNE: I didn't know what I was doing.

ASH: You knew exactly what you were doing. You do it for everyone — hold them together when they are falling apart. That is what you are.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it like a revelation. Like he's been studying humans for nine days and finally found the word for what I am. Not anchor. Not leash. Not bloodline or tool or means to an end. The person who holds things together.

RUNE (inner monologue): And the way he looks at me when he says it. The bond warms with the weight of his attention. Like I'm the only solid thing in a world that keeps dissolving.

ASH: They did not plan for you.

RUNE: I'm just a paramedic.

ASH: You are the reason I did not feed today.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment. The bond. His shoulder by my knee. And the sentence sitting between us like a door someone left open.

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — settled, the frequency of shared experience] [bed]

RUNE (inner monologue): I reach out. Not through the bond. Not the instinct-push from the sinkhole. This is my hand. My choice. My bruised, aching hand on his shoulder.

[SFX: fabric shifting — hand on shoulder. Bond tone responds, soft flare, warm, spreading] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He goes completely still. The same stillness from when I bandaged the burn — kindness arriving without a price tag, and his body locking up because it has no protocol for this. Like the thousand-year-old weapon built to consume gods has no subroutine for a hand on his shoulder that means nothing more than *I'm here.*

[SFX: the faintest shift of weight — fabric, a breath] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Ash leans in. An inch. Maybe less. Like someone remembering how gravity works]

RUNE (inner monologue): He leans into it. Just barely. Like someone remembering how to accept a touch.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond hums. Steady. Warm.

[SFX: bond-hum-settled — not the jagged current of the first days, not the roaring furnace of the sinkhole. Quieter. With a heartbeat] [bed, fade-out slowly]

RUNE (inner monologue): His shoulder under my hand. The warmth of him. The way the bond settled when I touched him. Outside, the city is wounded and something ancient is hunting us. But this is the first thing that's felt right in weeks.

[SFX: brief flash of nightmare sound — fire roar, chanting, abruptly cut] [spot]
[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): The dream flickers behind my eyes. The fire. The temple. The hand slipping. But tonight the hand doesn't slip. Tonight the hand holds.

[AMBIENT: apartment at night — breathing, bond-hum-warm faintly, stillness] [bed, fade-out over episode end]
