# Episode 6: "The God-Keepers"

**One job:** Name the cost. Thessa tells Rune the bond will kill him. Ash's silence confirms it. Then in the dark, Ash breaks — not with fury, but with the quiet admission that he can feel Rune dying. The power balance inverts: the weapon is afraid for the chain.
**The moment:** Ash, sitting in the dark, tracing his own scars: "I can feel you dying. In the bond. It's slow. But I can feel it." And then: "I do not want to feel that." Rune doesn't know if it's concern or complaint. He sits down anyway.

---

{Scene 1 — Darkness. A fragment of memory. Ash's voice, unguarded.}

[SFX: silence. Then — the faintest crackle of fire. Not modern fire. Something older, ritualistic. Cedar burning]

ASH (not speaking to anyone — surfacing, like a diver breaking water): There was a temple. There was a fire. There was —

[beat]

[SFX: the memory collapses. The sound cuts — abrupt, like a recording snapped in half. Silence]

ASH (barely audible): Nothing.

---

{Scene 2 — Rune's apartment doorway. Continuous from Episode 5. Thessa at the door.}

[SFX: the hallway fluorescent buzzing. Three people breathing. The bond humming, agitated]

THESSA: You are the anchor.

RUNE: And you are?

THESSA: Thessa. Keeper of the order in this city. I am not here for you, mortal. Step aside.

RUNE: No.

[beat]

THESSA: You do not understand what you are protecting.

RUNE: Then explain it.

THESSA: A god-eater is not a person. It is a weapon — forged through a forbidden ritual, designed to consume the divine. The ritual has not been performed in over a thousand years. It was prohibited for good reason. Whatever you believe you found in that shrine, whatever attachment you have formed — it is the bond manipulating you. Nothing more.

[SFX: behind Rune — Ash's voice. Low. Controlled. The temperature rising]

ASH: She speaks of me as if I am not present.

THESSA: I speak of you as what you are.

ASH: And what am I?

THESSA: An abomination. A violation of sacred law. Someone performed the awakening rite — someone with knowledge stolen from the order. You are evidence of that crime. And you are dangerous.

[SFX: the shadows in the apartment bending hard toward Ash. The lamp buzzing, straining. Thessa's acolytes shifting — a step back]

ASH: Try it.

RUNE: Ash.

ASH: She came to cage me. Let her try.

THESSA (to Rune, ignoring Ash): We are not here to destroy it. We are here for containment. The god-eater will be sealed — safely, humanely — until we determine who performed the ritual and why.

RUNE: Him.

THESSA: Excuse me?

RUNE: You keep saying "it." He has a name.

[beat]

THESSA: Names do not change nature.

RUNE: And containment doesn't fix whatever's killing gods. A building collapsed downtown. A god died. You know this. You were probably there before we were.

[SFX: a flicker — Thessa's composure cracking. Barely. The acolytes exchanging a glance]

THESSA: The god-deaths are precisely why the god-eater must be contained. The Meridian is destabilizing. Every dead god weakens the network. And in the center of this crisis stands a being designed to consume divine power. You cannot see the danger because the bond will not let you.

RUNE: I can see the danger fine. I'm also seeing the only person who felt the god die before anyone else did. Who knelt on the warm ground and mourned something he had no memory of. That's not a weapon. That's a witness.

[SFX: silence. The bond between Rune and Ash — still, for the first time. Held breath]

THESSA: Sentiment. You are choosing sentiment over survival.

RUNE: I'm choosing to not hand over a person to people who don't think he's one.

[beat]

THESSA: Then hear what the bond will cost you.

---

{Scene 3 — Rune's apartment. The cost.}

[SFX: Thessa stepping into the doorway. Not inside. Just across the threshold. Her voice drops — quieter, more precise. This is not a threat. This is a diagnosis]

THESSA: The anchor bond is parasitic in its default state. The god-eater's power draws on the anchor's life force. This is not a theory. It is documented across every text the order preserves. Every anchor who did not sever the bond in time — every one of them — was consumed.

RUNE: Consumed.

THESSA: Depleted. Burned through. The god-eater's power is too vast for a mortal body to sustain. The bond drains the anchor — slowly at first, then faster. Fatigue. Weakness. Then the body begins to fail. Months, perhaps a year. Then the anchor dies, and the god-eater becomes untethered and unstable.

[beat]

THESSA: He will eat you from the inside out. That is his nature.

[SFX: behind Rune — stillness. Ash has gone completely silent. The shadows have stopped moving. The temperature has dropped]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't turn around. I don't have to. The bond tells me everything. He heard. And whatever he's feeling right now — it's not rage. It's something colder. Something that turned inward.

THESSA: We are not your enemy, Mr. Alessi. We are the only people who understand what has been done to you. Surrender the god-eater. We will sever the bond. You will survive.

[beat]

RUNE: And what happens to him?

THESSA: Containment. Indefinite.

RUNE: A cage.

THESSA: A mercy. Compared to what he will do to you if the bond continues.

[beat]

RUNE: No.

THESSA: You are making a mistake.

RUNE: Probably. But it's my mistake.

[SFX: Thessa stepping back. The doorway. The fluorescent hallway]

THESSA (to her acolytes): We leave. For now.

[SFX: footsteps retreating. The elevator at the end of the hall — the ding, the doors opening]

THESSA (from down the hall, not turning): The offer remains open. When the symptoms begin — and they will — you know where to find us.

[SFX: elevator doors closing. Silence]

---

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. After. The question.}

[SFX: the door closing. The deadbolt. Rune's hand on the lock. Then — turning]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment is different. The temperature has dropped. The shadows are flat, ordinary. Ash is standing by the window, his back to me, and the space between us feels like an ocean.

RUNE: Is that true?

[beat]

RUNE: Ash. Is it true? The bond — will it kill me?

[SFX: nothing. The fridge. The clock. The silence where an answer should be]

RUNE (inner monologue): His silence is the loudest thing in the room. I've learned this about him in five days — he doesn't lie. He withholds. And when the answer is the thing he's most afraid of, he goes quiet. Like if he doesn't say it, it isn't happening.

RUNE: I need you to answer me.

ASH (still facing the window): The bond draws on you. I have felt it since the shrine. A current that flows one direction.

RUNE: And it's killing me.

[beat]

ASH: I do not know the timeline. But the drain is real.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it flat. Clinical. The way I give patients bad news — strip the emotion, deliver the fact, deal with the fallout after. He learned that from me. I can hear my own cadence in his voice and I hate it.

RUNE: Why didn't you tell me?

ASH: What would you have done?

RUNE: I don't know. Something.

ASH: There is nothing to do. The bond exists. You are the anchor. I am what I am.

RUNE: Kaya says it can be managed. That both parties have to —

ASH: Your professor theorizes from texts. I feel it in my body. The draw is constant. It does not ask permission.

[beat]

RUNE: So what — I just wait? Count down the months?

[SFX: Ash turning from the window. His footsteps — two, three — then stopping. Too far away. Deliberately too far away]

ASH: You could give me to them. The god-keepers. They sever the bond. You survive.

RUNE: And they lock you up.

ASH: That is not your concern.

RUNE: The hell it isn't.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He looks at me. I look at him. The distance between us is six feet and a thousand years and one parasitic bond that is apparently going to kill me. His face is unreadable. His silence is unreadable. Everything about him is built to be unreadable and I am reading him anyway because the lock behind my sternum won't let me stop.

RUNE: We're going to Kaya tomorrow. Both of us. He said there are precedents — anchors who survived. We find out how. That's the plan.

ASH: That is not a plan. That is hope.

RUNE: Yeah. It is.

[SFX: the bond between them. Humming. Steady. The sound of two people who don't know how to fix this and aren't leaving]

---

{Scene 5 — Rune's apartment. Late. Kaya on the phone.}

[SFX: Rune in the kitchen. Speaking low. Ash in the other room]

RUNE: She said months. Maybe a year.

KAYA (on phone): Thessa is not wrong about the mechanics. The default bond is parasitic. But she is wrong about the inevitability.

RUNE: How?

KAYA: The old texts describe a rite. A vow. Spoken freely by the god-eater. It restructures the bond from one-directional to reciprocal. Energy flows both ways. Neither party depletes the other.

RUNE: A vow.

KAYA: The Vow of Smoke. It is the oldest of the binding rites. There is a reason the order prohibits it — the vow permanently anchors the god-eater's humanity. It forecloses the possibility of full divine ascension. The god-eater becomes mortal-vulnerable. Ash would gain stability but lose the potential for unlimited power.

RUNE: And the anchor?

KAYA: The anchor survives. The bond becomes self-sustaining.

[beat]

RUNE: So there's a way.

KAYA: There is a way that requires the god-eater to choose, freely, to give up godhood for personhood. You understand — this cannot be coerced. Cannot be suggested. Cannot be implied. The vow must come from him, or it does not work.

RUNE (inner monologue): Freely. He wants Ash to freely choose to give up everything he is. Ash, who doesn't remember who he was. Who doesn't know what he's lost. Who is learning what a phone is and can't operate a stove.

RUNE: And if he doesn't choose it?

KAYA: Then we look for another way. I am not prepared to accept inevitability, Rune. Not yet.

[SFX: phone clicking off]

RUNE (inner monologue): I stand in my kitchen. The Vow of Smoke. A choice Ash has to make that I can never ask him to make. A door I know exists that I cannot open for him.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I put the phone down. I don't tell Ash about the vow. Not tonight. Not yet. He has enough doors he can't open.

---

{Scene 6 — Rune's apartment. Deep night. The dark.}

[SFX: the apartment at its quietest. 3 AM. No traffic. No fridge cycle. Just breathing and the hum]

RUNE (inner monologue): I can't sleep. The bond is doing the thing it does at night — amplifying. Every breath he takes in the other room registers in my chest. His stillness has a weight. His silence has a temperature.

[SFX: Rune getting up. Footsteps — bare feet on the floor. Walking toward the living room]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't know why I get up. The paramedic in me says: check on the patient. The rest of me says something less clinical.

[SFX: the living room. No lights. The window casting a pale rectangle on the floor. Ash's breathing — present, close]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's sitting on the floor. Not the couch — the floor, against the wall, in the dark. His sleeves are pushed up. He's tracing the scars on his own arms with his fingertips. Following the lines like he's reading a language he's forgotten.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't look up.

ASH (quietly): I can feel you dying. In the bond. It is slow. But I can feel it.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The words land in the dark and stay there.

ASH: It is not like the gods. When a god dies, there is a void. A sudden absence. This is — the opposite. A gradual thinning. Your signal getting weaker. Day by day.

[beat]

ASH: I do not want to feel that.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't know what that is. Concern or complaint. Whether he's saying *I care about you* or *your dying is inconvenient.* Whether the god-eater is mourning the anchor or mourning the leash.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): It doesn't matter. Because the way he said it — the pause before *I do not want* — that pause held more than he's said in five days.

[SFX: Rune sitting down. Not on the couch. On the floor, against the opposite wall. The same height. The same dark]

RUNE (inner monologue): I sit down. I don't say anything. He doesn't say anything. The bond hums between us like a wire stretched across the room — taut, vibrating, the note it makes not music but something more honest. The sound of two people held together by something neither chose, sitting in the dark because the alternative is sitting in the dark alone.

[SFX: the hum. Steady. Patient. The apartment around them, small and ordinary and holding something that is neither]

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): We stay like that. Not speaking. Not touching. Him on one side, me on the other, the bond running between us like a fault line — the kind that holds the ground together right up until it doesn't.

[SFX: the hum fades to near-silence. Then — under it, barely audible — the subsonic thrum. The city. The Meridian. Something old and vast and cracking, far below]

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): Somewhere out there, someone is killing gods. Somewhere out there, the god-keepers are deciding what to do about us. Somewhere in me, a timer is counting down that I didn't set and can't stop.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): And the man across the room — the weapon, the monster, the thing that is going to eat me alive — he is sitting in the dark tracing scars he can't read, and he told me he can feel me dying, and his voice cracked on the word *want.*

[SFX: the hum. The dark. The sound of something that will not break tonight]
