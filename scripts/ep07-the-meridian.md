# Episode 7: "The Meridian"

**One job:** Shift Rune's perception of Ash from dangerous unknown to someone in pain. The hunger is not predatory — it is shameful. And shame, Rune knows, is the most human thing there is.
**The moment:** Ash asks why Rune isn't afraid of him. Rune says he is. Then doesn't leave. The distinction between afraid OF and afraid FOR is the whole episode.

---

[SFX: city traffic, steady [bed]; underneath, a low resonance — subsonic, felt in the back teeth, not consciously heard [bed]; water pipes groaning, distant [under]]

RUNE (inner monologue): The dogs woke me up. Every dog in the building, the block, maybe the whole east side — barking at 6 AM like something walked through the walls. Then nothing.

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash was already standing at the window. He'd felt it before the dogs did.

[Beat — 800ms]

{Scene 2 — The dead grove. A neighborhood park. Morning.}

[AMBIENT: dead park — no birdsong, no wind through leaves, no insect hum; flat, muffled silence]

[SFX: car doors closing [spot]; footsteps on pavement then on dead grass — dry, brittle, crunching like old paper [bridge]]

RUNE (inner monologue): The park is four blocks from the apartment. I've run through it. There's a playground on the west end and a row of oaks that've been here longer than the neighborhood. Mika and I used to eat lunch on the bench by the fountain when we pulled south-side shifts.

[SFX: footsteps stopping [spot]]
[DIRECTION: Total absence of natural sound — no birdsong, no wind rustle. Dead air.]

RUNE (inner monologue): The oaks are gone. Not cut down. Not burned. Just — dead. Standing dead, every leaf black, the bark split and grey like they aged a hundred years overnight. The grass is charcoal. The fountain is dry. And in the center of the grove, a circle of earth where the ground is cracked open and warm.

[SFX: murmuring voices nearby — residents gathering at the edge of the park [under]]

RESIDENT 1: What happened to the trees—

RESIDENT 2: They were fine yesterday—

RUNE: Stay back. Could be a gas leak or — something.

RESIDENT: A gas leak doesn't kill every tree in a park overnight.

RUNE (inner monologue): No. It doesn't.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps on the dead grass — deliberate, steady, moving toward the center of the grove [bridge]]
[DIRECTION: Ash walks as though gravity shifted for him — pulled toward the center.]

RUNE: Ash. Wait.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps reaching the center, then stopping [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): He stands in the middle of the dead grove and something happens to his face. I've seen him hostile. I've seen him confused, withdrawn, furious. I have not seen this.

RUNE (inner monologue): His hands are shaking.

[SFX: meridian-thrum — low, close, intimate; divine residue dissipating [bed]]

RUNE (inner monologue): The god's power is still here. Leaking out of the ground like heat off asphalt. Dissipating. And his body — every cell of whatever he is — wants it. I can feel the pull through the bond. Not his choice. His nature. The hunger hitting him like a wave and him standing in it trying not to drown.

[SFX: Ash's breathing changing — rough, labored [under]]
[DIRECTION: Ash's breathing should sound involuntary — fast inhales catching in the throat, jaw clenched, fighting to swallow.]

ASH (strained): Rune.

RUNE: I'm here.

ASH: Do not let me stay here.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's asking me to pull him away from the thing his body was built to consume. He's standing in the center of a dead god's last breath and he's asking for help. Not from a position of weakness. From a position of war — the one he's fighting against himself.

[SFX: Rune stepping forward [spot]; his hand closing on Ash's arm — skin to skin [spot]]
[SFX: bond-hum-surge — a rush of shared sensation [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond hits me and this time what comes through isn't rage. Isn't confusion.

RUNE (inner monologue): Shame. He is ashamed of the hunger. Of what he is. Of what he wants to do to the fading power in the ground. The shame is so thick it has a texture — heavy, old, the kind you carry in your spine because your mind can't hold it.

RUNE: We're leaving. Now.

[SFX: footsteps — Ash unsteady, Rune's grip firm; dead grass crunching; moving toward the street [bridge]]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't let go of his arm. Not at the edge of the grove. Not on the sidewalk. Not until we're two blocks away and his breathing levels and the shaking stops and the bond settles from a scream into a hum. He doesn't pull away. He doesn't comment on it. He walks where I lead him and he lets me hold on.

RUNE (inner monologue): The god-keepers called him a weapon. Kaya calls him a being. The old texts call him a god-eater.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's a man fighting not to eat.

{Scene 3 — Rune's apartment. Afternoon. Tea.}

[AMBIENT: apartment interior — fridge hum, muted traffic through glass, afternoon quiet]

[SFX: apartment door closing [spot]; kettle being filled — water running [spot]; stove clicking on [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): I make tea because I don't know what else to do. There is no triage protocol for this. No checklist. A god died in a park four blocks from here and the man on my couch wanted to consume what was left and I pulled him away with my hand on his arm and now I'm boiling water.

[SFX: kettle heating [bed]; Ash shifting on the couch — cushion creak [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): He hasn't spoken since the grove. He's sitting on the couch with his arms on his knees and his head down and the scars on his forearms catching the afternoon light through the window. He looks like a man who lost a fight.

[SFX: kettle clicking off [spot]; water being poured [spot]; mug set on the coffee table [spot]]

RUNE: Here.

[SFX: Ash picking up the mug — ceramic against his palms [spot]]
[DIRECTION: No flinch, no blow to cool it. He drinks immediately — the heat is nothing to him.]

RUNE (inner monologue): He holds the mug and stares at it like it's a foreign artifact. Both hands, thumbs overlapping on the ceramic. Like the thing might break if he's careless. Like he's still learning the pressure required for objects that aren't weapons.

ASH (quietly): Why are you not afraid of me?

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: I am.

[SFX: bond-hum-neutral — low, steady [bed]; fridge cycling [under]]

ASH: Then why are you still here?

RUNE (inner monologue): Because being afraid of someone and being afraid for someone are two different things. And I crossed that line somewhere between the shrine and the tea.

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't say this. I sit down across from him and pick up my own mug and drink. He watches me. I drink. The bond hums between us — low, steady, still bruised from the grove.

[SFX: Rune drinking [spot]; ceramic set on the table [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't ask again. After a while he drinks too. The tea is bad — I never measure the leaves right, too strong, too bitter. He doesn't complain. He drinks it like it's the most important thing he's ever tasted.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): We sit in my apartment and drink bad tea and don't talk about the dead god or the hunger or the shame I felt through his skin. We just sit. The afternoon shifts. The light changes. He's on one side of the coffee table and I'm on the other and neither of us moves.

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. Evening. Kaya's theory.}

[AMBIENT: apartment evening — darker, quieter traffic, fridge hum, evening settling in]

[SFX: Rune's phone ringing [spot]; he picks up [spot]]

RUNE: Doctor.

KAYA (on phone): I have been analyzing the sacred site data. The grove on the south side — you were there today.

RUNE: Yeah. It's dead. Everything in it.

KAYA: The grove god was one of the older nodes in the southern grid. Its domain was biological — plant growth, soil health, the root systems that stabilize the neighborhood's foundations. Without it, the area will begin to erode. Sinkholes. Subsidence. Slow, but certain.

RUNE: How many is that now?

KAYA: Two confirmed deaths since Ash's awakening. The shrine god was already gone — that site was dormant. The Meridian Temple god was killed ten days ago. The grove god died this morning. Two active kills in ten days.

KAYA: Rune, I have been mapping the deaths against the old Meridian charts. The sacred sites form a network — a grid of divine energy running through the city's bedrock. The ancients called it the Meridian. Each god is a node. The connections between them hold the system stable.

RUNE: And someone's pulling out the nodes.

KAYA: In sequence. The Meridian Temple was a mid-level node. The grove was a secondary. If I am reading the pattern correctly, the kills are moving from the periphery inward. Weaker sites first. Building toward the major nodes at the center.

RUNE: What happens if the center goes?

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: The old texts are not specific. But they describe the collapse of a Meridian in another city — a city that no longer exists. The text says the ground opened.

KAYA: I do not mean metaphorically.

[SFX: phone against Rune's ear [under]]
[DIRECTION: Rune's voice drops — private, the phone pressed close. In the background, faint couch creak from Ash in the other room.]

RUNE (inner monologue): Someone is killing gods in order. Drawing a line through the city's foundations and pulling the thread. And in the middle of it, a man who was made to eat what they're killing.

RUNE: Can Ash help? Can he — I don't know — feel where the next one will be?

KAYA: Possibly. The god-eater's sensitivity to divine energy is unmatched. If he can identify the pattern from the Meridian's resonance, he may be able to predict the next target. But you understand, Rune — every time he is near a dying god, the hunger will be worse. You felt it today through the bond. It will escalate.

RUNE: I know.

KAYA: And the bond's drain on you will accelerate in proximity to divine events. Your body is not designed to channel what he channels. You understand — every time you pull him back, you pay for it.

RUNE: I know that too.

[SFX: phone clicking off [spot]]

RUNE (inner monologue): I stand in the kitchen and I look at the man on my couch. He's put the mug down. He's looking at his hands — at the scars, at the fingers that shook in the grove. He doesn't know I'm watching.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Two gods dead. A pattern. A countdown. And Kaya's voice saying *every time you pull him back, you pay for it* — like the cost is in the pulling and not in the letting go.
