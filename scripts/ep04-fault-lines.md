# Episode 4: "Fault Lines"

**One job:** Show Ash as something other than hostile — the first glimpse of grief beneath the armor — while Rune is pulled between his paramedic life and the thing growing behind his sternum.
**The moment:** Ash kneels on the warm earth where a god died. The hostility falls away. What's left is grief so old it predates his memory. Rune sees him — really sees him — for the first time.

---

{Scene 1 — Darkness. News audio. The collapse.}

[SFX: overlapping radio reports — tinny, urgent, layered on top of each other. Different stations, different anchors, the same disaster]

NEWS VOICE 1 (radio-filtered): — building collapse at the corner of Meridian and Fourth, downtown. Emergency crews are on the scene —

NEWS VOICE 2 (radio-filtered): — no structural explanation at this time. The three-story residential walk-up appears to have folded in on itself —

NEWS VOICE 3 (radio-filtered): — at least seven confirmed casualties, dozens injured, search and rescue ongoing —

[SFX: the radio reports blur together, then cut. Silence. Then the subsonic thrum — louder than before. A sound like stone exhaling]

RUNE (inner monologue): Four hours ago, Kaya said *it is beginning.*

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm starting to think he meant it literally.

---

{Scene 2 — Collapse site. Downtown. Dust and sirens. Paramedic mode.}

[SFX: ambulance doors slamming. Boots on rubble. Sirens — multiple, overlapping. The crunch and groan of unstable debris. Voices shouting. Dust. The roar of a rescue crew working against time]

MIKA: Rune, east side. Two trapped, one responsive.

RUNE: Copy.

[SFX: Rune climbing over rubble. Medical bag clinking. His breathing — controlled, steady, the rhythm of someone who does this]

RUNE (inner monologue): Back in my element. The part of the world that makes sense. There's rubble and there's people under it and I know what to do with both.

[SFX: a woman crying, somewhere close. Rune kneeling. Debris being shifted by hand]

RUNE: Ma'am. I'm Rune, I'm a paramedic. Can you tell me where you're hurt?

WOMAN (panicked, dusty): My leg — I can't feel my leg. And my husband, he was next to me, he was right next to me —

RUNE: We're going to get you both out. I need you to stay still. Can you do that for me?

WOMAN: He was right there —

RUNE: I know. Stay still.

[SFX: Rune working — tearing open a dressing, stabilizing. The practiced sounds of emergency medicine]

RUNE (inner monologue): I work for hours. Mika on the west side, me on the east. Triage, stabilize, hand off. Triage, stabilize, hand off. The building came down clean — straight into its own footprint, like it was built to collapse. No earthquake. No gas leak. The engineers are already arguing about it on the radio.

[SFX: radio chatter — fragments of confused structural assessment. "No seismic activity recorded—" "Foundation just gave way—"]

RUNE (inner monologue): I know what brought the building down. The engineers don't. And I can't tell them.

---

{Scene 3 — Collapse site perimeter. Later. Ash watching from the edge.}

[SFX: the rescue noise fading to background. Rune walking away from the rubble, peeling off gloves. Heavy breathing. Then — he feels it. The bond. A tug behind his sternum]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's here. I don't have to look. I know exactly where he's standing — north side, behind the tape, away from the lights. The bond tells me before my eyes do.

[SFX: Rune's footsteps changing from rubble to pavement. Approaching the perimeter]

MIKA (calling from behind): Rune.

[SFX: Rune stopping]

MIKA: Who's the guy?

RUNE: What guy?

MIKA: The one you keep looking at. Tall. Angry-looking. Standing by the barricade like he's deciding whether to eat it.

RUNE: Long story.

MIKA: You don't have long stories. You have work.

RUNE: Mika —

MIKA: You've looked at him four times in the last hour. You look at patients less. I'm asking.

[beat]

RUNE: He's someone I'm helping. Memory loss. He doesn't have anywhere to go.

MIKA: Uh-huh. And he followed you to a building collapse.

RUNE: He's — it's complicated.

MIKA: Everything with you is "complicated" or "fine." Neither is ever true.

[SFX: Mika pulling off her own gloves. Snapping them into a disposal bag]

MIKA: Go check on your guy. I've got the handoff.

RUNE (inner monologue): She doesn't push harder. Not because she's satisfied. Because she's triaging — and right now, whatever I'm not telling her is less urgent than the seventeen people we just pulled out of a building. She'll come back to it. She always comes back to it.

---

{Scene 4 — The ruins. Foundation exposed. The warm earth.}

[SFX: the collapse site, quieter now. Most of the rescue crews have moved on. Generators humming. Ash's footsteps — deliberate, drawn — moving into the rubble field. Rune following]

RUNE: Ash. Hey. The site isn't cleared yet.

[SFX: Ash doesn't stop. His footsteps descending — the ground slopes where the foundation cracked open. Loose stone shifting]

RUNE: What are you doing?

ASH: Something died here.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it the way I'd say *someone stopped breathing.* Clinical. But underneath it — through the bond — I feel something else. A pull. Not the hunger. Something quieter.

[SFX: they reach the bottom — the exposed foundation. The ambient sound changes: deeper, enclosed. The subsonic hum is louder here, resonating through the stone]

RUNE (inner monologue): The collapse tore the building down to its bones. Beneath the concrete foundation, older stone. Carved. Ancient. Symbols I've seen before — on his skin.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps stopping. Silence except for the hum]

RUNE (inner monologue): And in the center of the ruin, a patch of earth. The concrete is gone, the old stone is cracked open, and there's just — ground. Bare ground that shouldn't be warm in October.

[SFX: Ash kneeling. The soft sound of a hand pressing into earth]

RUNE (inner monologue): He kneels. Puts his hand flat on the warm earth. And the thing I've been watching for two days — the hostility, the coiled menace, the predator calculating every exit — it drops.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): What's left is older than him. Older than whatever he used to be. It's grief. Sourceless, bottomless, the kind you carry in your body because your mind can't hold it. I know that face. I've seen it on families in waiting rooms. I've never seen it on him.

ASH (quietly): There was a god here. It is gone now.

RUNE: Gone where?

ASH: Nowhere. Just... gone. Consumed. Not by me.

[SFX: Ash pulling his hand back from the earth. His breathing unsteady for the first time]

ASH: Something else is eating them.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He looks at me. The fury is gone. The calculation is gone. He's just a man kneeling in the dirt where something died, and his hand is shaking, and I want to help him and I don't know how. My training doesn't cover this. There's no triage protocol for a god-eater grieving a god he never met.

RUNE: Can you feel what happened? To the god?

ASH: Fragments. It was old. It remembered the river that used to run here, before the stone, before the buildings. It held the ground together.

[beat]

ASH: And someone tore it out.

[SFX: a siren in the distance. The modern world, still there. Still indifferent]

RUNE: We should go. The engineers will be back in the morning.

[SFX: Ash standing. Unsteady. His footsteps slower than before]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't argue. That's how I know it's bad. The Ash who argues, who bites back, who treats the world like an enemy — that Ash isn't here. This is someone running on something deeper than anger. And it's not enough to keep him upright.

---

{Scene 5 — Rune's apartment. Evening. Kaya on the phone.}

[SFX: apartment door closing. The familiar fridge hum. Rune setting down his bag. Then his phone ringing]

RUNE: Doctor.

KAYA (on phone): I have been researching the collapse site. The building on the corner of Meridian and Fourth — you were there today, yes?

RUNE: Yeah. Seven hours.

KAYA: Then you felt it. The residue.

RUNE: Ash felt it. I felt Ash.

[beat]

KAYA: The Meridian Temple was one of the oldest sacred sites in the city. The god bound to it — we called it the River's Memory. It sustained the geological stability of the entire downtown corridor. Without it, the bedrock is exposed. Vulnerable.

RUNE: In plain language.

KAYA: More buildings will fall, Rune. Not today. Not this week, perhaps. But the ground beneath that district is no longer held. The god was load-bearing. You understand — the infrastructure is secondary. The earth itself has lost its anchor.

RUNE (inner monologue): He says "anchor" and my hand goes to my chest. Reflex.

KAYA: There is more. I have been cross-referencing the sacred sites mapped in the old texts with the city's modern geography. The collapse at Meridian and Fourth is the second event. The shrine where you found Ash — that was the first. The god bound to that site was already gone. Consumed, or faded, or both.

RUNE: So someone's targeting these sites.

KAYA: The sites form a network. A grid — the ancients called it the Meridian. Each god is a node. The connections between them stabilize the entire system. If enough nodes fail —

[beat]

RUNE: How many is enough?

KAYA: That is what I am trying to determine. There are — or were — dozens of active sites. Many have faded naturally over centuries. But the major nodes... if the major nodes are destroyed in sequence...

[beat]

KAYA: I do not want to speculate without more data. But the pattern suggests intent. This is not natural decay. Someone is killing gods, Rune. And they are doing it in a specific order.

[SFX: Rune exhaling. The phone against his ear. In the next room — Ash, silent]

RUNE: I'll come by tomorrow.

KAYA: Bring him. I have more questions about what he sensed at the site.

[SFX: phone clicking off]

---

{Scene 6 — Rune's apartment. Night. The bond does something new.}

[SFX: the apartment at night. Rune in bed. Sheets shifting. The steady hum of the bond — low, constant, like a transformer on the other side of a wall]

RUNE (inner monologue): I can't sleep. Not the nightmare — that's almost familiar by now. This is different. I'm too aware of him. He's in the living room. He hasn't moved in an hour. He's facing the window. I know this the way I know my own pulse.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond has been getting louder since the collapse site. Since he touched the warm earth and I felt his grief through my own ribs. Like the frequency changed. Like it found a new station.

[SFX: Rune's breathing slowing. The drift toward sleep. Then — a shift. A lurch. Like being pulled sideways through a wall]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm standing at the window.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): No. *He's* standing at the window. I'm seeing through his eyes. The city below — all those lights, all those people, all those buildings balanced on the bones of things they've forgotten. And underneath the view, a hunger. Not for food. For the power dissipating from the dead god's site, still leaking into the bedrock. A pull downward, like gravity but specific. His body wants to go to where the god died and drink what's left.

[SFX: a sharp gasp — Rune snapping back into his own body. His own bed. His own breathing, ragged]

[beat]

ASH (from the other room, flat): Stay out of my head.

RUNE (inner monologue): I didn't go in. It pulled me.

[SFX: silence. The hum between them — louder now. Agitated. Two people trying not to feel each other and failing]

RUNE (inner monologue): I lie in the dark and I count my breaths and I try not to think about the hunger I felt through his eyes. Not because it was alien. Because for a second, it made sense. The pull toward the dead god's power — I understood it. The way you understand thirst.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): And that is not something I want to understand.

[SFX: the hum. Low. Steady. The sound of a bond that is learning new tricks neither of them asked for]
