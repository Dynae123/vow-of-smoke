# Episode 8: "Pulse"

**One job:** The bond stops being a thing that happens to them and becomes a thing they live inside. Mika sees what Rune can't name. Ash asks the question Rune can't answer. And on the roof, Ash looks at something beautiful and the audience knows it isn't the city.
**The moment:** Ash on the rooftop, looking at the city: "I do not remember what I was. Before. But I remember this — standing somewhere high and looking at something beautiful and knowing it would not last." He was looking at Rune.

---

{Scene 1 — Rune's apartment. Morning. Day eight.}

[SFX: the apartment. Morning. Coffee brewing — the gurgle of a cheap drip machine. Pages turning in the other room. Not fast. Normal speed]

RUNE (inner monologue): Day eight. He's still on my couch. He's stopped trying to disassemble my kitchen appliances. He reads instead. Last night I caught him reading my paramedic training manual and muttering about how primitive human anatomy is.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): But he'd bookmarked the page on CPR.

[SFX: Rune pouring coffee. Walking to the living room doorway. Leaning against the frame]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond is different now. Not the jagged surges from the first days — the empathic flashes that came and went like electrical faults. This is constant. A low current running between us like a wire under the floor. I know where he is in the apartment without looking. I know when he's hungry. When he's restless. When he's thinking too hard about something painful — the frequency shifts, gets heavier, and my chest tightens in response.

[SFX: Ash turning a page. The creak of the couch]

RUNE (inner monologue): And it goes both ways. He knows when my nightmares start before I do. Knows when I come home from a bad call. Two days ago I lost a patient — cardiac arrest in the ambulance, couldn't get the rhythm back — and when I walked through the door, Ash looked at me and said nothing and put a mug of tea on the counter. He made it too strong. He always makes it too strong. But he made it before I was through the door, which means he tasted the grief on me through the bond before I'd taken off my jacket.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): Neither of us talks about it. The constant awareness. The way the apartment has become a single nervous system with two bodies. The way I've started waking up three seconds before he does because his waking up wakes me up. The proximity is unbearable and essential in equal measure and I have stopped trying to figure out which.

[SFX: a knock at the door. Not sharp like the god-keepers. Familiar. Assertive. The knock of someone who's been here before]

RUNE (inner monologue): That's Mika's knock.

[SFX: Rune setting down his coffee. Walking to the door. Opening it]

MIKA: Hey. Off-shift. Brought food.

[SFX: a bag rustling. Mika walking in — boots, decisive footsteps. The door closing]

MIKA: I tried calling. Twice. You didn't pick up, which means you were either asleep, dead, or avoiding me. You're not dead, so —

RUNE: I was sleeping.

MIKA: At 11 AM?

RUNE: I worked a double.

MIKA: You always work doubles. You still answer your phone.

[SFX: Mika stopping. Her footsteps halting. She's seen Ash]

[beat]

MIKA: Hi.

[SFX: the couch creaking. Ash standing]

ASH: You are Mika.

MIKA: I am. And you're the long story.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): She looks at him the way she looks at new patients — quick, thorough, filing details without appearing to stare. His height. The scars visible at his wrists and neck. The way the shadows in the room bend slightly wrong. She notices. She notices everything. But she doesn't flinch.

MIKA: Rune's told me a little. Memory loss, right? How are you feeling?

ASH: I do not feel. In the sense you mean.

MIKA: Everyone feels. Some people are just bad at identifying it.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He blinks. Not the hostile narrowing I've seen him give Kaya or the god-keepers. An actual blink. Like someone said something unexpected and he needs a second.

ASH: That is... an observation.

MIKA: I'm a paramedic. Observations are what I do.

[SFX: Mika unpacking food on the kitchen counter. Takeout containers. Casual, practical]

MIKA: You're eating with us. Rune cooks like someone who learned from a manual, and based on that bookshelf, you've been living on whatever he's been making. Sit down.

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash sits. He sits at my kitchen table across from my sister and he holds himself like someone bracing for attack and she puts a container of pad thai in front of him and hands him a fork and says *the noodles are better when they're hot* and he looks at the fork and then at her and I see something cross his face that I've only ever seen in flickers through the bond. Not hostility. Not confusion.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't know what to do with kindness when it comes without a chain attached.

---

{Scene 2 — Rune's kitchen. Later. Mika watches.}

[SFX: lunch winding down. Containers on the table. Mika and Rune at the counter — the sibling shorthand of doing dishes together. Water running. Ash in the living room]

MIKA (low, to Rune): He's reading your EMS certification guide.

RUNE: He reads everything.

MIKA: He's reading it upside down.

RUNE: He does that sometimes. He says the orientation is irrelevant.

[beat]

MIKA: Rune.

RUNE: Mm.

MIKA: Who is he?

RUNE: I told you. Memory loss. I found him at that abandoned site off Route 9. He —

MIKA: Don't. I've watched you orbit around him for an hour. You passed him the salt before he reached for it. You adjusted the thermostat twice because he was — I don't know — warm? You keep looking at the scars on his arms and then looking away. That's not how you treat a stranger you're helping out.

[beat]

RUNE: It's complicated.

MIKA: Try me.

[beat]

RUNE: I can't. Not yet.

[SFX: the faucet. Water. Mika studying him the way she studies patients — without judgment, with absolute attention]

MIKA: Is he dangerous?

RUNE: Yes.

MIKA: Are you in danger?

[beat]

RUNE: Probably.

MIKA: Are you going to tell me the truth at some point?

RUNE: Yeah. Just — not today.

[SFX: Mika drying her hands. The towel on the counter. A pause]

MIKA: He's lost.

RUNE: What?

MIKA: Your guy in there. Whatever he is, whatever you're not telling me — he's lost. I've seen the look. You see it in patients who wake up in the ambulance and don't know where they are. Except his is worse because I don't think he knows *who* he is either.

[beat]

MIKA: He ate the whole container. And when I handed him the fork, he held it like it was the first time anyone handed him something without wanting something back.

RUNE (inner monologue): My sister. Thirty-one years old. Paramedic. Best instincts of anyone I've ever worked with. And she just read a thousand-year-old god-eater in the time it took to eat lunch.

MIKA: Whatever's going on — I'm your partner. When you're ready, I'm here.

[SFX: Mika grabbing her jacket. Keys. Moving toward the door]

RUNE: Mika.

MIKA: Yeah.

RUNE: Thanks. For the food. And for being — normal. With him.

MIKA: He's a person, Rune. That's the default.

[SFX: the door closing. Mika's footsteps fading down the hall]

---

{Scene 3 — Rune's apartment. Afternoon. After Mika.}

[SFX: the apartment after Mika leaves. Quieter. Ash in the living room. The book closing]

ASH: Your partner is perceptive.

RUNE: She's my best friend.

[beat]

ASH: And what am I?

[SFX: the bond — a ripple. Not agitated. Searching. Like a hand reaching across a table in the dark]

RUNE (inner monologue): The question sits in the room like a stone dropped into water. The ripples go everywhere.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't have an answer. He's not a patient. Not a roommate. Not a stranger. He's the thing that hums behind my sternum when I breathe. He's the man whose shame tasted like copper in my mouth in a dead grove this morning. He is the constant current and the bookmarked CPR page and the tea he makes too strong.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't say any of this.

RUNE: I don't know.

ASH: An honest answer.

RUNE: It's the only kind I've got.

[SFX: Ash picking the book back up. Pages opening. The conversation is over — but the question isn't. The bond between them holds the shape of it]

---

{Scene 4 — City street. Evening. Being watched.}

[SFX: street sounds. Rune walking home from a shift. Keys in his pocket. Fatigue in his step]

RUNE (inner monologue): Evening shift. Three calls. Two chest pains and a kid who fell off a swing and broke his wrist. Normal. Ordinary. The kind of shift that used to fill up my whole brain. Now there's a partition — the paramedic on one side, the anchor on the other, and the anchor is aware of Ash six blocks northeast, reading on the couch, the bond between us taut and steady like a fishing line with something on the other end.

[SFX: Rune's footsteps on pavement. Then — a shift. The shadows along the building wall bend. Not toward Rune. Toward something behind him]

RUNE (inner monologue): The shadows move. Not wind. Not headlights. They *bend* — toward the building, toward the alley, toward something I can't see. And through the bond, a spike. Ash. Alert. Sharp. The predator clicking on like a switch.

[SFX: the shadows tightening. A sound like fabric pulled taut. Then — footsteps behind Rune. Fast. Retreating]

RUNE (inner monologue): I turn. Nothing. An empty sidewalk. A streetlight flickering. But the shadows around me are still wrong — angled the wrong way, too dark, too sharp. And I can feel Ash through the bond like a hand on the back of my neck. Protective. Automatic. He didn't think about it. He felt the threat and the shadows answered.

[SFX: the shadows releasing. Settling back to normal. The streetlight steadying]

RUNE (inner monologue): Someone was following me. And six blocks away, without moving from the couch, Ash made them stop.

[SFX: Rune's phone buzzing. A text]

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash. A text. He's been using my old phone for a week now. His texts are precise, formal, punctuated like legal documents.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): *"They will not follow again."*

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): God-keeper acolyte. Had to be. Thessa's people, watching, waiting for me to be alone and vulnerable. Except I'm never alone anymore. Not really. The bond doesn't allow it and Ash doesn't either.

---

{Scene 5 — Rune's apartment building. Night. The roof.}

[SFX: the apartment. Late. Ash pacing — his footsteps tracing the circuit. Window, kitchen, bookshelf, window. The same path but faster. Tighter. The air warmer than it should be]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's been pacing for an hour. The apartment is too small. I can feel it through the bond — the walls pressing in, the ceiling too low, the space insufficient for whatever he's carrying. The dead grove is still in him. The hunger. The shame. He hasn't processed it. He's just walking it off, and the walking isn't working.

[SFX: Ash stopping at the window. His palm flat on the glass. A faint hiss — the heat of him on cold glass]

RUNE: Come on.

ASH: What?

RUNE: Get your jacket. The one Mika brought. Come on.

[SFX: Rune grabbing keys. The apartment door opening. Ash's hesitation — then footsteps following]

---

{Scene 6 — The rooftop. Night. The city below.}

[SFX: a heavy door opening — metal, stiff hinges. The wind. The city at night from above — distant traffic, a siren somewhere far, the white noise of a million lives happening at once. Vast. Open. The opposite of the apartment]

RUNE (inner monologue): The roof. Twelve floors up. The access door was supposed to be locked but the super lost the padlock two years ago and never replaced it. The view is the whole eastern skyline — the river, the bridge lights, the downtown cluster still partially dark from the Meridian Temple collapse.

[SFX: their footsteps on the tar paper roof. Ash moving to the edge — not reckless, drawn. The wind in his jacket]

RUNE (inner monologue): He stops at the ledge. Not leaning. Standing. The wind pushes his hair back and the city spreads out below him and I watch the tension drain out of his shoulders like water out of a cracked glass.

[SFX: the wind. The city. The distance between the roof and the ground — vertiginous, alive]

RUNE: Better?

ASH: Adequate.

RUNE: High praise.

[beat]

[SFX: Rune joining him at the ledge. Standing beside him. The wind between them]

RUNE (inner monologue): We stand there. The city is enormous and indifferent and beautiful in the way only things that don't care about you can be. Lights in windows. Cars on bridges. People going places they chose to go.

ASH: This city was built on the bones of things it forgot.

RUNE: Most cities are. People build on top of what came before.

ASH: They build because they forget. If they remembered, they would not dare.

[beat]

RUNE: Is that what you are? Something people forgot?

[SFX: the wind. Ash turning his head — not toward the city. Toward Rune]

ASH: I do not remember what I was. Before. But I remember this.

RUNE: What?

ASH: Standing somewhere high. And looking at something beautiful. And knowing it would not last.

[beat]

[SFX: the wind. The city. The space between two people standing at the edge of something]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's looking at the city. But when he says *beautiful*, he's looking at me.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I know this. The bond tells me. The bond tells me everything now — the temperature of his attention, the direction of his gaze, the way his pulse changes when he's saying something that costs him. And right now every signal is pointed at me and the word he used was *beautiful* and the word after that was *last* and I am standing on a rooftop in the wind and I cannot breathe.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't say anything. He doesn't say anything more. We stand there and the wind fills the silence and the city burns below us and I think: this. This is the thing I'm afraid of. Not the bond. Not the god-keepers. Not the hunger or the dying gods or whatever's counting down beneath the concrete.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): This. Him. Looking at me like that. And meaning it.

[SFX: the wind fading. The rooftop quiet. Two sets of breathing. The bond humming between them — not the wire anymore. Something warmer. Something with a pulse]
