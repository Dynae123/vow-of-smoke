## 2. CHARACTER VOICE CONSISTENCY

### CRITICAL

**[C-3] Ash -- excessive contractions throughout batch**
The voice spec says Ash uses no contractions early on, with more contractions permitted post-vow. In this batch (all post-vow), Ash uses contractions heavily:
- ep33: "don't" (L62), "can't" (L66x2), "That's" (L72), "It's" (L204), "I'm" (L250)
- ep34: "don't" (L124, L128, L152), "doesn't" (L124), "don't" (L198), "It's" (L202, L218), "I've" (L180), "You're" (L210)
- ep35: "can't" (L50), "won't" (L274, L302x2), "It's" (L322), contractions throughout
- ep38: "don't" (L126, L142), "won't" (L120), "can't" (L150), "I'm" (L150, L264), "That's" (L264)

**Verdict:** Post-vow contractions are permitted per the spec. However, the density is very high. By ep38, Ash sounds nearly identical to a modern speaker. The "archaic undertow" on divine topics is still present in some passages (ep34 L124-128 on the cello, ep38 L126-150 to Selin), but disappears in casual scenes.
- **Fix:** In at least 30-40% of Ash's casual lines, replace contractions with full forms to maintain the slight formal register. Prioritize lines where the archaic quality would add texture (e.g., ep34 L198: "Shadows do not have steering wheels" reads more Ash than "Shadows don't have steering wheels").

## 6. PLOT LOGIC -- SIPHON / VOW-BREAK / MERIDIAN RESTORATION

### MODERATE

**[M-15] Siphon mechanics -- why can't god-keepers simply cut power?**

The siphon is described as ancient ritual architecture fused with modern engineering (ep34 L314-316). Selin says it's "anchored to the Meridian itself" and breaking it breaks the network (ep35 L82-84). Kaya confirms this (ep35 L146). However, the device has modern components: "copper wiring, glass tubing, circuitry" (ep34 L314). A listener might ask: why not sever the modern components? The scripts address this implicitly (the ritual mathematics are the foundation; the modern components are the interface, not the engine), but a single line of explicit clarification from Kaya would close the gap.
- **Fix:** Add a line in ep35 Scene 1 or Scene 3: "We tried disabling the modern components. The ritual architecture continues to function independently. The wiring is the interface, not the engine."

**[M-16] The "third way" mechanics -- energy conservation question**

Kaya's solution (ep36 L56, L168): the siphon draws energy from the Meridian, the bond redirects it through Ash and Rune, and it flows back into the Meridian. The siphon becomes a "circulation pump."

Question: If the energy just loops back, why does it need to flow through Ash and Rune at all? Why not loop it directly back into the network?

The answer is implied: the siphon is a one-way drain with no return path, and the bond's bidirectional architecture is the only known structure that can create a return path. But this could be stated more explicitly.
- **Fix:** Add a line from Kaya in ep36: "The siphon has no return channel. Its ritual architecture only extracts. The bond is the only existing structure with a proven bidirectional pathway."

**[M-17] Permanent conduit -- what are the ongoing costs?**

ep36 L186-190: The bond becomes permanent. They feel every sacred site. They are "living anchors." ep38 shows the aftermath: Rune is stronger, sleeps better, feels the network as background noise. ep38 L210-232 establishes the First Root awareness as the ongoing cost/weight.

The scripts handle this well. The only gap: if the bond is now the Meridian's central conduit, what happens if Rune or Ash is injured or unconscious? The earlier episodes established that anchor distress affects the network. Is the city now vulnerable to one car accident? ep38 and ep39 don't address this.
- **Fix:** Consider adding a brief exchange (ep38 or ep39) where Kaya or Rune addresses this: "The circuit has redundancy. If one anchor is compromised, the other carries the load. The network doesn't depend on both being conscious -- it depends on both being alive."

### MINOR

**[m-7] Selin's siphon knowledge vs. her cell constraints**

ep35: Selin provides detailed analysis of the siphon's function and limitations from her cell. She can describe the device she built but says she "cannot undo what I did not build" (L116). This is internally consistent. However, the scripts don't clarify how she knows the timer was added post-capture -- she says "I do not know which" follower did it (L104), but she speaks with certainty about the modifications. If she's been in a sealed cell, how does she know the triggering architecture was changed?

Possible answer: Thessa or Kaya showed her the schematics/data. But this isn't stated.
- **Fix:** Add one line from Thessa before the interrogation begins: "We brought the schematics from the laboratory. She has reviewed them."

## 9. OVERALL ASSESSMENT

The batch is strong. The arc from ep33 (quiet aftermath) through ep37 (climactic ritual) to ep40 (denouement) is well paced and emotionally coherent. Character voices are largely consistent, with Ash's contraction density being the primary voice concern. The siphon mechanics are logically sound but would benefit from 2-3 clarifying lines to close gaps a careful listener might notice.

The most urgent production issues are: (1) SFX tag length and content discipline -- many tags contain narrative, thematic, or meta content that belongs in DIRECTION or production notes, not in sound descriptions; (2) the NARRATOR tag anomaly in ep39; and (3) the fourth-wall references embedded in SFX tags.

The Selin silence in ep38 is confirmed clean. The emotional beats land. The finale mirrors ep1/ep8 effectively. The First Root thread is seeded for potential future content without undermining the resolution.

**Recommended priority for revision pass:**
1. SFX tag audit (length + producibility)
