# Episode 11: "Smoke Signals"

**One job:** Rune stops being able to pretend. The cataloguing — the way Ash holds books, stands at windows, changes his voice for Rune — floods into conscious awareness and can't be put back. Meanwhile, Ash traces the pattern: the gods are being killed in order to feed him. The hand-brush at the kitchen table is the earthquake neither of them acknowledges.
**The moment:** Ash's hand brushes Rune's over the map. Neither moves. Rune's internal catches on it and won't let go: the scar on Ash's wrist, the parenthesis, the thumb. The denial cracks.

---

{Scene 1 — Rune's apartment. Morning. The new normal.}

[SFX: the apartment. Morning. Coffee brewing. But the soundscape is different now — fuller. A knock at the door. Kaya's voice in the hallway. Papers being spread on the kitchen table. Mika arriving ten minutes later with takeout bags and the blunt efficiency of someone who has decided to be helpful and will not be stopped]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment has become something else. Not mine anymore. Not the quiet box where I slept between shifts and ate standing up and pretended the nightmares were stress. It's a headquarters now. Kaya brings research — stacks of photocopied maps, hand-drawn overlays, timelines scrawled on the backs of envelopes. Mika brings food and bandages and the kind of practical competence that keeps rooms from falling into chaos. And the ceasefire with Thessa means her people are watching the remaining sacred sites, which means we get reports — clipped, hostile, delivered to Kaya's phone because the god-keepers will not call me directly.

[SFX: Mika unpacking food. Kaya moving papers. The controlled bustle of people working a problem]

MIKA: Three containers. Eat while it's hot. You too.

[SFX: a container being set in front of Ash. The same casual inclusion from the last visit]

ASH: Thank you.

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it now. Thank you. Two words he didn't have ten days ago. Not the concept — the willingness. Mika earned it by handing him a fork like he was a person.

[SFX: everyone settling. Eating. The map on the table between them — paper, weighted at the corners with mugs]

RUNE (inner monologue): And in the middle of the organized chaos, the thing I can't stop doing.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm watching him.

[SFX: Ash turning a page of Kaya's research. The paper between his hands]

RUNE (inner monologue): The way he holds books — both hands, like they're fragile. I noticed this on day three. I've noticed it every day since. He holds paperbacks and ancient texts and my paramedic training manual with the same care, like the information inside might break if he presses too hard. He's holding Kaya's Meridian research the same way now. Both hands. Thumbs at the margins.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The way he stands at windows. Not centered. Always slightly to one side, his shoulder angled, like someone used to being a target. I've watched him at every window in this apartment and the angle is always the same. Left shoulder forward. Weight on the back foot. Ready to move.

[SFX: Ash looking up from the page. Saying something to Kaya — his voice at its academic register, formal, precise]

ASH: The pattern is consistent through the first three kills. Each site is further from the periphery. The summoner is moving inward.

[SFX: then turning to Rune. Asking for the map. His voice different — lower, softer. The edge filed down]

ASH: Rune. The overlay. Can you pass it?

RUNE (inner monologue): His voice changes when he speaks to me. Softer. Less guarded. I noticed this a week ago. I've been trying not to notice it since.

RUNE: Here.

[SFX: paper sliding across the table]

RUNE (inner monologue): The awareness is flooding in and I cannot put it back.

---

{Scene 2 — Rune's kitchen. The map. The pattern.}

[SFX: the table. Maps layered over each other — ancient site charts under modern city grids. Pencil marks. Kaya's neat handwriting. Ash's sharper annotations in the margins]

KAYA: The Meridian's architecture is not symmetric. The nodes are distributed according to the natural geography — water sources, elevated ground, geological features — overlaid with human settlement patterns. The result is a web. The connections between nodes carry the network's energy.

RUNE: And the kills are following the web.

KAYA: Following and severing. Each death cuts a connection. The pattern is deliberate — the summoner is not simply killing gods. They are disassembling the Meridian's structure. Dismantling the cage.

MIKA: Cage?

[beat]

KAYA: The Meridian serves multiple functions. Stabilization. Communication between gods. And — containment. There is something beneath the city. Beneath the bedrock. The old texts call it the First Root. It is not a god. It predates the gods.

MIKA: What is it?

KAYA: Raw divine potential. Undifferentiated. Vast. The Meridian's weight keeps it dormant. If enough nodes fail —

MIKA: The cage opens.

KAYA: Yes.

[SFX: silence. The map. The reality of what they're looking at settling over the table]

MIKA: So someone is taking apart the lock on a box under the city. And you're telling me what's inside the box makes him look like a house cat.

[SFX: a beat. Then Ash — a sound that might be a breath leaving through his nose. Not a laugh. The predecessor of one]

ASH: House cat.

MIKA: You've got better reflexes.

[SFX: Ash studying the map again. His finger tracing the pattern — the line connecting the dead sites]

ASH: This is not random destruction. It is a harvest. They are killing the gods in order — weakest to strongest — and collecting the power.

RUNE: Collecting it for what?

[SFX: Ash's finger stopping on the map. The stillness of someone who has arrived at the answer they didn't want]

ASH: For me.

[beat]

ASH: This is a summoning ritual writ large. Someone woke me up. Bound me to you. And is now killing gods so I will have no choice but to consume their power. They are feeding me.

[SFX: the kitchen. The maps. The weight of what he's said pressing the room smaller]

KAYA: That is consistent with the texts. The god-eater ritual is designed to be activated in stages. The awakening is stage one — the being is released and bound. Stage two is the building of power — the god-eater consumes dying gods and grows stronger. Stage three —

ASH: Stage three is the reason they woke me up. Whatever they need me powerful enough to do.

RUNE: Destroy the First Root?

KAYA: Or free it. Or absorb it. The texts are unclear. But whichever it is, the summoner needs Ash at full power. Every god they kill is a meal they are placing in front of him.

[SFX: the silence after strategy turns personal. Ash staring at the map. His hands flat on the table]

ASH: And every meal I refuse costs time. Which is why the kills are accelerating. I did not feed at the grove. I did not feed at the park. The summoner is compensating — killing faster, killing larger gods, trying to create enough power that I cannot resist.

MIKA: Can you resist?

[beat]

ASH: I do not know.

[SFX: the kitchen. The truth of that sitting on the table between the maps and the empty food containers]

---

{Scene 3 — Rune's kitchen. Later. The brush.}

[SFX: Mika and Kaya gone. The apartment quieter. Late afternoon light. Rune and Ash at the kitchen table, the maps still spread between them. Two mugs of tea — Ash made it, too strong as always]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment empties and the noise goes with it and what's left is the two of us and the map and the silence and the thing I've been avoiding all day.

[SFX: Ash leaning over the map. Pencil in hand. Marking possible sites — the remaining nodes, the vulnerable connections. His focus absolute]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's been working the pattern for an hour. Cross-referencing Kaya's historical charts with the modern grid. His handwriting is sharp and precise — he learned to write in English three days after arriving and his letters already look like they belong in an architecture firm. He's circled two sites in red. The underground spring in the old market district. The cathedral node at the city center.

[SFX: Rune reaching for the same section of the map. Pointing at a node in the south quarter]

RUNE: What about this one? The south quarter. Kaya mentioned a library site —

ASH: Dormant. The god there is fading but not dead. Not a priority for the summoner — the power yield would be minimal.

RUNE: So the spring or the cathedral. Those are the targets.

ASH: The spring first. It is weaker. The pattern follows the hierarchy — weak to strong. The cathedral node will be last.

[SFX: Ash marking the spring on the map. His hand moving across the paper — and brushing Rune's]

[beat]

[SFX: skin on skin. Brief. Accidental. The sound of two hands occupying the same space over a map of the end of the world]

RUNE (inner monologue): His hand brushes mine. Neither of us moves.

[SFX: the bond — a spark. Not a surge. A warmth that blooms from the point of contact and spreads through the wrist, the forearm, the chest. The bond registering something it's been waiting for]

RUNE (inner monologue): His fingers are an inch from mine. The scar on his wrist — the one that curves like a parenthesis, the binding mark that runs from his palm to the crook of his elbow — is right there. I could trace it with my thumb. I could feel the texture of the healed ritual cut under my skin and learn the topography of what was done to him.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I am thinking about the Meridian. I am thinking about dead gods and sacred geometry and the next target.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I am absolutely not thinking about the scar on his wrist that curves like a parenthesis and how it would feel under my thumb.

[SFX: Ash's hand still. Not pulling away. The bond humming between them — warm, searching, the frequency of two people pretending a coincidence isn't a confession]

ASH (quieter than before): The spring. Three days. If Kaya's timeline is correct.

RUNE: Then we have three days to figure out how to stop it.

[SFX: Ash pulling his hand back. Slowly. Not a flinch. A retreat with full awareness of what he's retreating from]

RUNE (inner monologue): He pulls away. I let him. The inch of air between our hands fills back in and I feel it like a door closing.

[SFX: the map. The tea, cooling. The light shifting through the window]

---

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. Evening. Kaya's lead.}

[SFX: Rune's phone ringing. Evening. The apartment darker. Ash in the living room — the sound of a page turning. The new normal]

RUNE: Doctor.

KAYA (on phone): I have a lead. A ritual site — not the summoner's current location, but a place they have been using. The old quarter, beneath the Church of Saint Erasmus. There is a passage from the church basement that connects to the older tunnel network under the historic district.

RUNE: How do you know this?

KAYA: The church was built on the foundations of a pagan temple. The temple was built on the foundations of a sacred chamber that predates written record in this region. I have known about the passage for decades. But the site has been inactive — sealed — for as long as I have been studying it. Until three weeks ago.

RUNE: Three weeks. When Ash was awakened.

KAYA: Yes. One of my monitoring instruments — a vibration sensor I placed at the entrance years ago — began registering activity. Someone has been using the chamber. The activity patterns suggest ritual use: clustered visits, each lasting several hours, then silence for days.

RUNE: You've had a sensor there for years.

KAYA: I monitor many of the old sites. Most produce nothing. This one has been dormant since I installed the sensor.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The list. The running list of things Kaya knows and hasn't told me. Monitoring instruments at sacred sites. Decades of data. A passage he's known about and never mentioned.

RUNE: Anything else you've been monitoring that might be useful?

[beat]

KAYA: I will send you a full inventory of my monitoring network. It is... extensive.

RUNE: I'll bet.

KAYA: Rune. The chamber beneath Saint Erasmus. If the summoner has been using it, there may be evidence of their methods. Their planning. Perhaps enough to identify them.

RUNE: We'll check it out.

KAYA: Be careful. The tunnels beneath the old quarter are not stable. And if the summoner has warded the site —

RUNE: I'll have Ash.

[beat]

KAYA: That is what concerns me.

[SFX: the phone clicking off. Rune in the kitchen, the light from the living room where Ash is reading. The bond between them — constant, present, the warmth from the afternoon still lingering in it like an afterimage]

---

{Scene 5 — Rune's apartment. Night. The admission.}

[SFX: the apartment at night. Rune alone in the kitchen after everyone is gone. The maps still on the table. Two mugs — his and Ash's — side by side by the sink. The faucet dripping. The city outside, muffled]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment is quiet. Ash is in the living room. Reading. The bond tells me he's calm — the hum steady, the warmth from this afternoon still there, banked like coals.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I stand at the sink and I look at the two mugs. His and mine. Side by side. The same chipped blue ceramic because I only own four and two of them are his now by habit.

[SFX: the faucet. The drip. The quiet of a man standing in his kitchen wrestling with something that won't stay quiet]

RUNE (inner monologue): Somewhere between the shrine and today, the monster on my couch became the person at my table. The god-eater became the man who holds books with both hands and stands at windows like a soldier and makes tea too strong because he doesn't know his own strength in small things.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): And I can pretend that's not what's happening. I can pretend the way my chest tightens when he looks at me is the bond. Can call it the empathic connection, the anchor resonance, the parasitic drain. There are so many clinical words and all of them are true and none of them are the right one.

[SFX: the drip. The apartment. Ash turning a page in the other room — the sound barely there but the bond makes it loud]

RUNE (inner monologue): The right word is the one I keep swallowing. The one that lives in the inch between our hands over a map. The one that's been building since day one and I have been filing under every other category because I am a paramedic and I diagnose what I can treat and this — this I cannot treat.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): I have never been a good liar. Especially to myself.

[SFX: the faucet turning off. The kitchen going silent. The bond humming through the wall — warm, steady, the pulse of someone reading in the next room who has no idea what's happening in the kitchen. Or who knows exactly what's happening and is giving Rune the space to arrive at it on his own]

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He brushed my hand over a map of the dying city and I am standing in my kitchen at midnight and I can still feel it.

[SFX: the clock. The apartment. The bond — a pulse now, not a hum. Two rhythms in the same space. Getting closer to sync]
