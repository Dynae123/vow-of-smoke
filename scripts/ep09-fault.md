# Episode 9: "Fault"

**One job:** Reveal the trap. Rune was chosen — his bloodline, his family, his specific life — selected by someone to be the chain on a weapon. Ash's fury at this is not about himself. It is about Rune. And in the bedroom doorway, when Rune asks "Are you willing?", Ash answers by sitting down and not leaving.
**The moment:** Ash sits on the far edge of Rune's bed. Doesn't speak. Doesn't leave. It's not much. It's everything.

---

{Scene 1 — A lecture hall. Recorded audio. Kaya's voice through speakers.}

[SFX: the hiss of a recording. Ambient echo — a lecture hall, empty. Then Kaya's voice, slightly tinny, the cadence of an academic performing knowledge]

KAYA (recorded): The god-eater myth exists across cultures, always with the same structure: a being of immense power, bound to a mortal anchor, summoned in times of divine crisis. The anchor is always described as "the one who holds the chain." But what the myths never explain is why the anchor survives at all.

[beat]

KAYA (recorded): The god-eater is a conduit for divine energy. The anchor is a human body tethered to that conduit. By every metric we understand, the anchor should be destroyed by the connection within days. And yet the texts describe anchors who lived. Who endured. Some for decades.

[beat]

KAYA (recorded): This suggests a mechanism we do not yet fully understand. Something in the bond that preserves even as it consumes. A paradox at the heart of the oldest ritual in human history.

[SFX: the recording clicking off. Silence]

RUNE (inner monologue): I found the lecture online. Kaya gave it six years ago at a comparative mythology conference. Fourteen people attended. He published the paper. Nobody cited it.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): Six years ago. Someone performed the awakening ritual. Kaya was already lecturing about god-eaters. I file this away in the part of my brain that's started keeping a list of things Kaya knows and hasn't told me.

---

{Scene 2 — Rune's apartment. Morning. The body keeping score.}

[SFX: Rune's alarm. The buzz of a phone on wood. His hand slapping it off. Then — a cough. Dry, persistent, the kind that lives in your chest]

RUNE (inner monologue): Day nine. The cough started two days ago and won't clear. I told myself it was the weather, the dust from the grove, the bad sleep. I'm a paramedic. I know what I'm doing. I know exactly which symptoms to dismiss and which to worry about.

[SFX: Rune getting up. Footsteps. The bathroom faucet. Water splashing on his face]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bruises are harder to explain. One on my forearm. One on my ribs. One behind my knee. Not impact bruises — no tenderness, no swelling. They just appeared. Dark purple, the color of something leaking.

[SFX: Rune rolling up his sleeve. Looking]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm a paramedic. I know what spontaneous bruising means. I know the list of causes and most of them are bad. But none of them are *the divine bond tethering you to a god-eater is draining your life force.* That's not in the manual.

[SFX: the kitchen. Ash already there — the sound of the kettle. He's making tea. He makes it every morning now. Too strong. Always too strong]

RUNE: Morning.

[SFX: a mug being set on the counter. Then — nothing. Ash's silence. The specific silence of someone who sees something and chooses not to speak]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's looking at my arm. At the bruise. His face does the thing it does when the emotion is too big for the expression — goes flat. Still. The god-eater equivalent of a scream.

RUNE: It's nothing. Probably low platelets. I'll get bloodwork.

[SFX: Ash's hand on the counter. The ceramic of the mug. His breathing — controlled, deliberate]

ASH: You are lying.

RUNE: I'm diagnosing.

ASH: You are lying to yourself.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond between us hums and what comes through is not the shame from the grove. Not confusion. This is sharper. This is Ash looking at evidence of what he is doing to me and the feeling underneath is something close to grief.

RUNE: The tea's ready.

[SFX: Rune picking up the mug. Turning away. The conversation closed — except through the bond, where nothing is ever closed]

---

{Scene 3 — Kaya's office. University. Afternoon.}

[SFX: a heavy wooden door opening. Kaya's office — old, cluttered. The sound of books stacked too high, papers shifting. A window with city noise beyond it. Three people settling into chairs — Rune, Ash, Kaya]

KAYA: Thank you for coming. Both of you. I have new information, and it changes the nature of what we are dealing with.

RUNE: Go ahead.

KAYA: The awakening ritual. I have been studying the texts more carefully, with the additional context of the two god-deaths. The ritual requires three components. First: a god-eater's dormant essence — sealed in a sacred site, preserved. In this case, the shrine on the eastern edge where you found Ash.

RUNE: We knew that.

KAYA: Second: a summoner with knowledge of the old rites. Someone with deep familiarity with god-keeper texts and the ability to perform ancient ritual work. This person is still at large. Third —

[beat]

KAYA: A mortal anchor with a specific bloodline. Temple priest lineage. The god-keeper sensitivity. Without it, the bond cannot form. The anchor must be genetically capable of resonating with divine energy.

[beat]

KAYA: Your bloodline, Rune. Your family. The Alessi line is descended from Mediterranean temple priests who migrated north centuries ago. The knowledge was lost, but the sensitivity — your paramedic instinct, your ability to read patients before the monitors, the way you knew something was wrong at the shrine before you had any reason to —

RUNE: Wait.

KAYA: — that is not training. That is inheritance. An attenuated form of the god-keeper sensitivity that has been dormant in your bloodline for generations.

[beat]

RUNE: You're telling me I was born into this.

KAYA: I am telling you that you were *chosen* for this. Whoever performed the awakening ritual did not select a random anchor. They researched bloodlines. They identified you specifically. The Alessi temple priest lineage made you a viable candidate. Your proximity to the eastern shrine made you accessible.

RUNE: So this was planned. I was not random.

KAYA: Nothing about this is random.

[SFX: the office. Books. Dust. The weight of what's been said settling into the room]

RUNE (inner monologue): Planned. Researched. Selected. Someone out there spent time — years, maybe — going through records, tracing bloodlines, finding the right body to plug into their weapon. And they found me. Rune Alessi. Paramedic. Temple priest's great-great-great-grandson. The anchor they needed.

[SFX: movement — sudden. Ash standing. The chair pushed back. The temperature in the room dropping]

ASH: You are telling me someone chose him to be my leash. That someone picked the specific human I would slowly kill.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The room goes cold. Not warm — cold. Ash's anger usually runs hot. Fire answers it. Shadows bend. But this is different. This is glacial. The fury so compressed it burns in the other direction.

KAYA: That appears to be the case, yes. The summoner —

ASH: Find them.

[beat]

KAYA: We are trying —

ASH: You are researching. You are cross-referencing texts and mapping sites and giving lectures about myths. Find. Them.

[SFX: the shadows in the office sharpening. Books on the nearest shelf shifting — not falling, tilting. The window glass humming]

KAYA: I understand your anger.

ASH: You do not.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The room is a held breath. Kaya's hands flat on his desk. Ash standing, shadows coiled around him like something alive. And the fury — I can taste it through the bond. It's not about being summoned. Not about being made into a weapon. It's about me. Ash is furious that someone chose me. That someone looked at my file and my blood and my address and decided I would be the body that burns.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's angry that I'm dying. Not because it weakens him. Not because it threatens the bond.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): Because it's me.

RUNE: Ash.

[SFX: the shadows pulling back. Slow. Controlled. The glass stopping its hum]

RUNE: Sit down.

[beat]

[SFX: a long pause. Then Ash sitting. The chair. The shadows settling]

KAYA (carefully): I have leads. The summoner must have had access to god-keeper archives — the awakening ritual is not public knowledge. They must have had proximity to the eastern shrine for an extended period. And they must have the academic or institutional resources to conduct bloodline research.

RUNE: That's a short list.

KAYA: It is. I am working through it. I will have more for you within the week.

[SFX: papers being gathered. The meeting ending]

KAYA: Rune. One more thing. The bruises on your arms. I noticed them.

[beat]

KAYA: The deterioration is progressing. You understand — the timeline Thessa described may be optimistic.

[SFX: Rune standing. Jacket. The sound of someone who doesn't want to hear this but won't flinch from it]

RUNE: I know.

---

{Scene 4 — City street. Walking home. The bond surges.}

[SFX: the city. Late afternoon. Rune and Ash walking. Footsteps on pavement — Rune's steady, Ash's harder, still carrying the fury from Kaya's office. Traffic. A bus passing. The normal sounds of a city that doesn't know its foundations are cracking]

RUNE (inner monologue): We walk in silence. Six blocks from the university to the apartment. The route is familiar — past the coffee shop on Elm, the laundromat with the broken sign, the corner where the bus stop bench has one leg shorter than the others. I've walked this a hundred times. Never with him.

[SFX: their footsteps. The rhythm of two people walking together who haven't learned how to match their pace]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond is a roar. His fury hasn't subsided — it's gone underground, compacted into something denser. I can feel it in my jaw, my shoulders, the base of my skull. His anger living in my body.

[SFX: Ash stopping. Abrupt. His footsteps cutting off]

RUNE (inner monologue): He stops. Middle of the sidewalk, three blocks from home. Just stops.

[SFX: Rune stopping. Turning]

RUNE: Ash?

[SFX: the city flowing around them. People. Traffic. Two men standing still in the current]

RUNE (inner monologue): He looks at me. And whatever's been building in him since Kaya's office — the fury about the bloodline, the summoner, the plan — it surfaces. Not as heat. Not as shadow. As something in his face I've never seen before. Something raw and terrified and human.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): Somewhere in the last nine days, the monster on my couch started caring whether I live or die. And that terrifies him more than anything else.

ASH: I did not choose this.

RUNE: I know.

ASH: If I could undo the bond —

RUNE: Don't.

[beat]

ASH: You are dying because someone decided you were useful. Because a line in your blood made you compatible with a weapon. That is not —

[beat]

RUNE: What?

ASH: That is not acceptable.

[SFX: the sidewalk. The city. Two people standing in the middle of a Thursday afternoon saying things that belong in the dark]

RUNE (inner monologue): *Not acceptable.* He says it like a verdict. Like he's ruled on it and the universe needs to comply. And the thing underneath — the thing the bond is screaming at me — is that this is the closest Ash has come to saying he cares. Not the word. The refusal. The absolute rejection of the situation, not because it hurts him, but because it hurts me.

RUNE: We'll figure it out.

ASH: You keep saying that.

RUNE: Yeah. I keep meaning it too.

[SFX: they start walking again. Closer now. Not touching. But closer]

---

{Scene 5 — Rune's apartment. Night. The doorway.}

[SFX: the apartment. Night. Late. The sounds pared down — no traffic, no fridge cycle. Rune on his bed. The exhaustion is physical: the springs creaking as he sits, the heaviness in his breathing]

RUNE (inner monologue): I am tired. Not the tired from a shift — I know that tired, I've mapped it, it has borders. This is different. This is the tired that lives in your marrow. The kind where sitting on the edge of your bed feels like the last thing your body can do today.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bruise on my forearm is darker than this morning. The cough hasn't stopped. And underneath everything, the bond — still running, still drawing, still pulling energy from me to him in a current I can't shut off and he can't stop.

[SFX: footsteps in the hallway. Ash. Stopping at the bedroom door]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's in the doorway. Standing where he stood when the dream woke me. But this time the lights are on and I can see his face and the expression on it is not the hunter-guard from two nights ago. It's the look from the sidewalk. The one that terrified him.

ASH: The bond is killing you.

RUNE: I know.

ASH: I should leave. If I am not near you, the drain slows.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it flat. Factual. The way I deliver bad news on a call — strip the emotion, give the information, deal with the rest after. He learned that from me. I can hear my own cadence in his voice.

RUNE: Is that what you want?

ASH: What I want is irrelevant.

RUNE: It's not.

[beat]

RUNE: Kaya said both parties have to be willing. To manage it. The bond.

ASH: Your professor theorizes.

RUNE: He says there are precedents. Anchors who survived. It's possible if both sides are willing to try.

[SFX: the doorway. The bedroom. The space between them — ten feet and everything that hasn't been said in nine days]

[beat]

RUNE: Are you willing?

[SFX: silence. The apartment. The bond between them — humming, raw, the frequency of two people standing on either side of a question that will determine if one of them lives or dies]

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't answer. Not with words.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps. Two, three. Crossing the threshold into the room. Then — the bed. The far edge. The springs shifting under weight placed as far from Rune as the mattress allows]

RUNE (inner monologue): He sits on the edge of my bed. The far corner. His hands on his knees. His back straight. He looks at the wall, not at me.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't leave.

[SFX: the bed. Two people. The maximum possible distance on a queen mattress. The bond between them — not humming now. Settled. The note it's been searching for found]

RUNE (inner monologue): It's not much. He's four feet away and he won't look at me and he hasn't said a word. It's the smallest thing. The minimum gesture. A body in a room, choosing to stay when leaving was an option.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): It's everything.

[SFX: the bond. Steady. Warm. The sound of a choice made without a single word]

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): We sit like that. Him on the far edge, me where I am. Not talking. Not touching. The clock on my nightstand ticking. The city outside doing what cities do — going on, indifferent, alive.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): The god-keepers say he'll eat me alive. Kaya says someone planned this. The bruises are spreading and the cough won't stop and someone out there chose my blood to feed a weapon.

[beat]

RUNE (inner monologue): And the weapon is sitting on my bed, looking at the wall, staying because I asked.

[SFX: the clock. The breathing. The bond between them — the note it makes not a hum anymore but a pulse. Two rhythms learning to sync]
