# Episode 31: "Daybreak"

**One job:** The world after the war. Rune goes back to work. Ash begins the absurd, tender process of becoming a civilian. Thessa offers Ash a protector role. He accepts on his terms. Ash sleeps in Rune's bed — properly, fully, the last wall gone. The listener breathes.
**The moment:** "I'm the guy who makes terrible coffee and reads too many books and is learning to use a washing machine." — Ash answering Thessa's question about what he is, with Rune in the doorway, and the answer containing the entire series.

He sleeps the way humans sleep — deep, unaware, his body generating heat that makes the sheets almost too warm. He drools slightly. I'm not telling him that.

[SFX: Rune — carefully moving Ash's arm. The slow, deliberate extraction of a person trying not to wake someone who runs hot and holds on tight. Fabric shifting, skin on sheets, the small protest of a body disturbed] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I watch him for ten seconds I don't have. The scar that curls behind his ear. The one on his wrist that curves like a parenthesis. His hand on the pillow where my head was, fingers curled slightly, holding the space I left. He's wearing my shirt. The grey one that's too tight across his shoulders and too long in the sleeves. He stopped asking before wearing my clothes around day thirty. I stopped minding around day four.

[SFX: Rune getting up — feet on cold floor. The bathroom. Water running. The routine sounds of a man resuming a life that paused for five weeks of divine crisis. Toothbrush. No blood in the sink] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): No blood. I brush my teeth and spit and the water runs clear. The headache is gone. The tremor is gone. The nosebleeds stopped the night of the vow and haven't come back. My body is mine again. The drain ended and the well refilled and I am standing in my bathroom at 5:35 AM and the only thing wrong with me is bruised ribs and a man in my bed who holds pillows like they're people and I have a shift in forty-five minutes.

[Beat — 400ms]

{Scene 3 — Rune's apartment. Afternoon. Ash's adjustment.}

[SFX: Ash — behind him. His warmth close behind. Not touching. Standing close. The bond humming between them in the small kitchen the way it always does — the frequency of shared space — too small, neither of them wanting more room] [spot]

Scorched eggs and wet towels and a man who reads at a pace that should be impossible and can't operate a spin cycle. This is the eternity he traded godhood for. Mine.

[SFX: Ash — looking across the room. At Rune in the doorway, putting on his jacket. Warmth — settled, certain. The answer that has been building since the night Ash named himself on a couch in a stranger's apartment] [spot]

[SFX: Rune — from the doorway. Not turning around. The voice of someone who has heard the most important sentence of the evening and is choosing to undercut it because that's what they do] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Domestic. Warm. Banter where there used to be crisis. A god-eater and a paramedic who have been through the end of the world, arguing about laundry]

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: Thessa — moving to leave. Her hand on the doorframe. A pause. The weight of someone who has something left to say and is deciding whether the formality of her entire life permits it] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: the door — closing behind her. The hallway. The apartment settling back to two] [spot]
