# Pitch — Vow of Smoke

## Logline
A paramedic finds a god-eater with no memory at an abandoned shrine — bound to him by an ancient ritual as gods across the city begin dying.

## Format
- **Episodes:** 40
- **Target length:** ~5 minutes each
- **Rating:** 16+
- **Genre:** Dark Fantasy / Supernatural + Enemies-to-Lovers BL

## Premise
Rune Alessi is a paramedic who keeps having the same nightmare: a burning temple, a voice calling his name, and the feeling of a hand slipping out of his. When he responds to a call at an abandoned shrine and finds a man covered in ritual scars with no memory of who he is, Rune's life fractures into the supernatural.

The man calls himself Ash — volatile, unsettling, and bound to Rune through an ancient ritual. As gods across the city begin dying, Rune and Ash are dragged into a war between those trying to save the old gods and those trying to harvest their power. Ash is the weapon everyone wants to control. Rune is the leash. And the bond between them is doing something neither of them expected: making Ash human, and awakening something ancient in Rune.

## Thematic Core
The Vow of Smoke is a choice — not compelled, not required, freely given — to be human. To accept limitation, mortality, and love as preferable to power, immortality, and isolation. In a universe where gods are dying and the ground is shaking, the most radical act is choosing to be small.

## Structure
- **Act I (Eps 1-10):** The Binding — Rune finds Ash, learns about the bond, meets the factions, first god-deaths
- **Act II (Eps 11-20):** The Hunger — Ash's power grows, the bond deepens, the Meridian destabilizes, enemies close in
- **Act III (Eps 21-30):** The War — Open conflict between factions, Ash's past revealed, Rune's ancestry awakens
- **Act IV (Eps 31-40):** The Vow — The Summoner revealed, the First Root threatens to wake, Ash makes his choice

## Narrator
Rune. First-person internal monologue grounds the supernatural in human emotion. He's a paramedic trained to save people, now bound to the most dangerous being in the city and slowly realizing he'd burn the world down to keep him safe.

## Sonic Identity
- Internal monologue uses bandpass filter (300 Hz–4 kHz) + micro-echo (20 ms) to separate inner voice from spoken dialogue
- Ash's presence signaled by thermal/shadow sound design — heat shimmer, flame response, shadows with weight
- The Meridian has a subsonic thrum that builds across the series
- God-deaths are seismic + organic — stone exhaling, water draining, root systems snapping
