# Episode 12: "Sacred Ground"

**One job:** Ash recovers a memory: he was human once. Before the ritual, before the scars, before the hunger — someone. The revelation wrecks him. Walking home, his hand finds Rune's. Not an accident. A choice. The first deliberate touch. And in the apartment doorway, neither sleeps, suspended on the edge of something neither has named.
**The moment:** Ash's hand finding Rune's on the walk home. Deliberately. A choice made by a being who has had nothing to choose in a thousand years.

{Scene 1 — The old quarter. Night. The descent.}

[SFX: footsteps on cobblestone] [bridge]
[AMBIENT: old quarter at night — close-walled echo, muffled church bell, distant city sounds receding] [bed]

RUNE (inner monologue): The Church of Saint Erasmus is in the oldest part of the city. The streets are too narrow for cars. The buildings lean toward each other like they're sharing secrets. The church itself is small — stone, dark, a side door that Kaya's instructions say leads to the basement.

[SFX: side door opening — old hinges, heavy wood] [spot]
[AMBIENT: church basement — enclosed, underground, sound changing. Smoke residue, old incense atmosphere] [bed, crossfade]

ASH: The air is wrong here.

RUNE: Wrong how?

ASH: Old. And used. Someone has been breathing this air. Recently.

[SFX: stairs — narrow, stone, spiraling down. Two sets of footsteps echoing off close walls] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): The stairs go deeper than they should. Past the church basement. Past the foundations. Into stone that predates the building above by centuries. My phone light catches carvings on the wall — symbols I don't recognize, worn smooth by time and touched hands.

[SFX: descent continuing — air heavier, water dripping through stone. Then stairs end and the space opens] [bridge]

ASH: Here.

[AMBIENT: underground chamber — vast natural reverb, amplified water drip, low meridian-thrum in stone] [bed, crossfade]
[DIRECTION: The ceiling is high enough to swallow sound. The Meridian hum is felt more than heard]

RUNE (inner monologue): The chamber. It opens around us like a held breath. The ceiling is lost in the dark. The walls curve — natural stone, not carved. A cave system connected to the tunnels. And on the floor, in the center of the space —

[SFX: Rune's footsteps stopping. Phone light sweeping across stone] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): A circle. Carved into the stone floor. Deep, precise, the kind of line that took weeks to cut. Inside the circle, grooves and channels running in patterns I've seen before — on Ash's skin. Binding marks. The same language of scars, written in stone instead of flesh.

[SFX: candle wax crunching underfoot. Rune kneeling] [spot]

RUNE: Someone's been using this. The wax is fresh. Weeks old at most.

ASH: Older than that. This circle has been in use for months. Perhaps years.

[SFX: Ash's footsteps through the chamber — slow, drawn, deliberate] [bridge]
[DIRECTION: Ash's footsteps slow involuntarily — the same drawn quality as the grove. His breathing deepens.]

RUNE (inner monologue): Then he sees what I missed. Not on the floor. On the wall.

[SFX: Ash stopping] [spot]

ASH: Rune. Bring the light.

[SFX: Rune crossing the chamber. Phone light sweeping up to the wall] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Photographs. Dozens of them. Pinned to the stone wall with small nails driven into the mortar. Every sacred site in the city — the grove, the Meridian Temple, the park, the spring, the cathedral, sites I don't recognize. Each one labeled with a number and a date. Some dates are in the past. Some are in the future.

RUNE: It's a schedule.

[SFX: Rune moving closer. Paper rustling against stone] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The past dates match the god-deaths. The grove. The Meridian Temple. The park. All marked, all dated, all correct. And in the future column — the underground spring. Three days from now. The date matches Ash's prediction exactly.

RUNE: The spring. Three days. You were right.

ASH: I know.

RUNE: There's something else here. Scratched into the wall below the photos.

[SFX: Rune's fingers tracing carved lines in stone] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): A timeline. Not the kill schedule — something larger. A sequence of events stretching back years. Dates, locations, annotations in a handwriting I've never seen. Precise, academic. The handwriting of someone who plans the way Kaya researches — methodically, obsessively, across decades.

RUNE: This goes back six years. The planning for this — all of it — started six years ago.

{Scene 2 — The chamber. The circle. The memory.}

[SFX: Ash's footsteps moving back to the circle — slow, drawn] [bridge]
[DIRECTION: Ash's voice tightens with each line — something building. Rune's breathing should shorten in response.]

RUNE (inner monologue): Ash returns to the circle. Stands at its edge. The carved lines glow faintly in my phone light — not literally. But the shadows around Ash respond to the pattern like iron filings to a magnet, pooling in the grooves, tracing the ritual marks.

ASH: This is where it was done.

RUNE: The awakening ritual?

ASH: No. Older. This is where the original ritual was performed. A thousand years ago. This chamber. This circle. This is where they made me.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: water drip. Meridian-thrum in stone] [bed]

RUNE: You remember?

ASH: No. The marks remember. The scars on my skin and the marks in this stone — they are the same. I can feel the resonance. Like hearing a song and knowing you knew the words once.

[SFX: Ash stepping into the circle — footstep on carved stone] [spot]

RUNE: Ash —

[SFX: bond detonation — massive surge. Chamber hum rises to teeth-vibrating intensity. Rune's footstep stumbling back] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Not hostile. Not painful. But massive. Rune staggers back a step from the wave]

RUNE (inner monologue): The bond hits me and I see it. Not my memory. His.

[SFX: memory flash — roaring fire, real stone temple acoustics, ancient chanting in an older language than previous dream sequences] [bed]
[DIRECTION: This is real, not a dream. Figures in white — the predecessors of the god-keepers. A stone altar in the center. Production note: must sound distinct from Rune's recurring nightmare — different reverb, different fire texture, different vocal processing]

RUNE (inner monologue): A man. Young. Maybe twenty. Dark hair. Brown skin. His hands are chained to the stone and his eyes are open and they are terrified and ordinary and human. He's screaming. The scars are being cut into him as I watch — the same marks, the same patterns, carved into living skin while he's still awake. And the fire is answering the cuts, pouring into them, filling the grooves with something that burns white —

[SFX: memory snap — violent, like glass shattering. All sound cuts] [spot]

[SFX: Ash on his knees — heavy impact, breathing hard. Chamber echo swallowing the sound] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The memory collapses. I'm back in the chamber. My own body. My own breathing. And Ash is on his knees in the center of the circle, his hands flat on the stone, his head down.

[SFX: Rune crossing the circle. Kneeling beside him. Hand on Ash's back — fabric shifting] [spot]
[DIRECTION: The physical contact is instinctive, automatic. The paramedic and the anchor and something else all moving at once]

RUNE: I'm here. I've got you. Breathe.

[SFX: bond equalizing — the surge draining back to normal. Shared weight] [under]

ASH (his voice wrecked): I was human once.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: Before. I was human and they made me into this.

[SFX: his breathing. The chamber. The ancient marks under his hands] [under]
[DIRECTION: Ash's voice is wrecked — raw, cracking. Not performed grief. The sound of something breaking open.]

RUNE (inner monologue): His voice. I've heard him hostile, cold, sarcastic, furious, ashamed. I have not heard this. This is the sound of someone finding a piece of themselves they didn't know was missing and discovering it's a wound.

RUNE: What do you remember?

ASH: The altar. The chains. My hands. My hands before the scars. I had — I had hands.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: I had a face. I cannot see it. But I had one.

RUNE (inner monologue): My hand on his back. The bond transmitting everything — the grief, the shock, the vast emptiness of a man who just learned he wasn't always a monster. That once, before the fire and the hunger and the binding marks, he was someone who had hands and a face and a name that wasn't *close enough.*

RUNE: You still have a face.

ASH: Not that one.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[AMBIENT: chamber — drip, meridian-thrum, knees on stone, shared breathing] [bed]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't move my hand. He doesn't ask me to. The bond flows between us and the weight is shared — not halved, shared. His grief in my chest. My steadiness in his. The way the anchor works when it works right.

{Scene 3 — The tunnels. Ascending. Evidence.}

[AMBIENT: tunnel stairs — echoing footsteps, dripping, enclosed, air less heavy] [bed, crossfade from chamber]

[SFX: two sets of footsteps ascending. Ash's heavier than before] [bridge]
[DIRECTION: Ash's footsteps land heavier on the ascent — weighted, deliberate, changed.]

RUNE (inner monologue): We photographed everything. The wall schedule. The carved timeline. The circle. I'll send it to Kaya in the morning. Six years of planning, documented on a wall beneath a church, and the person who did it is still out there.

RUNE: Are you —

ASH: Do not ask if I am all right.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: I was going to ask if you need water.

[SFX: Ash — a sharp exhale through the nose. Almost a laugh] [spot]

ASH: No.

RUNE: There's a fountain outside the church.

ASH: I said no.

[SFX: climb continuing — footsteps on stone stairs] [bridge]

ASH: What you saw. Through the bond. The memory.

RUNE: Yeah.

ASH: What did I look like?

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Young. Terrified. Human. A boy on a stone altar with ordinary brown eyes who didn't know what was about to happen to him. Or who knew exactly what was about to happen and couldn't stop it.

RUNE: Young. You looked young.

ASH: Was I afraid?

RUNE: Yes.

ASH: Good.

RUNE: Good?

ASH: It means I was still human enough to be afraid. Whatever they did — they started with a person. Not a weapon. A person.

[SFX: side door opening — night air. Cobblestones, damp] [spot]

{Scene 4 — The walk home. Dawn. The hand.}

[AMBIENT: city at dawn — grey-gold quiet, distant delivery truck, a bird testing, minimal traffic] [bed]

[SFX: footsteps on cobblestone, shifting to pavement. Side by side — rhythm more aligned than before] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): We walk home through the city at dawn. The route from the old quarter to the apartment is long — forty minutes if you don't rush. We don't rush. The streets are empty. The shops are closed. The streetlights are still on but losing their argument with the sky.

RUNE (inner monologue): We don't speak. There is nothing to say that the bond isn't already saying. The memory from the chamber is in both of us now — the young man on the altar, the hands before the scars, the fear that proved he was a person before he was a weapon. It sits between us like a third presence, shared, heavy, unresolvable.

[SFX: footsteps continuing — steady, unhurried. A window opening above. A door closing somewhere] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): And underneath the memory, underneath the grief and the mission and the dying Meridian and everything that should be filling my thoughts — the hand. The brush over the map. The warmth of him that isn't the bond, isn't the fire affinity, isn't anything supernatural. Just the warmth of another person close enough to touch.

[SFX: footsteps. A block passing. Then another] [bridge]

[SFX: Ash's hand — fingers opening. Not a fist. Not reaching. Available] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: fingers closing around fingers — skin to skin. Bond tone flares warm, resolving] [under]
[DIRECTION: Deliberate. Not a brush. Not an accident. A choice]

RUNE (inner monologue): His hand. In mine. Not because the bond surged or the hunger hit or someone needed saving. Because somewhere between the chamber and this block he decided.

[SFX: footsteps continuing — same pace, hand in hand. Dawn building] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): His hand is rough and too warm and I can feel his pulse through his palm and it is faster than it should be for someone who isn't supposed to have a heartbeat. The scar — the parenthesis — runs under my thumb and the texture is exactly what I imagined. Raised. Smooth. A story told in skin.

RUNE (inner monologue): He doesn't look at me. I don't look at him. We walk through the city holding hands and staring straight ahead like two people who have just made a decision and are terrified of examining it too closely.

[SFX: footsteps — block after block. City beginning its morning around them] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): Four blocks. Three blocks. Two. The apartment building visible now, the familiar brick, the fire escape, the window where Ash stands at night and watches the city. All of it the same as yesterday. All of it different.

[SFX: footsteps slowing. Approaching the building. The hand still held] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): I am walking through my city holding the hand of a god-eater and I have never been more terrified or more certain of anything in my life.

{Scene 5 — The apartment. Dawn. The threshold.}

[AMBIENT: apartment building — hallway carpet, fluorescent light buzzing on the third floor landing] [bed]

[SFX: keys. Door opening] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): At the apartment door, we separate. Not a pull. Not a flinch. Just the natural release of two hands that know the walk is over. His fingers leaving mine. The air where his palm was.

[AMBIENT: apartment at dawn — early light through windows, maps still on kitchen table, the space exactly as they left it] [bed, crossfade]

RUNE: You should sleep. Or — whatever you do.

ASH: I do not sleep.

RUNE: I know. But you should try.

[DIRECTION: The threshold — living room to bedroom hallway. Neither moves past it. Voices low, close to whisper.]

ASH: Rune.

RUNE: Yeah.

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — charged, full of the walk and the hand and the chamber and the memory] [under]

ASH: In the chamber. The memory. The person on the altar.

RUNE: Yeah.

ASH: That person did not choose what was done to him. He was taken and remade and sealed in the dark for a thousand years. He did not choose the ritual. He did not choose the hunger. He did not choose the bond.

[Silence — 2500ms]

ASH: I am choosing this.

[DIRECTION: The words land — quiet, precise, heavy as the stone floor of the chamber]

RUNE (inner monologue): Flat. Direct. No preamble. The truth like a stone dropped into still water.

RUNE: Okay.

ASH: Okay.

[Beat — 400ms]
[SFX: footsteps separating — one toward the couch, one toward the bedroom] [spot]
[SFX: bond tone — clear, sustained, new frequency. Resolved] [bed, fade-out slowly]
[DIRECTION: Bond tone should resolve to a clear, sustained note — no searching quality, no tension. Settled.]

RUNE (inner monologue): He goes to the couch. I go to my room. The door stays open. It's been staying open for a week but tonight the openness means something different.

[SFX: mattress springs. Shoes pulled off] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I lie down. He lies down. I know because the bond tells me — the shift in weight, the settling, the breathing that slows but doesn't find sleep. Neither does mine.

[SFX: bond tone — two rhythmic pulses, approaching sync but not yet matching] [bed]
[DIRECTION: Both breathing audible — awake, aware of each other. The bond tone should hold a sustained, expectant note underneath.]

RUNE (inner monologue): Someone planned all of this — six years of obsessive work carved into a wall, a schedule ticking down, and the next god dies in three days. A thousand years ago a boy was chained to an altar and made into something that should have stayed buried.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): And he held my hand for four blocks and said *I am choosing this* and I am lying in my bed and his pulse hums through the wall like a second heartbeat.

RUNE (inner monologue): Three days until the spring. His hand in mine, rough and warm, the scar like a parenthesis. The sentence it closes: everything before. The sentence it opens: everything after.

[AMBIENT: apartment at dawn — city waking, bond-hum-warm faintly, the space between them charged and chosen] [bed, fade-out over episode end]
