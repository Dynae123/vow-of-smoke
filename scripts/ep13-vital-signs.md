# Episode 13: "Vital Signs"

**One job:** The bond's physical toll on Rune accelerates past anyone's timeline. Mika sees him collapse and refuses to be deflected. Rune tells her everything — not the Meridian, which she already knows, but the part he's been hiding: the bond is killing him. Meanwhile, Ash and Kaya clash over memory recovery, and Kaya's pendant surfaces. Then the summoner calls.
**The moment:** Mika's voice dropping into clinical mode as she counts the symptoms Rune can't hide anymore: the timeline is wrong, the deterioration is too fast, and the woman who taught him to be a paramedic is now triaging him.

---

{Scene 1 — Ambulance bay. Late afternoon. A routine call goes wrong.}

[AMBIENT: ambulance bay — idling diesel engine, radio chatter, distant hospital PA, equipment rattle] [bed]

[SFX: stretcher wheels locking into place. Rune's gloves snapping off] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Mrs. Petrakis. Eighty-three. Fell in the bathroom, possible hip fracture. Textbook call. Mika drives, I stabilize, we transport. I've done this a thousand times. My hands are steady. My vitals are good. The patient's vitals are good.

[SFX: ambulance rear doors slamming shut. Two sets of footsteps on concrete] [bridge]

MIKA: Clean run. You want to do the handoff or should I?

RUNE: I've got it.

RUNE (inner monologue): I take two steps toward the hospital entrance and the ground tilts.

[SFX: stumble — boot scraping, body catching against the ambulance panel. Metal impact, dull] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Not a fall. Not quite. My legs just — stop working. For maybe two seconds the signals between my brain and my knees are on different frequencies and I'm leaning against the side of the rig with my palm flat on the metal and the concrete is very interesting from this angle.

[SFX: Mika's footsteps — fast, precise] [spot]

MIKA: Rune.

RUNE: I'm fine. Stood up too fast.

MIKA: You were already standing.

[SFX: Mika's hand on his arm — firm, clinical grip] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Assessing, not comforting]

RUNE: Mika —

MIKA: Don't. How long have you been dizzy?

RUNE: I'm not dizzy. I just —

MIKA: How long.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: A few days. It comes and goes.

[SFX: Mika exhaling — controlled, measured] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Choosing not to yell]

MIKA: Inside. Now. I'll do the handoff.

RUNE (inner monologue): She doesn't wait for me to argue. She's already walking. And the way she said "now" — I know that voice. That's the voice she uses when a patient is coding and she needs the room clear. That's the voice that means she's scared and converting it to action.

[SFX: automatic hospital doors opening and closing. Mika's footsteps receding] [spot]

{Scene 2 — Hospital break room. The disclosure.}

[AMBIENT: break room — fluorescent buzz, vending machine hum, muffled hospital corridor noise, a clock] [bed]

[SFX: two chairs pulled out. Plastic table. Coffee cups, untouched] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): She finds me in the break room twenty minutes later. I'm sitting in the plastic chair by the vending machine and my hands have stopped shaking and I've rehearsed four different versions of "it's nothing" and none of them are going to work.

MIKA: Talk.

RUNE: Look —

MIKA: No. Not "look." Not "bad sleep, that's all." Not "long story." Rune. You went down in the field. Your arms look like you lost a fight with a door. You've dropped weight — I can see your collarbones through your shirt and that has never been true in the entire time I've known you. And that man living in your apartment —

[Beat — 400ms]

MIKA: I know about the gods. I know about the Meridian. I was at your kitchen table when Kaya laid out the kill pattern. What I don't know is why you look like you're dying.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Rune's fingers on the coffee cup — turning it. Plastic scraping on table] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The word she used. Dying. She said it clinical and direct — the same register she uses on calls, because the clinical word is easier to work with than the feeling behind it.

RUNE: The bond is parasitic.

MIKA: What does that mean?

RUNE: It means the connection between me and Ash — it flows one direction. His power draws on my life force to stay stable. The god-keepers told me. Months, maybe a year. Then I burn out.

[Beat — 800ms]

MIKA: When did you learn this?

RUNE: Week one.

[SFX: Mika's chair shifting back — sharp, involuntary] [spot]

MIKA: Week one. You've known for twelve days that this thing is killing you and you didn't —

RUNE: I didn't want to —

MIKA: Didn't want to what? Let your partner help? Let your sister know? You don't get to make that call alone, Rune. That's not how this works.

[SFX: the vending machine cycling. Clock ticking] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): She's not yelling. That's worse. Mika yelling I can handle. Mika quiet and precise with her hands flat on the table — that means she's already past the anger and into the planning.

MIKA (quieter): How fast is it happening?

RUNE: Faster than they said. The god-keepers estimated months. It's been twelve days and I'm already —

[SFX: Rune gesturing — sleeve pulling back, fabric shifting] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Rune's voice goes flat — showing, not telling. The admission costs him]

MIKA: The bruises aren't impact bruises. They're spontaneous.

RUNE: Yeah.

MIKA: Petechiae?

RUNE: Some. On my chest.

MIKA (clinical mode, her voice dropping flat): Weight loss. Spontaneous bruising. Petechiae. Dizziness. Syncope in the field. Rune, that's not months. That's a coagulopathy pattern. That's —

MIKA: Months, they said. It's been twelve days.

[SFX: clock ticking] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The timeline is wrong. Her mind is already on it — a blood panel that doesn't match the presentation. The numbers don't add up. The deterioration is happening too fast and neither of us knows why.

MIKA: Okay. So what do you need?

RUNE (inner monologue): She says it the way she's said it for every emergency we've ever worked. Not "what do we do." Not "how do we fix it." What do you need. Because Mika doesn't start with the crisis. She starts with the person.

RUNE: I need you to keep being my partner. And I need you to not tell anyone.

MIKA: Done. But you're getting a full bloodwork panel tomorrow and that is non-negotiable.

RUNE: Mika —

MIKA: Non-negotiable. I'll draw it myself if I have to. We're going to get a baseline and we're going to track it and we're going to figure out what accelerated the timeline. That's the job.

[SFX: Mika picking up her coffee. Drinking. Setting it down] [spot]
[DIRECTION: She has made a decision]

RUNE (inner monologue): She doesn't run. She doesn't call me crazy. She picks up her coffee and drinks it and behind her eyes I can see her building the triage protocol — assess, stabilize, monitor. The same thing she does for every patient. Except the patient is me.

{Scene 3 — Kaya's office. University. Ash and Kaya alone.}

[AMBIENT: university office — old radiator ticking, books shifting on shelves, distant hallway footsteps, window cracked to outside traffic] [bed]

[SFX: heavy book placed on desk. Pages flipping — old binding, thick paper] [spot]

KAYA: The marks on your forearms correspond to the third phase of the transformation ritual. The binding phase. Before these were carved, there would have been a preparatory sequence — fasting, isolation, specific incantations over a period of days. The texts describe it as a process of —

ASH: Emptying.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: Yes. You understand the terminology?

ASH: I do not understand the terminology. I remember the feeling.

[SFX: Ash's hand on the desk — flat, deliberate] [spot]

KAYA: What do you remember specifically? The chamber — you described stone walls, a circular space. Can you recall sounds? Voices? How many people were present during the —

ASH: You are asking questions the way a surgeon asks where it hurts. Clinical. Detached. As though the answer does not cost anything.

[SFX: radiator ticking. A pen set down] [under]

KAYA: I am trying to help you recover memories that may identify —

ASH: You are trying to extract data. There is a difference.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: The memories you recovered in the chamber beneath Saint Erasmus — the altar, the chains, the transformation — those fragments could contain details that identify the original ritualists. And the original ritual may share signatures with whoever performed the modern awakening. The summoner used ancient knowledge. If we can trace —

ASH: The chains were iron. They burned where they touched. There were voices — many, chanting, and one that spoke directly. Giving instructions. Calm. The calm was the worst part.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir — slight, restless] [under]

ASH: What else do you want. What the stone smelled like. How the fire felt when it entered the cuts. Whether I screamed.

[Silence — 1500ms]

KAYA: I am sorry. I know this is not —

ASH: You know nothing about what this is.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir intensifying — temperature-spike, books shifting on shelves] [spot]

[DIRECTION: The room responding to Ash's temper. Not an explosion — a warning]

KAYA: I have spent forty years studying the old gods. Their births, their lives, their deaths. I have stood in the ruins of temples that were old when Rome was young and I have read texts that describe the god-eater ritual in precise, unflinching detail. I know what was done to you because I have read the instructions.

KAYA: And I have seen worse than you, god-eater. I have seen what happens when gods die angry. I held one in my arms while it happened.

[SFX: ash-shadows settling — the temperature dropping back] [under]

ASH: You have met one. A god. Have you not.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Kaya's hand moving to his throat — fabric shifting. A chain, thin, old, pulled from beneath his collar] [spot]

KAYA: During my time as a god-keeper — before my expulsion — I was assigned to a minor node in the harbor district. The god of a river that no longer exists. The river was paved over for a highway extension when I was twenty-six. The god took three years to fade.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: I visited every week. We could not speak — gods do not converse the way humans do. But there was a presence. A warmth in the stone. A feeling of being recognized. And at the end, when the fading was almost complete, it manifested. Just once. A face in the water — young, tired, beautiful in the way that old things are beautiful.

[SFX: pendant against shirt — old bronze, small, the weight of decades] [spot]

KAYA: It gave me this. A fragment of its essence. A keepsake. So I would remember that it existed.

[DIRECTION: Kaya's voice thins — the lecturing tone is gone. This is personal, unguarded. Ash registers the shift: quieter, less certain]

ASH: You loved it.

KAYA: I held its hand while it died. Love seems inadequate and accurate in equal measure.

[SFX: Ash standing — chair scraping back. Footsteps to the window] [spot]

ASH (quieter): The fragments hurt because they are incomplete. A face without a name. Chains without a reason. A fire that felt like dying and also like being born. I do not resist your questions because I do not want answers. I resist because every fragment I recover reminds me that I was a person and that person is gone.

KAYA: Then we have something in common. I study what I have lost and call it scholarship.

[SFX: window traffic. Radiator] [under]
[DIRECTION: Rune's delivery slows — each word measured, keeping emotion at arm's length. Ash matches the register, guarded but present]

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. Evening. The phone call.}

[AMBIENT: apartment evening — fridge hum, distant traffic, empty room quiet] [bed]

[SFX: apartment door opening and closing — Rune entering. Keys on the counter. Jacket dropped] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The apartment is empty. Ash is still at the university with Kaya. The maps are still on the kitchen table. Two mugs by the sink — his and mine. The bond tells me he's twelve blocks away and agitated and I'm too tired to push and ask why.

[SFX: Rune sitting heavily — couch springs. A long exhale] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Mika's face in the break room. Her voice counting symptoms like items on a checklist. Weight loss. Bruising. Petechiae. Syncope. She looked at me the way we look at the ones who aren't going to make it — not with pity, never pity, but with that clinical urgency that means *we are running out of time and I need you to let me work.*

[SFX: phone buzzing on the kitchen counter — single vibration] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I almost don't check it. Then I do.

[SFX: Rune standing. Crossing to the counter. Phone picked up] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Unknown number.

[SFX: phone answering — the click of connection] [spot]

RUNE: Hello?

[SFX: distorted voice — processed, unrecognizable, calm. Real-time vocal processing, not pre-recorded] [spot]
[DIRECTION: The Summoner's voice. Cold, deliberate, each word placed. Not threatening — informing]

THE SUMMONER (distorted): The spring dies in two days. Your god-eater can stop it. Or he can feed. The choice has always been his.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE: Who is this?

THE SUMMONER (distorted): Someone who knows what he is. And what you are. Two days, Rune. Bring him to the spring. Or don't. Either way, I get what I need.

[SFX: line disconnecting — hard, final] [spot]

[SFX: phone lowered. Rune's breathing — steady on the surface, controlled] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): I stand in my kitchen holding my phone and the sentence loops. *Either way, I get what I need.* They know my name. They know about Ash. They know the spring is a trap and they're telling me anyway because the telling is part of it.

[SFX: bond-hum-agitated — responding to Rune's spike of cold, specific dread] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): They're not hiding. They're inviting. And the worst part isn't the threat — it's the confidence. Whoever this is, they've already accounted for every move we could make.

[SFX: apartment door opening — Ash returning. His footsteps, heavier than usual] [spot]

ASH: Rune.

RUNE (inner monologue): He's in the doorway. The bond carries him into my chest — his agitation, his cold anger, the thing underneath both that I'm not ready to name.

ASH: What happened.

RUNE: The summoner called.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir — immediate, involuntary] [under]

ASH: What did they say.

RUNE: Two days. The spring. They said you can stop the ritual or you can feed. And they said it doesn't matter which — either way, they get what they need.

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — brief, hot, Ash's fury transmitted through the connection] [spot]

ASH: They want me at the spring.

RUNE: Yeah. They're not even pretending it's not a trap.

ASH: Then we do not pretend either. We go. And I do not feed.

[SFX: bond tone settling — still agitated but resolving toward a shared register] [under]
[DIRECTION: Both voices tighten — clipped, focused, the anger shared but controlled]

RUNE (inner monologue): The summoner isn't hiding anymore. They're inviting us. Two days until the spring. And somewhere in this city, someone who knows both our names is waiting for us to walk into exactly the shape they designed.

[SFX: apartment settling — fridge hum, distant traffic, the maps rustling faintly on the table] [bed, fade-out over episode end]
[DIRECTION: Rune's monologue is quiet, grounded — the voice of someone who has made a decision he can't take back]

RUNE (inner monologue): Two days. And my blood doesn't clot right anymore and the summoner knows my name and the god-eater standing in my doorway is the most dangerous thing in the city and I would follow him into the ground.
