## CRITICAL

### C-01 | Ep33, line 106 | Fourth-wall break in Mika's dialogue
> MIKA: "I've seen it since episode -- since week one."

Mika says "episode" before self-correcting to "since week one." The word "episode" is diegetically meaningless; Mika has no concept of episodes. The dash-correction reads as a writer catching themselves mid-sentence rather than a character speaking. This will be audible to actors and listeners.

**Fix:** Replace with "since the beginning" or "since he moved in" or delete the false start entirely.

### C-02 | Ep35, lines 16-20 vs. line 150-154 | Siphon timeline inconsistency -- "three days" vs. drain-rate logic
Kaya says the siphon gives them **three days** (line 16). But later in ep35 Scene 3 (line 140), it is already **day two** and Kaya re-briefs the same three options. Selin in Scene 2 (line 122) says the slow-drain option takes **decades**, while Kaya's briefing says three days until network failure. The issue: if the siphon's slow drain takes decades (option 1), why does the timer give them only three days? The script doesn't clarify that the timer is accelerating the siphon beyond its normal drain rate. Selin says she "never installed a timer" (line 98) and designed the siphon for a slow decades-long drain -- so the Kindling modification apparently changed the drain rate from decades to days, but this is never stated explicitly.

**Fix:** Add one line from Kaya or Selin clarifying that the Kindling's countdown modification accelerated the siphon's drain rate from its designed decades-long pace to a three-day catastrophic extraction. Without this, the audience will wonder why Selin's decades-long backup suddenly has a three-day fuse.

### C-03 | Ep35, line 338 | Vow mechanics claim may contradict STORY-BIBLE
> ASH: "The vow binds me to you. Not you to me. If I die, the vow breaks. You survive."

STORY-BIBLE.md describes the vow as creating a **bidirectional** circuit: "Energy flows both ways -- the god-eater stabilizes the anchor, the anchor stabilizes the god-eater. Neither depletes the other. The bond becomes self-sustaining." A fully symbiotic/bidirectional bond that "breaks cleanly" when one side dies, leaving the other unharmed, is not established in the lore. Ash may be wrong (which is powerful dramatically), or the mechanics may need a supporting line from Kaya confirming or complicating the claim.

**Fix:** Either (a) add a beat in Scene 3 where Kaya corroborates or challenges Ash's claim about the vow breaking cleanly, or (b) add an internal Rune line noting he doesn't know if Ash is right -- making the uncertainty part of the tension. As written, the audience may take Ash's statement as established lore, which it isn't.

### W-01 | Ep33-35 | "Two people" construction -- 6 occurrences across three episodes
- ep33, line 80: "Two people from a place that doesn't exist anymore"
- ep33, line 264: "two people holding hands over a bandaged wound"
- ep34, line 114: "two people eating lunch on a blanket"

- ep34, line 234: "The kiss of two people who have time"

Six uses in three episodes is pattern saturation. The construction works best sparingly; at this density it becomes a verbal tic of the script rather than an intentional motif.

### N-12 | Ep35, Scene 5 | Non-actable direction in SFX tags
Several SFX tags across all three episodes contain internal-state descriptions that are not producible as audio:
- ep33, line 28: "Two kinds of divine energy in proximity, the eater and the eaten, and neither one flinching" -- not audible.
- ep33, line 92: "the recalibration of a woman who has adapted to the supernatural with the same pragmatism she applies to multi-vehicle pileups" -- character note, not SFX.
- ep34, line 90: "The pause of someone encountering a new flavor and cross-referencing it against everything they've learned about food" -- not producible.
- ep34, line 142: "The curiosity of a man who has been alive for a thousand years and dead for most of them" -- not audible.
- ep34, line 158: "A god-eater considering the prospect of sitting in a public hall" -- not producible.
- ep35, line 80: "The pause that means the answer is worse than the question" -- direction for actor, not SFX.
- ep35, line 120: "The woman who has been holding something back" -- character note.
- ep35, line 278: "The flat truth of a man who has been running the numbers through the Meridian for two days" -- not producible.

These read as director's notes or prose-novel descriptions embedded in SFX brackets. The audio team cannot produce "the recalibration of a woman." Some serve the writer's intent well and may be useful for voice actors as motivation notes, but they should be marked differently (e.g., [DIR:] tag) to distinguish from producible SFX.

**Suggestion:** Introduce a [DIR:] or [NOTE:] tag for non-producible character motivation cues, keeping [SFX:] for actual sound design.
