# Episode 35: "Countdown"

**One job:** Three impossible choices. None of them survivable. The team briefs, Thessa interrogates, and Ash decides he's willing to die. Rune refuses to let him. The worst silence in the series.
**The moment:** The rooftop. "The vow binds me to you. Not you to me. If I die, the vow breaks. You survive." / "You think I want to survive that?"

---

{Scene 1 — Beneath the eastern shrine. The briefing. Day one of three.}

[AMBIENT: the laboratory beneath the shrine — now a war room. More lights. More people. God-keeper researchers at workstations. Cables and monitoring equipment surrounding the siphon. The cycling whine of the device constant — low, mechanical, cycling on a fixed interval. The meridian-thrum audible behind it, strained, like a heartbeat working against a tourniquet] [bed]

[SFX: Kaya — at the central table. Maps of the Meridian network. Diagrams of the siphon's connections. His voice in the briefing register: no pauses, no lecture cadence. Information delivered at speed because there is no time for context] [spot]

[SFX: the room — Thessa standing, arms crossed. Two senior god-keepers beside her. Ash against the wall, his scars warm, the proximity to the siphon visible in the tension along his jaw. Rune beside him. Mika on speaker from the ambulance bay — she's coordinating surface operations and monitoring the briefing] [spot]

KAYA: Three days. Based on the siphon's current drain rate and the Meridian's remaining reserves, we have three days before the network fails. The device is drawing power continuously — a slow extraction, manageable in short bursts, but cumulative. Every hour it runs, the Meridian weakens. When the reserves drop below a critical threshold, the network collapses.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: When the Meridian fails, the seal on the First Root breaks. The entity beneath the city — the undifferentiated divine potential that the Meridian's weight keeps dormant — wakes. Everything Selin wanted to prevent by ending the gods on her terms happens anyway, but without any structure to contain it.

[SFX: Thessa — her voice. The Keeper. Cold. Precise. Not frightened. The fear is in the formality] [spot]

THESSA: Then the siphon is not merely a drain. It is a key to a cage.

KAYA: Yes.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Mika — through the speaker. Tinny. The ambulance bay background noise: an engine, radio chatter. Her voice clipped, professional. Mika in triage mode] [spot]

MIKA: What's the surface look like if this thing goes off.

KAYA: Catastrophic infrastructure failure. Every sacred site in the city destabilizes simultaneously. Sinkholes. Structural collapse. Water main ruptures. Everything the solstice caused, multiplied. And the seismic activity from the First Root's awakening on top of it.

MIKA: So I need to start moving people.

RUNE: Mika —

MIKA: Don't "Mika" me. If we have three days, I need at least two for evacuations. What's the cover story.

RUNE: Gas leak. That's — I can coordinate with emergency services. Gas leak protocol for the neighborhoods nearest the shrine, the harbor, the north quarter. All the active sites.

[SFX: bond-harmonic — Ash's frequency. Not the alarm from the harbor attack. Something colder. Older. The god-eater's circuitry fully engaged with the siphon's dissonance. He can feel it drawing. Feel the Meridian thinning. Feel the sacred sites losing pressure — hemorrhaging] [under]

ASH: I can map it. The siphon's connections through the Meridian. I can feel where it's drawing from. Which nodes are weakest. Which will fail first.

[SFX: Ash — pushing off the wall. Moving to the table. His hand on the map. His scars glowing faintly. The diagnostic running. The god-eater doing the thing he was built to do: interface with the divine network. But this time, to save it] [spot]

ASH: Harbor district first. The threshold node is already compromised from the solstice. The siphon is pulling hardest there. Then the north quarter — the new node is too young. It can't resist the drain. The cathedral holds longest. The last god's presence is too strong to drain quickly.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: That gives us a sequence. Harbor fails first, then the periphery, then the new nodes, then the cathedral last. The seal breaks when the cathedral node falls.

[SFX: Rune — his phone out. Already dialing. The paramedic and the anchor intersecting. The man who handles the human side of divine crises because someone has to and he's always been the one who steps in] [spot]

RUNE: I'm calling dispatch. Gas leak protocol, eastern district first. Mika, can you take the north quarter?

MIKA: On it. Keep me posted.

[SFX: the speaker cutting. Mika, gone. Moving. The surface response beginning while the underground team works the impossible math] [spot]

{Scene 2 — God-keeper holding facility. Day one. The interrogation.}

[AMBIENT: the cell — the same cell from Kaya's visit. Fluorescent buzz. Sealed concrete. Two chairs. But the air is different. Thessa in one chair. Selin in the other. Not Kaya's grief. Not the old wound. This is the Keeper and the prisoner. A chess match with the city on the board] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Thessa — seated. Still. The chair doesn't creak. Her voice formal, clipped] [spot]

[SFX: Selin — across the table. Her voice stripped of the Summoner's distortion. What remains: intelligence, precision, and the careful delivery of a woman who knows she has something they need and is deciding what it costs to give it] [spot]

THESSA: The siphon.

SELIN: My contingency. If the god-eater was contained or destroyed before the work was done, the siphon would complete the extraction over decades. A slower mercy. Less elegant. But inevitable.

THESSA: Can it be destroyed.

[SFX: Selin — a breath. The pause that means the answer is worse than the question] [spot]

SELIN: The siphon is anchored to the Meridian itself. The same ritual mathematics that bind the nodes, that maintain the seal, that hold the network together — I used them as the siphon's foundation. Breaking the device breaks the anchor points. Breaking the anchor points breaks the network.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: You built a bomb that cannot be defused without detonating it.

SELIN: I built a tool. The bomb was added later.

[Silence — 1500ms]

THESSA: Explain.

SELIN: I designed the siphon to activate under specific conditions — Meridian collapse. If the network failed naturally, the siphon would manage the energy release. A controlled drain instead of a catastrophic one. The dying gods would feed back into the earth gently. The First Root would remain sealed. It was not a weapon. It was a hospice.

[Beat — 400ms]

SELIN: I never installed a timer. Countdown activation was not part of my design.

[SFX: Thessa — the silence of a woman recalculating. The Keeper's mind working. The implication landing] [spot]

THESSA: Someone modified your work.

SELIN: After my capture. My laboratory was secured, but the Kindling had access before that. One of my followers — I do not know which — understood enough of the design to add a countdown trigger. My design drained slowly — decades. Theirs accelerated the extraction rate. Crude. Dangerous. They wanted to finish what I started. On their schedule. Without the safeguards.

THESSA: The security logs show access three days after the solstice. Before we secured the site.

[SFX: the fluorescent buzz — filling the gap. The ugliness of it. The sound of an ideology that outlived its founder] [spot]

THESSA: The enemy is not you.

SELIN: The enemy was never me. It was the idea. And ideas do not stop when you lock the person who spoke them in a concrete room.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: Can you disarm it.

SELIN: No. The modifications changed the triggering architecture. I cannot undo what I did not build.

THESSA: Then what can be done.

[SFX: Selin — shifting in the chair. A measured breath before speaking] [spot]

SELIN: Three options. Let the siphon run. The drain takes decades. The Meridian thins slowly. The gods die one by one. The seal weakens gradually. You buy time. Generations, perhaps. But the end is the same.

[Beat — 400ms]

SELIN: Destroy the siphon. The Meridian collapses instantly. The First Root wakes. The gods die. The seal breaks. Immediate catastrophe instead of slow decline.

[Beat — 400ms]

SELIN: Or the god-eater absorbs it. The siphon's function, the drain itself — a god-eater can take it into his body. Redirect the extraction from the Meridian into himself. But the power involved is four nodes' worth of accumulated divine energy channeled through a single vessel.

[Silence — 1500ms]

SELIN: It would kill him.

{Scene 3 — Beneath the eastern shrine. Day two. Kaya delivers the options.}

[AMBIENT: the laboratory — day two. Fewer people. The researchers have done their work. The monitoring equipment running. The siphon cycling. The whine a degree louder than yesterday — or maybe it's the same and Rune's hearing has sharpened because he hasn't slept] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Kaya — at the table. The maps. The diagrams. His voice strained. The measured cadence barely holding. The professor delivering the worst lecture of his career] [spot]

[SFX: the room — smaller now. Rune and Ash across from Kaya. Thessa standing. The core team. The people who will make the decision. The bond-harmonic between Ash and Rune running at a frequency Rune hasn't felt since before the vow: dread] [spot]

KAYA: Selin confirmed what our analysis suggested. The siphon cannot be destroyed without collapsing the Meridian. And it cannot be disarmed — the modifications are beyond her design. You understand. We have three options, and none of them are solutions.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: Option one. Let the siphon run. Slow drain. Decades. The Meridian weakens gradually. The gods fade. The seal deteriorates. We buy time but the outcome is terminal.

THESSA: Unacceptable. We do not choose a slow death for the gods we are sworn to protect.

KAYA: Option two. Destroy the siphon. Instant drain. The Meridian collapses. Everything we prevented at the solstice happens in minutes. The First Root wakes.

[Silence — 1500ms]

KAYA: Option three.

[SFX: Kaya's voice — changing. Tighter. The measured cadence faltering] [spot]

KAYA: A god-eater can absorb the siphon's function. Take the drain into yourself instead of the Meridian. The power would flow through you instead of through the network. The Meridian survives. The seal holds. The gods live.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: But the power involved —

ASH: Would kill me.

[DIRECTION: Flat. Subject. Verb. Object. The god-eater naming what the professor can't]

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: No.

[DIRECTION: The word arriving before the thought. The paramedic's reflex: when the patient codes, you say no and you work]

ASH: Rune —

RUNE: I said no.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — two frequencies colliding. Ash: calm. Resolved. The terrifying calm of someone who has already made the calculation. Rune: static. Interference. The anchor refusing the information] [under]

KAYA: There may be alternatives we have not yet —

RUNE: Then find them.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: You have a day. Find something else.

[SFX: Rune — standing. Chair scraping stone. Hands flat on the table. Jaw locked. Breathing controlled] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash watching Rune. Calm. Unsurprised by the refusal]

[Silence — 1500ms]

{Scene 4 — The city. Day two. Evacuations.}

[AMBIENT: the north quarter — daytime. Emergency vehicles. A PA system echoing instructions. The controlled chaos of a gas leak evacuation. Residents moving. Children. Dogs. The sound of a neighborhood being emptied under false pretenses by a paramedic who knows the real reason and can't say it] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Rune — on the ground. In uniform. Directing foot traffic. His radio crackling. Mika's voice on the channel, clipped, efficient. The intersection of his two worlds — the paramedic and the anchor — operating as one] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Gas leak. I say the words forty times in two hours. "Precautionary evacuation. Gas leak. Please take essential items. Temporary shelters are set up at the school gymnasium on Ninth." I say it to the old man who doesn't want to leave his cats. I say it to the mother with three kids and a bag already packed because she's been through this before. I say it to the teenager who asks if the building will collapse like the ones at the solstice and I look her in the eye and say "no, this is just a gas leak" and I lie. I lie with steady hands and a calm voice because that's what paramedics do. We manage the information so the patient doesn't panic.

[SFX: the evacuation continuing — vehicles moving. Doors. Voices. The choreography of displacement. Rune moving through it, directing, calm, professional. Through the bond: Ash, underground, mapping the siphon's network with the god-keepers. His concentration reaching Rune as a low pressure behind the eyes] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: Mika — approaching. On foot. Clipboard. She's run three blocks. Her breathing controlled] [spot]

MIKA: North quarter clear. Harbor district is seventy percent. The shelters are filling up.

RUNE: How long can we hold the cover.

MIKA: A day. Maybe two. After that people start pushing back. There's already a guy with a podcast claiming the government is hiding a sinkhole.

RUNE: He's not wrong.

MIKA: He thinks it's aliens.

[Beat — 400ms]

MIKA: Rune. The third option. Ash.

RUNE: Don't.

MIKA: I'm not saying —

RUNE: I know what you're not saying. And the answer is we find something else.

[SFX: Mika — a pause. The particular quality of her silence before she decides what to say] [spot]

MIKA: Okay. Then we find something else. But you need to sleep. You look like the before picture in a stress study.

[SFX: Rune — the exhale. Almost a laugh. Hollowed out] [spot]

RUNE: I'll sleep when the ground stops trying to eat the city.

MIKA: You'll sleep tonight or I'm calling Ash and telling him to sit on you.

RUNE: He would.

MIKA: I know. That's why it's a credible threat.

[SFX: the radio — crackling. Another update. Another block cleared] [spot]

{Scene 5 — The rooftop. Night. Day two. The argument.}

[AMBIENT: rooftop — night. The same rooftop. The same city below. But different. Quieter. The evacuated neighborhoods dark. The streetlights still on but the windows unlit. A city going hollow at the edges. Wind. The winter cold. The meridian-thrum audible up here — strained, weakening. The meridian-thrum — strained, weakening. The city's pulse dropping] [bed]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — wrong. Two frequencies that should be aligned, pulling apart. Strained. Discordant] [under]

[SFX: Rune — on the roof. Hands gripping the metal railing. Cold. His breathing slow, controlled, audible in the winter air] [spot]

[SFX: the roof door — opening behind him. Ash. His footsteps on concrete. The door closing. Heavy. Deliberate] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

ASH: You haven't slept.

RUNE: Neither have you.

ASH: I've been mapping the siphon's connection points. The cathedral node can hold another thirty-six hours. After that the drain reaches the central seal.

RUNE: Kaya's still looking for alternatives.

ASH: Kaya won't find one.

[Beat — 800ms]

[DIRECTION: The words landing flat between them. The god-eater delivering a verdict, not an argument]

RUNE: You don't know that.

ASH: I do. The siphon's integration with the Meridian is too deep. Selin built it into the foundation. There is no surgical option. No workaround. No clever third path that saves everything. There are three options. One kills the gods slowly. One kills them instantly. One kills me.

RUNE: Stop.

ASH: Rune —

RUNE: I said stop.

[SFX: Rune — turning. Facing Ash. The full paramedic stance: feet planted, shoulders square, hands at his sides. The posture of a man who is about to fight something that can't be fought with posture. His voice controlled. Clinical. The professional calm that is its own kind of scream] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash: calm, plain, discussing his own death without affect]

ASH: The slow drain gives you decades. The Meridian weakens. The gods fade. But the seal holds long enough for — I don't know. Something. A generation of scholars working the problem. A new god-eater. A technology that doesn't exist yet.

RUNE: That's option one.

ASH: Option one is borrowed time. And the interest rate is every sacred site in the city dying by inches while the people who live above them suffer the consequences. Sinkholes. Contamination. Quakes. For decades.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: I won't choose that. I won't choose a slow poison for the city that the vow was supposed to save.

RUNE: Then we destroy it. Option two. We take the hit. Rebuild.

ASH: The First Root wakes. You've read the texts. Kaya showed you. The First Root is not a god. It is pre-god. Raw divine potential with no consciousness and no restraint. If it surfaces, there is nothing — no vow, no bond, no god-eater — that can contain it. The city dies.

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's calm. That's the worst part. Not the words — the words are terrible, but I can fight words. I can argue with words. What I can't fight is the calm. The stillness in his voice. The steadiness. He sounds like the night of the vow — certain. Decided. The calm of a man who has already made the choice and is waiting for the world to catch up.

[SFX: Ash — stepping closer. Not all the way. Closing half the distance. His voice dropping. The late register at its quietest. The voice he uses for things that matter too much to say loudly] [spot]

ASH: I absorb the siphon. The drain redirects through me instead of the Meridian. The network stabilizes. The seal holds. The gods survive. The new god in the north quarter keeps growing. The city stands.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: And I die.

RUNE: No.

ASH: It's the only option that doesn't kill thousands of —

RUNE: I don't care.

[SFX: the words — out before Rune can catch them. The bond-harmonic spiking. A raw, involuntary transmission across the rooftop] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: That's not — I don't mean —

ASH: I know what you mean.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: the wind. The city below. The dark windows. The meridian-thrum weakening. The siphon, underground, cycling. The countdown that doesn't care about love or vows or a man's hands on a railing] [bed]

ASH: The vow binds me to you. Not you to me. If I die, the vow breaks. You survive. The bond dissolves. The anchor holds because the anchor was always the stronger architecture. You lose the circuit. You lose the harmonic. You lose the part of your nervous system that carries my heartbeat. But you live.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: Rune. You live.

RUNE: You think I want to survive that?

[Silence — 5000ms]

[SFX: the rooftop. Wind. The bond-harmonic stuttering — unsteady for the first time since the vow. Two frequencies pulling against each other. The circuit carrying too much] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He asks me to survive him. He says it like it's a gift. Like the vow dissolving and the bond going silent and his heartbeat disappearing from my chest is something I should want because it means I'm alive. And I stand on this roof and I look at him — the bandage on his arm, the scar behind his ear, the man who can't cook and loves the cello and reads medical textbooks to understand how I think — and I know. I know with the same certainty I've always known when a patient is going to make it or isn't. I know in my hands, in my chest, in the part of me that has been his since the night I touched him in the ashes and the lock turned.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He says it like he knows. Like the lore is settled. But the vow is a circuit — bidirectional, symbiotic. Kaya never said what happens to the anchor if the circuit breaks. Neither did Sable. Ash is guessing. He's guessing and calling it certainty because certainty makes the sacrifice cleaner.

RUNE (inner monologue): And even if he's right — even if the anchor holds and the body survives — I would not survive it. The body would. The heart would keep its hundred thousand contractions a day. The lungs would fill and empty. The hands would not shake. But the man who lives inside those systems — the one who wakes up reaching for the warmth beside him and falls asleep to a heartbeat that isn't his own — that man is gone the moment the bond goes silent. That's not survival. That's a body with the lights on and nobody home.

[SFX: Ash — waiting. His breathing audible in the cold. Through the bond: grief. Preemptive. Heavy] [spot]

[Silence — 2500ms]

[DIRECTION: Rune says nothing. Ten feet apart. If they touch, the argument ends and nothing is resolved and the siphon keeps counting]

[SFX: wind. The siphon's distant cycling, faint. No one moving. No one speaking] [bed]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — raw. Unfiltered. Two heartbeats offset, the rhythm wrong. The circuit bearing a load it was never designed for] [under]

[Silence — 5000ms]

[SFX: the city below. A siren — not for them. The meridian-thrum, weak. The siphon, cycling, underground, counting. And on the rooftop: nothing. The absence of resolution. The hook that will not let go] [bed, fade-out]
