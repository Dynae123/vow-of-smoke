# Episode 18: "Fever"

**One job:** Rune's condition crosses a line. The pretending ends. Ash says "I need you" and means it without the bond as an excuse. The first kiss — not gentle, not careful, not rational. The emotional midpoint of the series: two people who have run out of reasons not to want each other.
**The moment:** "This isn't the bond. I know what the bond feels like. This is me." The sentence that ends the argument they've been having since the shrine.

---

{Scene 1 — Ambulance bay. Morning. Rune falling apart.}

[AMBIENT: ambulance bay — morning shift change, engines, radio chatter, the metallic bustle of a station gearing up. Cold air] [bed]

[SFX: Rune's footsteps — measured, deliberate. The gait of someone focusing on walking correctly] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): Tuesday. Shift starts at six. I'm here at five-forty because Mika is drawing blood again before clock-in and I need the twenty minutes.

RUNE (inner monologue): The mirror. I look at the mirror. The face looking back has lost the layer between skin and bone that makes a face look like it belongs to someone who sleeps. My eyes are dark underneath — not bruise dark, hollow dark. The kind of dark that's not a symptom of anything specific. It's the color of depletion.

[SFX: Mika entering — her locker beside his. Kit already in hand. The practiced efficiency of a repeat procedure] [spot]

MIKA: Arm.

RUNE: Good morning to you too.

MIKA: I said good morning three days ago and you told me to stop being gentle. So. Arm.

RUNE (inner monologue): She doesn't ask how I'm feeling. She stopped asking two days ago, when I lied and she clocked the lie before I finished the sentence. Now she draws blood and reads the numbers and the numbers say what I won't.

[SFX: vacutainer filling. Mika's breathing steady, professional] [under]

MIKA: Nosebleed this morning?

RUNE: Small one.

MIKA: How small.

RUNE: A minute. Maybe less. It stopped on its own.

MIKA: INR?

RUNE: I don't have a point-of-care INR kit at home, Mika.

MIKA: I'll have one for you tonight. And a blood pressure cuff. Non-negotiable.

[SFX: needle out. Cotton ball. Tape. The practiced choreography] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — involuntary, sudden. Rune's perspective splitting for half a second] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Rune's voice cuts mid-breath — a half-second of disorientation. When he speaks again, the register shifts slightly, as if he was somewhere else and snapped back]

RUNE (inner monologue): The room folds. For half a second I'm here and at the window and the window is Ash's window and the city is the view from my apartment and then it snaps back and I'm in the locker room and Mika's hand is on my shoulder.

MIKA: Rune.

RUNE: I'm fine. Stood up too fast.

MIKA: You're sitting down.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: Yeah.

[SFX: Mika's hand — grip shifting from shoulder to the back of his neck. Checking temperature] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Mika's touch reads as clinical — checking temperature — but her hand lingers a beat too long]

MIKA: You're warm.

RUNE: It's the locker room. The heating —

MIKA: You're warm and your eyes just went somewhere that wasn't here and your nosebleed this morning was probably longer than a minute and you are going to tell me the truth right now or I am pulling you from active rotation.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE: The vision thing is new. Started yesterday. I see through his eyes sometimes. Not on purpose. It's like the bond hiccups and for a second I'm in two places.

MIKA: How often?

RUNE: Twice yesterday. Once this morning.

MIKA: The nosebleed.

RUNE: Five minutes. I timed it.

[SFX: Mika sitting down on the bench beside him] [spot]

MIKA: We're past the monitoring phase, Rune.

RUNE: I know.

MIKA: The bloodwork is going to come back worse than last time. I can see it in your face.

RUNE: I know.

MIKA: Then what are we doing? What is the plan?

RUNE: The plan is the aqueduct. Four days. We stop the summoner, we stop the kill pattern, we buy time to figure out the bond.

MIKA: And if the bond figures you out first?

[SFX: locker room — someone else entering. Distant. The noise of a shift beginning] [under]

RUNE: Then you pull me out. Like you said.

[SFX: Mika standing — the lab tubes in her pocket, her bag on her shoulder] [spot]

MIKA: I'll have the results by end of shift. Don't push through the bond today. Don't anchor him. Don't do anything heroic. Can you do that for eight hours?

RUNE: I'll try.

MIKA: Not good enough.

RUNE: It's what I've got.

[SFX: Mika's footsteps leaving. The locker room door. Rune alone with the mirror and the fluorescent] [spot]

{Scene 2 — Rune's apartment. Evening. The confrontation.}

[AMBIENT: apartment, evening — the light going. Kitchen. Bond-hum-agitated, running beneath everything. Ash has been waiting] [bed]

[SFX: apartment door — Rune entering. The keys miss the counter. Clatter on the floor. Rune doesn't pick them up] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I get home and I'm tired in a way that isn't about the shift. The shift was fine. Routine calls. Mika drove. I didn't push through the bond. I didn't anchor. I just — worked. And the tiredness is underneath the work, in the place where the bond draws from, and it doesn't care if I'm on a call or sitting in the ambulance or sleeping. It just takes.

[SFX: Ash — already in the kitchen. Standing. Not at the window. In the center of the room. Planted] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash delivers his opening line with no preamble — no greeting, no pause. The voice of someone who has been rehearsing this for hours]

[SFX: bond-hum-agitated — sharp. Ash's frequency, not Rune's. The agitation pre-existing, aimed] [under]

RUNE: Hey.

ASH: Your temperature is elevated. I have been feeling it through the bond since noon. It spiked at two-seventeen and has not returned to baseline.

RUNE: I'm fine.

ASH: Your vision split three times today. I felt each instance — the bond inverts when it happens. Like a current reversing. It was disorienting on my end. I can only imagine what it does to yours.

RUNE: Ash —

ASH: The nosebleed this morning lasted five minutes and twelve seconds. I timed it from across the city through the bond because I felt the moment your blood pressure dropped and the moment it stabilized and the thirteen-second gap in between where I was not certain your heart would maintain rhythm.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-agitated — building. Sharpening into something harder to name] [under]

ASH: You are lying to everyone. Going to work. Running calls. Pretending to Mika that the monitoring is enough. Coming home and smiling at me as though I cannot feel you breaking apart from the inside.

RUNE: I'm not pretending. I'm managing.

ASH: You are managing the way a bridge manages rust. The structure holds until it does not. And when it fails it fails completely.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: What do you want me to do? Quit? Sit on the couch and watch my hands bruise? People need me at work. Kaya needs me for the aqueduct. You need me to —

ASH: I need you alive.

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice drops, not rises. The volume decreasing as the weight increases]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He said it. The two words that have been building between us since the spring. Since before the spring. Since the night he sat on the floor and read about intubation. Since the bathroom. Since the rooftop. The two words that aren't about the bond or the anchor or the Meridian or any of it.

RUNE: I don't have the luxury of falling apart. People need me.

[SFX: Ash's step — one, forward. Not aggressive. Deliberate. The shadows at his feet tightening, involuntary] [spot]

ASH: I need you.

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — massive, involuntary. Not the anchor frequency. Not the parasitic draw. Something else entirely] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's own line surprises him. A beat of silence after — deliver the next lines with the unguarded quality of someone who has said more than he intended]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He didn't mean to say it. Or he did and didn't expect it to be true. The shock on his face is real — the look of someone who has just fallen through a floor they didn't know was there.

RUNE: The bond —

ASH: This is not the bond.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: I know what the bond feels like. The anchor draw. The empathic bleed. The current between us that exists because someone carved marks into my skin and chose you to hold the other end. I know what the bond feels like, Rune.

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice — barely audible. The resonance thinning to something private, unarmored. The voice of the person he was before the altar]

ASH: This isn't the bond. This is me.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: I need you. Not the anchor. You.

[SFX: the kitchen continuing — the fridge, the clock, the maps on the table] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): My hands are shaking. Not the tremor from the drain — a different shake. The kind that starts in the chest and works outward. The kind that has nothing to do with platelet counts or clotting factors.

{Scene 3 — Kitchen. The kiss.}

[AMBIENT: the kitchen ambient continuing — but narrowing. Fewer sounds reaching in. The world pulling back] [bed]

[SFX: Rune's step — one step forward. His boot on the kitchen tile. The distance between them halving] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): I step forward. One step. That's all.

[SFX: Ash closing the distance — two steps. His hands finding Rune — one on the back of his neck, one at his waist. The contact immediate, total] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum-incandescent — a new variant, brighter and fuller than any previous bond tone. The sound of a circuit completing] [spot]

[DIRECTION: The kiss is not gentle. Urgent, desperate. Both of them breathing hard into it — no grace, no choreography. The sound of two people colliding]

[SFX: the kiss — breath caught. Fabric gripping. Rune's hands in Ash's hair] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Not choreographed. Clumsy. The real physical sounds — fabric gripping, a stumbled step, caught breath]

RUNE (inner monologue): His mouth.

[SFX: Rune's back against the kitchen counter — the impact, light, the edge of the counter against his spine. Maps on the table rustling from the movement] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Smoke. He tastes like smoke. Not fire — the aftermath of fire. The thing that's left when the burning stops. And my hands are in his hair and his hands are on my waist and the bond is singing — not humming, not pulsing, singing, a sound I've never felt before that fills every hollow the drain carved out.

[SFX: Ash's hands at Rune's waist — the fabric of Rune's shirt shifting. The temperature of Ash's palms rising, a faint hiss of heat against cloth] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Temperature rising — Ash's thermal response. The hiss of heat against fabric is the audio cue]

[SFX: breathing — close, ragged, shared. The intimate acoustics of breath synchronizing, two rhythms collapsing into one] [under]

[SFX: the separation — foreheads together. Both breathing hard. The bond tone settling from incandescent to something deep and warm and continuous] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Neither pulls away. The heat from Ash's hands subsides — the SFX hiss fading]

RUNE (inner monologue): Foreheads. His and mine. The bridge of his nose against my cheek. His breath on my mouth. His eyes — dark but clear. Not the black of the god-eater. Not the hunger. Just Ash. Just want. My thumb finds the scar on his wrist — the parenthesis mark from the binding, the one I traced in the dark weeks ago. It's warm. It's always warm.

ASH (barely audible): Rune.

RUNE (rough): We shouldn't —

ASH: I know.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — humming at a new, higher frequency. The one that started when their mouths met] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Neither of us moves. The "shouldn't" sits in the air between our mouths and neither of us steps back to give it room. His forehead against mine. His breath coming down. My hands still in his hair. The bond at a frequency I've never heard before — not draining, not neutral, not settled. Alive. Like it was waiting for this. Like it was always going to sound like this and everything before was warm-up.

[SFX: Ash's thumb — tracing a line along Rune's hip, through the shirt. Involuntary] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): His thumb on my hip. Through the shirt. A line. Back and forth. He might not know he's doing it.

ASH (still barely audible): Your fever is worse.

RUNE: Yeah.

ASH: That may be my fault.

RUNE: I don't care.

[SFX: Ash exhaling — a rough sound. Not a laugh. The breath of someone undone] [spot]

ASH: You should care.

RUNE: Probably.

[SFX: Ash pulling back — an inch. Two. The forehead contact breaking] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — settling. Not to neutral. To a new baseline. Higher, warmer, more present than anything before] [under]

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. Late. The aftermath.}

[AMBIENT: apartment, late night — the city dimmed. The lamp by the window. The kitchen exactly as they left it — maps, cold tea, the counter where his back was. The bond humming at its new frequency] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Rune in bed — on his back. Staring at the ceiling. His fingers touching his own mouth] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): They left the kitchen separately. That was two hours ago. Ash is on the couch. Not in the bedroom. The bed they shared last night is occupied by only me and the memory of what happened against the counter and I am staring at the ceiling and my mouth still feels like someone else's mouth was on it because it was.

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — the new baseline. Not settled the way it was last night. Not agitated. Something between] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He kissed me. I kissed him. The distinction is academic. There was a moment where there were two separate people in a kitchen and then there was a moment where there weren't and my back was against the counter and his hands were on my waist and glowing and I could taste smoke on his mouth and the bond didn't drain. It sang.

[SFX: Rune's hand dropping from his mouth to his chest — flat palm, feeling his own heartbeat] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's there. Through the wall. Not sleeping. Not reading. Just — existing. On the couch. Probably looking at the ceiling the same way I'm looking at the ceiling. Both of us in separate rooms staring up and thinking about the same three minutes in a kitchen.

[SFX: the radiator ticking. The clock. The city] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The kiss didn't drain. The bond sang instead of pulled. And I don't know what that means — whether the singing is just a different kind of cost I haven't been invoiced for yet. Whether the body keeps score even when the bond doesn't. My fever is worse tonight than it was this morning. The singing and the cost might not be separate things.

[SFX: bond-hum — pulsing gently. The rhythm of two heartbeats in two rooms, synced without trying] [under]

[SFX: from the living room — Ash shifting on the couch. One movement. Then still again. Then, barely audible, a breath that might be Rune's name] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He kissed me. The god-eater kissed me in the kitchen and said my name against my mouth and his hands were glowing and the bond sang and I can still feel exactly where his thumb traced my hip through my shirt.

[SFX: Rune turning on his side — facing the wall. The sheets pulling. Eyes open in the dark] [spot]

[SFX: the apartment at its quietest — radiator, distant traffic, the clock. No bond-hum. Just the building breathing around them, neither sleeping] [bed, fade-out over episode end]

RUNE (inner monologue): I think the line was never there. The one between the bond and the person. Between the anchor and the man. Between what the ritual made and what we chose. I've been looking for that line since the shrine, and he stood in the kitchen and told me: this is not the bond. This is me.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I believe him. And my fever is climbing. And the bond sang. And somewhere in the space between the singing and the fever is the answer to whether this saves me or finishes me, and I don't have it yet.
