## 1. Cross-Batch Fact Drift

### Thessa's "Two weeks ago, four gods were still alive" -- Repeated Line, Shifting Reference

- **Ep10** (line 211): THESSA: "Two weeks ago, four gods were still alive."
- **Ep22** (line 139): THESSA: "Two weeks ago, four gods were still alive."

In Ep10, this refers to two weeks before the central park collapse (~day 10-14 of story). By Ep22, Ash says "You tried to cage me two weeks ago," and Thessa echoes the same line. But story time has advanced significantly -- the spring (Ep15) and aqueduct (Ep20) events have occurred. If "two weeks ago" in Ep22 still refers to four gods alive, the total elapsed story time from Ep6 (cage attempt) to Ep22 is only about two weeks, which compresses Eps 6-22 into roughly 14 days.

This is internally consistent if the story moves fast (plausible given the timeline markers: "twelve days" in Ep13, "three weeks" in Ep16, "four weeks" in Ep19). However, the identical phrasing creates the impression of a copy-paste rather than intentional echo.

> :yellow_circle: **Warning** -- Verify that the "two weeks ago" in Ep22 is correct relative to the established timeline. If Ep6's confrontation is the cage attempt, and Ep19 says "four weeks" have passed since the bond, then by Ep22 more than two weeks have passed since the cage attempt. Consider adjusting to "three weeks ago" or "a month ago" in Ep22.

### God-Death Count Arithmetic

The kill sequence across all 22 episodes:
1. **Shrine god** -- pre-series (faded/consumed; site where Ash was sealed)
2. **Meridian Temple god** -- Ep3-4 (confirmed dead; "the second event" per Kaya Ep4)
3. **Grove god** -- Ep7 (confirmed dead)
4. **Central Park / Great Temple god** -- Ep10 (confirmed dead; "three gods in ten days" per Kaya)
5. **Spring god** -- Ep15 (confirmed dead; ritual completed despite intervention)
6. **Aqueduct god** -- Ep20 (dormant, NOT dead; "the spring is dormant. Not dead" per Thessa)

Kaya's "three gods in ten days" in Ep10 counts Meridian Temple + Grove + Central Park = 3. This excludes the shrine god, which was pre-series. Correct.

Ep21 says "Four gods dead or dying." This counts Meridian Temple + Grove + Central Park + Spring = 4. The aqueduct god is dormant but not dead. Correct.

Thessa's "four gods still alive" in Ep10 implies the starting roster was at least 7 (4 alive + 3 just killed). But Ep10 also says "Two major nodes remain active" and the summoner's photos in Ep12 show "every sacred site in the city." STORY-BIBLE.md says there are "dozens of active sites" but "many have faded naturally." The specific count of living gods at series start is never pinned down.

> :green_circle: **Note** -- The god-count arithmetic is internally consistent but relies on the audience not tracking the exact total roster. The number of gods alive at series start is deliberately vague (STORY-BIBLE says "dozens" historically, but many faded). The kill pattern tracks only the summoner's targets, not the full roster. No fix needed, but consider establishing a firm number if future episodes reference it.

### Kaya's Vow Knowledge Timeline

- **Ep6** (line 244): Kaya tells Rune the Vow of Smoke exists -- "the oldest of the binding rites" that "permanently anchors the god-eater's humanity."
- **Ep19** (line 214): Kaya says "I told Rune about the Vow of Smoke weeks ago. The existence of it. The principle... What I did not tell him -- what I did not fully understand at the time -- are the mechanics. The cost."
- **Ep19** (line 220): Kaya reveals the full mechanics -- symbiotic bond, permanent humanity, godhood foreclosed.

This cross-batch thread is clean. Ep6 gives the concept; Ep19 gives the mechanics. Kaya explicitly acknowledges the gap in Ep19. No contradiction.

> :green_circle: **Note** -- Well-structured information layering across batch boundary.

### Ep13 "Twelve Days" vs. Ep16 "Three Weeks" vs. Ep19 "Four Weeks"

- Ep16 (line 170): Kaya says "someone three weeks into an unmanaged anchor bond."

This implies: Ep13 = ~day 12, Ep16 = ~day 21, Ep19 = ~day 28. Events spanning Eps 13-19 cover roughly 16 days. This fits the pacing (spring prep in Ep14, spring operation Ep15, recovery Ep16, several days pass through Eps 17-18, kiss Ep18, morning after Ep19).

> :green_circle: **Note** -- Timeline markers are consistent across batches.

## 2. Cumulative Character Knowledge States

### What Each Character Knows by Ep22

**Potential Issue -- Mika's Knowledge of the Vow of Smoke:**

Mika is told about the bond's parasitic nature in Ep13. She learns about the spring operation (Ep14). She is present for the spring descent (Ep14-15). She draws blood and monitors Rune through Eps 16-18. But there is no scene where Mika is explicitly told about the Vow of Smoke or its mechanics. Kaya's Ep19 call is to Rune (and Ash is present), but Mika is not mentioned as being in the room.

By Ep22, the Vow is being discussed openly as the solution. If Mika is present or informed off-screen, this works. But the script never shows Mika learning about the Vow.

> :yellow_circle: **Warning** -- Mika's knowledge of the Vow of Smoke is never established on-screen. If she needs to reference it in future episodes, an exposition scene or acknowledgment is needed. If she remains unaware, that's a dramatic lever -- but it should be intentional.

**Potential Issue -- Thessa's Knowledge of the Summoner's Identity:**

Thessa knows about the Kindling (Ep22). She knows there's a traitor in the god-keepers (Ep22, "seven acolytes I can confirm loyal... out of thirty-two"). But the scripts never show Thessa learning about the summoner's chamber under Saint Erasmus (discovered Ep12) or the ritual circle details. Kaya has this information; Ash and Rune have it. The ceasefire intelligence-sharing is mentioned but the specific transfer of this information to Thessa is not dramatized.

> :green_circle: **Note** -- Off-screen intelligence sharing is plausible given the ceasefire. No fix required unless a future scene depends on Thessa NOT knowing about the chamber.

## 4. Ash's Register Evolution

### Contraction Tracking Across All 22 Episodes

Per SPEECH-PATTERNS.md:
- **Eps 1-10:** Contraction frequency = **Never**
- **Eps 11-20:** Contraction frequency = **Low**
- **Eps 21-40:** Contraction frequency = **Medium**

**Actual contraction usage in Ash's dialogue lines:**

- **Eps 1-10:** ZERO contractions found in Ash's dialogue. "Define 'okay'" in Ep3 is not a contraction -- he's interrogating the word. Fully compliant.
- **Eps 11-20:** ZERO contractions found in Ash's dialogue lines (grep across all ep11-20 scripts). This is slightly stricter than "Low" would suggest. Not a violation -- "Low" permits zero -- but the intended evolution isn't visible yet.
- **Eps 21-22:** First contractions appear:
  - Ep21 (line 332): "a purpose I **can't** see"
  - Ep21 (line 354): "I **can't** stop you" and "what**'s** between us"
  - Ep22 (line 323): "I **don't** know if 'okay' covers it"
  - Ep22 (line 341): "It **hasn't** diminished"

> :yellow_circle: **Warning** -- Ash's register evolution from "Never" to "Low" contractions is supposed to begin in the Eps 11-20 range, but ZERO contractions appear in Ash's dialogue for Eps 11-20. The jump from zero contractions (Eps 1-20) to multiple contractions (Eps 21-22) is abrupt. Consider seeding 1-2 contractions in key emotional moments in the mid-range (e.g., Ep18's "I need you" scene, Ep16's doorway scene) to smooth the transition.

### Slang/Colloquialism Tracking

Per SPEECH-PATTERNS.md: "Ash NEVER uses slang or colloquialisms in the first ten episodes. He does not say 'okay,' 'cool,' 'yeah,' or 'sure' until mid-series."

- Ep3: ASH: "Define 'okay.'" -- Not natural use; he's rejecting the word. Compliant.
- Ep16: ASH: "you are not okay" -- Using the word to mirror/reject Rune's claim. Borderline compliant.
- Ep22: ASH: "I don't know if 'okay' covers it." -- First natural use. Appropriate for late-range.

> :green_circle: **Note** -- Ash's relationship to the word "okay" is a subtle three-beat arc (Ep3: rejects it, Ep16: throws it back, Ep22: adopts it cautiously). Whether intentional or not, this works well.

### Sentence Length Evolution

Early Ash (Eps 1-10) targets 4-8 words per sentence. Late Ash (Eps 21+) targets 8-14 words.

Spot-checking Ep21 line 332: "Of course I am scared. I am a weapon and you are the thing that makes me vulnerable and someone built us both for a purpose I can't see." The second sentence is 25 words. Ep21 line 354 contains a 30+ word sentence.

> :yellow_circle: **Warning** -- Ash's sentence length in Ep21-22 exceeds the 8-14 word target for Late Ash, reaching 25-30+ words in emotional monologues. These are powerful lines and cutting them would lose impact, but they violate the stated constraint. Consider either updating the SPEECH-PATTERNS.md target for Late Ash emotional peaks, or breaking the longest speeches into shorter syntactic units with periods or em-dashes.

## 6. Thematic Coherence: "Choosing to Be Human/Small"

The core thematic question of the series: Can a thousand-year-old divine weapon choose to be human? And is that choice worth the cost?

### Theme Arc Across 22 Episodes

- **Eps 1-4:** Ash as alien/threat. No humanity visible. The theme is latent.
- **Eps 5-7:** First cracks. Ash monitors Rune's dreams (Ep5). Ash feels shame at the grove (Ep7). The "choosing" begins as involuntary feeling.
- **Eps 8-10:** Ash on the rooftop (Ep8) -- "I am learning to be small." First articulation of the theme. Ash chooses NOT to feed at the park (Ep10) -- the first major act of choosing human restraint.
- **Eps 11-12:** Ash's pre-transformation memory (Ep12) -- he was human. The scars were carved into a person. The theme deepens: it's not choosing to BECOME human, it's choosing to REMEMBER being human.
- **Eps 13-16:** The cost of the choice becomes visible. Rune deteriorating (Ep13). Ash's fingers on Rune's jaw (Ep14) -- choosing tenderness. Ash reading Rune's textbook aloud (Ep16) -- choosing care. Kaya's pendant story (Ep13) -- parallel theme of loving a divine being.
- **Eps 17-18:** Ash in Rune's sweatshirt (Ep17) -- choosing domestic. The kiss (Ep18) -- "This is not the bond. This is me." The definitive statement of the theme: the human feeling is REAL, not engineered.
- **Eps 19-20:** The Vow of Smoke formalizes the choice -- permanent humanity traded for godhood. Ash at the aqueduct (Ep20) -- resists feeding again, with greater difficulty.
- **Eps 21-22:** The summoner's revelation threatens the theme -- was the "choosing" itself designed? Ep21 destabilizes trust. Ep22 restores it through a new frame: god-eaters were ORIGINALLY vessels/caretakers, not weapons. The "human choice" isn't a betrayal of Ash's nature -- it IS his nature, corrupted.

> :green_circle: **Note** -- The thematic arc is the strongest structural element of the series. The Ep22 revelation (god-eaters as caretakers) retroactively enriches every previous "choosing to be human" moment. No thematic drift detected.
