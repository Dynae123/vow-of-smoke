# Episode 22: "Schism"

**One job:** The safe house attack reveals the scale of the summoner's operation — followers called the Kindling. Thessa's certainty fractures into need. And Kaya discovers the god-eater's original purpose was preservation, not destruction — reframing everything Ash is and everything they've been fighting.
**The moment:** "I was made to save gods. Not eat them." — The sentence that gives Ash a new origin and cracks a thousand years of shame.

---

{Scene 2 — The safe house. The Kindling revealed.}

[AMBIENT: same — the sounds settling. The urgency of triage giving way to the heavier quiet of people realizing the scope of what they face] [bed]

[SFX: Rune finishing with the concussed acolyte — bandage, stabilizing position] [spot]

[SFX: the other two acolytes sitting up, holding gauze to their own cuts] [under]
[DIRECTION: The acolytes' voices are tight, clipped — people who failed at the thing they trained for]

[SFX: Thessa pacing — three steps, turn, three steps] [spot]

THESSA: In two thousand years, the god-keepers have never been breached from within. The schisms were doctrinal. The expulsions were clean. We have never been — infiltrated.

KAYA: The summoner was one of us, Thessa. Expelled, yes. But trained in our methods. Versed in our doctrine. They did not need to infiltrate. They needed to recruit.

KAYA: The old texts describe splinter movements — factions within and adjacent to the god-keepers who believed the gods should die. Not from cruelty. From conviction. They saw the gods as an anchor holding humanity in a pre-modern relationship with the earth. The gods as obstacle to progress. As imprisonment dressed as protection.

ASH: Zealots.

KAYA: Believers. The distinction matters. A zealot acts from fervor. A believer acts from the certainty that they are saving the world. The people who attacked this safe house — they believe what they are doing is right.

[SFX: Thessa stopping mid-pace — her voice dropping below its usual register] [spot]
[DIRECTION: The cold authority replaced by something rawer]

THESSA: They call themselves the Kindling.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: It surfaced in intercepted communications two months ago. I dismissed it. An online forum. Rhetoric. I believed it was marginal — disaffected god-keeper descendants and amateur occultists playing at revolution.

[SFX: Thessa's hand on the scorched wall — touching the mark] [spot]

THESSA: I was wrong.

[SFX: bond-hum — a brief shift in Rune's frequency] [under]
[DIRECTION: Thessa admitting error. A first]

RUNE (inner monologue): She was wrong. Thessa — who does not trail off, does not joke, does not apologize — just said she was wrong. And what it means in this ruined safe house is that the last pillar of the old order is cracking.

[SFX: the concussed acolyte stirring — a groan. Rune's hand on her shoulder, steadying] [spot]

RUNE: Easy. Don't move. Ambulance is coming.

ACOLYTE (dazed): They said — they said the gods should have died centuries ago. That we were keeping them alive like — machines. Hooked up to machines that should be turned off.

ACOLYTE: They said the Kindling would finish what nature started.

[SFX: Ash — standing in the center of the room. Shadows still] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Ash is completely still during this exchange. When he speaks, his voice is low and careful]

ASH: Kindling. The thing you light to start a fire.

KAYA: The thing that burns first. Before the larger wood catches. They see themselves as the spark.

ASH: And I am the wood.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Thessa turning to Rune and Ash — her posture shifting] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Thessa's cadence changes — still formal, but the sharp edges are gone. She is asking, not commanding]

THESSA: My order is fractured. The traitor is still unidentified. I have seven acolytes I can confirm loyal. Seven, out of thirty-two in this city. The rest — I do not know. I cannot trust my own people and I cannot protect the remaining sacred sites with what I have.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: I need the god-eater's power.

[Silence — 1500ms]
[DIRECTION: Ash's silence — long enough to be its own statement]

ASH: You tried to cage me five weeks ago.

THESSA: Five weeks ago, four gods were still alive.

[SFX: bond-hum — Rune's frequency. The memory carried through the bond] [under]
[DIRECTION: The memory of Thessa calling Ash "it." The memory of her at his door, demanding containment. And now, in a broken safe house, asking for help]

RUNE (inner monologue): Five weeks ago. When she stood in my doorway and looked at Ash like a disease. Five weeks. Four dead gods. A city cracking. And Thessa, who has never needed anything from anyone, standing in the ruins of her operation and asking the abomination for help.

THESSA: You can sense the divine. You can feel the remaining gods, assess their strength, predict which the summoner will target. My people cannot do this. Our sensitivity is — limited. Yours is the instrument we need.

[SFX: Ash looking at Rune — the bond carrying the look before Rune sees it] [under]
[DIRECTION: Not asking permission. A question that isn't "should I" but "are we"]

ASH: The hunger does not stop because we are on the same side. You understand this. I can map the remaining sites. I can feel the gods. But standing close to them — the pull will be there. Every time.

THESSA: I am aware.

ASH: And you accept this.

[Beat — 400ms]
[DIRECTION: Thessa pauses — longer than her usual authoritative beats. When she resumes, her voice is quieter]

THESSA: You stood in the aqueduct chamber with a dying god and chose not to feed. With no witness. No anchor. No reason to refuse except the reason you carry in the bond.

THESSA: I accept this.

[SFX: fabric shifting — Ash nodding. The shadows at his feet easing into a new configuration] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Not watchful-hostile. Watchful-aligned]

RUNE (inner monologue): The alliance was always uneasy. The ceasefire was always fragile. But this — Thessa asking and Ash accepting — this is something different. Not trust. Not yet. But the recognition that the enemy of my enemy is standing in my ruined living room and I need him more than I need my pride.

{Scene 3 — The safe house. Later. Kaya and the old texts.}

[AMBIENT: the safe house quieting — the ambulance has come and gone, the injured acolyte transported. Plywood hammering, plastic sheeting. The sounds of people making a broken space functional because they have nowhere else to go] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: a table cleared of debris — Kaya spreading texts. Old papers, documents Thessa's people salvaged. Flashlights arranged for reading light] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — settling into the new context. The dissonance from the rooftop argument backgrounded, dull instead of sharp] [under]

[SFX: folding chair scraping — Rune sitting, coat still on. First-aid supplies packed away] [spot]

[SFX: Ash across the table — hands moving across the city grid, marking remaining sacred sites. Pausing, fingers hovering] [under]
[DIRECTION: Sensing through the bond and through whatever deeper sense the god-eater carries]

ASH: The north quarter node is stable. The god there is small — a threshold god, dormant but intact. The harbor district — weaker. I can feel the decay from here. And the cathedral...

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: meridian-thrum — brief, low. Pulsing once through the floor as Ash speaks, as if responding to the mention of the network] [under]

ASH: The cathedral is the strongest. The oldest. The anchor of the Meridian. If the summoner targets it last, the accumulated power from every dead god flows into the breach. The collapse would be total.

THESSA: The cathedral is our most fortified site. Three senior keepers on rotation. Wards renewed weekly.

ASH: The wards are designed for divine incursion. The Kindling are mortal. With harvested power. Your wards filter for the wrong frequency.

[Beat — 400ms]
[DIRECTION: Thessa hearing something she does not want to hear and cannot argue with]

KAYA: There is something else.

[SFX: Kaya looking up from the texts — reading glasses pushed up, his finger on a passage he's been circling with his pen] [spot]

KAYA: I have been following the modified ritual symbol — the one from the summoner's circle. The pre-human element. I traced it through the restricted texts that Thessa's archive made accessible tonight.

[SFX: old parchment sliding across the table — laminated, covered in symbols that predate written language] [spot]

KAYA: This is from the foundational records. The earliest account of the god-eater ritual. Not the god-keeper version. The original. Written — if the dating is correct — before the god-keepers existed as an order.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: The original purpose of the god-eater was not destruction.

[SFX: all ambient activity dropping — hammering stops, movement stops. Sudden hush] [spot]

[Silence — 2500ms]

KAYA: The earliest texts describe the god-eater as a vessel. A living repository. When a god died — naturally, from the fading of its sacred site, from the natural end of its lifecycle — the energy had to be managed. If it dissipated violently, it destabilized the Meridian. The god-eater was created to absorb that energy and hold it. Safely. Until it could be redistributed through the network.

[SFX: bond-hum — something happening beneath the regular frequency. Ash's frequency deepening, broadening] [under]
[DIRECTION: The bond-hum shifts audibly here — a deeper register, as if the bond itself is responding to this information]

KAYA: The god-eater was not a weapon. It was a — the nearest translation is caretaker. A spiritual shock absorber. A being designed to catch what falls so the system does not break.

KAYA: The god-eater was always meant to save the system. Not destroy it.

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Save. Not destroy. The word lands in the room and I watch it hit Ash the way I've watched medications hit patients in crisis — the delay, the silence while it travels from the ear to the place where it matters, and then the change. The visible, physical change.

[SFX: Ash's hand lifting from the map — slowly. Palm open] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Ash goes quiet. When he speaks again, his voice has changed — something has unlocked. Deliver "I was made to save gods" with genuine discovery]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's looking at his scars. The binding marks that Kaya identified in week one — the marks that said "weapon," that said "leash," that said "god-eater." The same marks. But the word for what they mean just changed.

KAYA: The corruption came later. The god-keepers — our predecessors, in the early organization — they saw the god-eater's power and repurposed the ritual. The vessel became a weapon. The caretaker became a consumer. The original intention was buried and the modified version — the one that strips humanity, that creates the hunger as a feature rather than a flaw — became the standard rite.

THESSA (quietly): We perverted it.

KAYA: The order perverted it. Centuries ago. Before your time. Before mine. But yes. The god-keepers took a being made to save gods and turned it into a being that devours them. The hunger is not the design. It is the damage.

[SFX: chair scraping back — Ash standing. Not violent. Deliberate] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the deeper resonance building. A frequency Rune has never heard before] [under]
[DIRECTION: Not agitation. Not the dissonance from the summoner's call. Something tectonic. A foundation being rebuilt beneath a standing structure]

ASH: I was made to save gods. Not eat them.

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice — unfamiliar. Not hostile, not vulnerable, not furious. Something new: wonder mixed with grief. The delivery should feel like a first breath]

ASH: I was made to save them. And they turned me into the thing that kills them.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Rune — standing, crossing the room. His hand on Ash's forearm. Simple, deliberate contact] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Not the wrist grip of the aqueduct, not the jaw-touch of the bathroom. The forearm. One person grounding another]

[SFX: bond-hum — the deep resonance meeting Rune's frequency. The dissonance from the summoner's call breaking apart] [under]
[DIRECTION: Not because the argument is resolved. Because the ground beneath the argument has shifted. The shame that has been running beneath Ash since the shrine just lost its foundation]

RUNE (inner monologue): My hand on his arm. The bond carrying what my mouth can't say fast enough — that the hunger he's hated, the nature he's fought, the thing he refused to be in the aqueduct chamber when he chose to starve rather than feed — it was never the design. It was the damage done to the design. He was built to save. Someone broke the instructions.

[SFX: Ash's arm — muscle tensing, then releasing under Rune's hand] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Ash's arm tenses under Rune's hand, then deliberately releases. An audible exhale — letting it in]

ASH (barely audible): The hunger.

KAYA: The hunger is the corrupted drive. The original impulse — to absorb, to hold, to redistribute — was transformed into consumption. The god-eater was never meant to destroy what it takes in. It was meant to carry it. To hold the dying god's energy in trust until the Meridian could process the loss.

ASH: A vessel. Not a weapon.

KAYA: A vessel.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — Ash's frequency and Rune's converging] [under]
[DIRECTION: A held silence between them. The bond-hum carries what their voices don't need to]

RUNE (inner monologue): He looks at me. And through the bond I feel something I haven't felt from him before. Not the want from the kitchen. Not the terror from the aqueduct. Not the dissonant withdrawal from the summoner's call. Something fragile and enormous and new. Hope. Terrified, raw, newborn hope. The kind that doesn't know how to stand yet. The kind that reaches for the nearest solid thing and holds on.

[SFX: bond-hum — the frequencies realigning. Cleaner than the pre-summoner harmony, as though a static running since the pilot just cleared] [under]

KAYA: The summoner — whoever they are — they are using the corrupted version. The completion ritual would strip the remaining humanity and lock the god-eater into the weapon configuration permanently. The original design — the vessel, the caretaker — would be gone forever.

[SFX: Thessa speaking — her voice different. Not the cold pronouncement] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Thessa's voice gains momentum — she is thinking aloud, building to a conclusion she didn't expect to reach]

THESSA: The Vow of Smoke. It preserves the human core.

KAYA: Yes.

THESSA: And if the human core is preserved — the original design persists. The vessel function. The ability to hold rather than consume.

KAYA: Yes.

THESSA: Then the vow does not only save the anchor. It saves the god-eater. It restores the original purpose.

[Silence — 2500ms]
[DIRECTION: A collective beat of silence. Then Mika breaks it — her delivery is dry, practical, cutting through the weight]

MIKA (from the corner, where she has been listening while packing bandages): So. The magic vow that saves Rune also saves Ash also saves the whole system. And the bad guy's plan is the opposite of all of that.

MIKA: Just making sure I have the summary.

[SFX: a collective exhale — brief, involuntary, the release of people who are exhausted and overwhelmed and just heard the simplest version of a very complicated situation] [under]

{Scene 4 — Outside the safe house. Late night. The ending.}

[AMBIENT: street outside the safe house — late, quiet. The north quarter residential, mostly asleep. A dog somewhere. Streetlights flickering — the Meridian instability reaching the power grid. Cold air, stillness] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Rune and Ash — outside, leaning against the car. Standing, not sitting] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — the restored frequency. Not the pre-summoner harmony. Something new. The baseline that formed after the revelation: cleaner, deeper] [under]

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): The car is cold. The hood is cold. Ash leans against it and the metal warms in seconds because he runs hot, always, the thermal anomaly that made Mika squint at her thermometer on day one. The street is empty. Thessa's people are inside, rebuilding what they can. Kaya is still at the table, translating passages with the fervor of a man who has found the text that justifies thirty years of exile.

[SFX: Ash breathing — even. Genuinely calm] [under]
[DIRECTION: Not controlled. The breathing of someone who has put something down]

RUNE: You okay?

ASH: I don't know if "okay" covers it.

RUNE: Try.

ASH: I have spent — since the shrine. Since I opened my eyes and you were there and everything was wrong. I have spent every day knowing what I am. God-eater. The thing with the hunger. The weapon someone made. And every day I fought it — the feeding, the instinct, the pull — because I decided the human part was worth keeping. Even when it hurt. I fought it because I thought the god-eater was the disease and the human was the cure.

[SFX: Ash turning his head — the flickering streetlight casting his face in intervals of light and shadow] [spot]

ASH: And now Kaya tells me the god-eater was never the disease. It was the treatment. Someone just rewrote the prescription.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: Does that change things?

ASH: It changes everything.

[SFX: bond-hum — a pulse. The frequency that started on the rooftop when Rune said "that's ours" and deepened in the safe house when the word "vessel" entered the room] [under]

ASH: For the first time since I woke up, the shame is — quiet. The hunger is the same. It hasn't diminished. I can feel the north quarter god from here, the way you feel heat from a distance. But the shame of it — the belief that I was built wrong, that my nature is the error — that is different now.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): The shame is quiet. Four words. And I feel it through the bond — the absence of something that has been running beneath everything since the shrine. A static I got so used to I stopped hearing it. Gone. Not the hunger. Not the power. Not the shadows or the fire. Just the conviction that he was built for destruction. That's what's gone. And what's left is a being who was made to hold dying things gently and was broken into something that consumes them and is standing beside a paramedic in the cold and feeling, for the first time, like his nature and his choice might be pointing in the same direction.

[SFX: Rune's hand — finding Ash's on the car hood. Laying his hand over Ash's. Light, deliberate] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — the frequency steadying at its new depth. Two systems aligning] [under]
[DIRECTION: Quiet contact. No urgency. The bond-hum here should be warm and steady — the lowest-energy version of connection]

RUNE (inner monologue): The summoner said they gave you to me to soften you. To make you vulnerable. They said the feelings were the mechanism.

ASH: Yes.

RUNE (inner monologue): And here's what the summoner didn't account for. The feelings aren't just the mechanism — they're why the vow works. A god-eater who doesn't care can't speak the vow. It won't take. The old texts, the ones Kaya found — the vow requires a freely given choice. You can't freely choose to be human if you don't have something human to choose for.

RUNE: The summoner built the lock and the key. They just didn't realize the key opens a different door.

ASH: You are saying the thing the summoner used against us is also the thing that saves us.

RUNE: I'm saying the summoner built a weapon. We built something else.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: the city — late sounds. The dog again. A distant siren resolving into silence. The Meridian thrum low and steady beneath the pavement. The flickering streetlight steadying, then flickering again] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): His hand in mine on the cold car. Not the walk home from the church — that was discovery. Not the aqueduct reunion — that was relief. This is something else. His palm is warm and his grip is steady and the bond between us is running at a depth I don't have a map for yet. The summoner heard the old frequency. The incandescent one. The one that started in a kitchen and deepened in an embrace and almost broke on a rooftop. They think they know what the bond sounds like.

[SFX: Ash's hand — turning under Rune's. Palm up. Fingers closing around Rune's hand. Slow, unhurried] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Slow, unhurried hand contact. The SFX should carry it — palm turning, fingers closing. No rush]

RUNE (inner monologue): They don't know this one. The one that formed when a god-eater learned he was built to save things and a paramedic was standing close enough to feel it happen. This frequency is ours. No one designed it. No one predicted it. It just happened because we stood in a broken room and heard the truth and held on.

[SFX: the city continuing — distant, layered, the hum of infrastructure and night] [bed, fade-out]

RUNE (inner monologue): The summoner is out there. The Kindling are organized. Thessa needs us. Kaya has answers he hasn't finished translating. And the vow is waiting — the words that would lock his humanity in and the summoner's plan out and us together for whatever comes after forever.

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): We don't talk about the vow tonight. Not yet. But his hand is in mine and the shame is quiet and for the first time since a phone call broke the world open, I think: the summoner gave me a weapon. And the weapon grew a heartbeat. And the heartbeat chose me. And no amount of planning accounts for that.
