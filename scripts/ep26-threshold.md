# Episode 26: "Threshold"

**One job:** Kaya confirms the Vow of Smoke is the only option — it saves Rune AND blocks the summoner's completion ritual. Ash's rage isn't at the choice but at the universe for making it look like sacrifice when it's the only thing he's ever wanted. They decide: tomorrow, on the roof.
**The moment:** "Don't give me a noble exit." — Ash refusing Rune's offer of an alternative because the alternative means Rune keeps dying, and Ash will not allow that even if the vow costs him everything.

[SFX: a knock — the front door. Measured, deliberate. Kaya's knock. Three, then a pause, then one] [spot]

{Scene 2 — The apartment. Kaya's office hours.}

[AMBIENT: apartment — the war room configuration returning. Kaya's coat on the hook, his satchel on the table. The bruise above his left eye still dark. The lamp at its wrong angle. Maps and texts that have become furniture] [bed]

[SFX: Kaya setting up — his notebook open, three laminated pages from the false bottom laid flat. The rustle of ancient text translated in modern hand. Pen cap off] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — attentive. Both frequencies leaning forward] [under]

[DIRECTION: Kaya's voice is careful in a specific way — the academic composure holding, but thinner than before the kidnapping. The bruise is audible in how he pauses. He's choosing every word like a man who almost didn't get to say any]

KAYA: I have spent the last twenty-four hours with these texts. What Sable told you about the vow is accurate. I have confirmed it across three independent sources — the restricted archive, the pre-human codex fragments Thessa provided, and a passage from my own research that I had previously misinterpreted.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: The Vow of Smoke is real. And it is the only thing that can save Rune.

[SFX: bond-hum — a pulse. The sentence registering through both frequencies. Not surprise — confirmation of what they already knew. But hearing it from Kaya, with footnotes, makes it heavier] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The only thing. Not "one option among several." Not "a possible avenue worth exploring." The only thing. Kaya doesn't use absolutes. In five weeks I've heard him qualify, hedge, contextualize, and withhold. He has never said "the only thing" about anything. And the fact that he's saying it now, with the bruise from Selin's people still dark above his eye, with his voice stripped of every academic cushion — that's when I understand how close I am.

RUNE: Tell us what it does.

KAYA: You know the broad mechanism. The vow restructures the bond from parasitic to symbiotic. The drain stops. Energy flows both directions. The anchor stabilizes. The god-eater stabilizes. Neither depletes the other.

[SFX: Kaya — picking up one of the laminated pages. Holding it to the light] [spot]

KAYA: But there is more. The vow does not merely stabilize — it completes. It locks the god-eater's humanity in place. Permanently. The divine scaffolding remains. The power remains. But the mortal core becomes fixed. Immovable. The god-eater can never fully become divine again.

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: Ash — still. The shadows at his feet motionless. Listening with the attention of something ancient hearing its own sentence pronounced] [spot]

KAYA: You understand what this means for the summoner's plan.

ASH: The completion ritual requires a fully divine god-eater. A vessel with no mortal interference. If the vow locks the human part —

KAYA: Then the completion is impossible. The mechanism she designed — making you consume a god willingly, out of love — cannot override the vow's seal. The mortal core would refuse the transformation. You would absorb the power but remain what you are. Human-touched. Bounded. Yours.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum — a shift. Something opening in the frequency. Not relief — the breath before relief, where the body recognizes a door and hasn't decided whether to walk through it] [under]

ASH: So the vow saves Rune and stops the summoner.

[Beat — 800ms]

[DIRECTION: Kaya's voice drops. The academic cadence breaks. What comes out is stripped, honest, the voice of a man delivering a cost]

KAYA: It saves Rune and traps you. Forever human. Forever mortal. Forever bound.

[SFX: the apartment — the radiator clicking in the silence. The street noise through the glass. The world outside continuing its countdown while the room holds its breath] [under]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Traps. Kaya chose that word and I watch it land on Ash like a physical thing. Not "transforms." Not "completes." Traps. Forever human. Forever mortal. Forever bound. The door to godhood sealed with a word, and the key melted, and the man who was once an ancient weapon spending the rest of his life making bad coffee and reading medical textbooks and growing old in a body that used to be infinite. That's the trade. That's what the vow costs.

[SFX: Kaya — standing. Gathering his satchel. The sounds deliberate, giving them space] [spot]

KAYA: This is not a decision anyone else can make. The vow must be spoken freely, with full knowledge, by the god-eater. Coerced or desperate, the bond may not hold. Sable confirmed this.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: I will leave the texts. The pronunciation guide is annotated. The words are in the old language — Ash will know them. The god-eater's memory holds the sounds even if the mind does not.

[SFX: the door — Kaya leaving. The latch. His footsteps in the hall, slower than they used to be] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the apartment settling into the frequency Kaya's departure leaves behind. The shape of the choice he laid on the table] [under]

{Scene 3 — The apartment. After.}

[AMBIENT: apartment — quiet. Late morning light through the window. The laminated pages on the table catching the sun. The coffee mugs cold. The radiator clicking] [bed]

[SFX: Ash — at the window. One shoulder against the frame, jaw set. The shadows at his feet long and still] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — open. Raw. The managed frequency they've maintained since the aqueduct dropped like a guard they're both too tired to hold] [under]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's been at the window for twenty minutes. Through the bond I can feel the war — not the old one, the god-eater against the human, the hunger fighting the shame. This is new. This is a man standing at the edge of something permanent and feeling every door it closes at once. The power without limit. The thousand-year span of a divine being. The possibility that one day the hunger might win and he'd become something vast and terrible and free. All of it, sealed shut by a promise spoken on a rooftop to a paramedic whose hands shake.

[SFX: Rune standing — the chair pushing back. His footsteps crossing the apartment. Stopping short of the window. Not touching Ash. Standing beside him] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the proximity registering. Both frequencies aware, exposed] [under]

RUNE: You don't have to do this.

[SFX: Ash — a sound. Not a word. The sharp exhale of someone who has been waiting for a sentence and hating it in advance] [spot]

ASH: Don't.

RUNE: I mean it. There might be another —

[SFX: Ash turning from the window — the movement sudden. Shadows flaring at his feet, pulling tight. Temperature in the apartment rising two degrees in a breath] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice is not loud. It is low and precise and shaking. The formal register stripped away. Modern. Raw. Contractions present. This is Ash at the edge of control, and what comes out is not the god-eater — it's the man]

ASH: I said don't. Don't give me a noble exit. Don't tell me I have a choice when we both know I made it the night I stayed in your bed.

[SFX: bond-hum — trembling. The frequency vibrating at a pitch that is neither warm nor cold. Raw. Pressurized] [under]

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: Don't stand there and offer me an alternative as if the alternative is anything other than watching you die. Don't tell me there might be another way when every day I feel you getting thinner through the bond — the pulse fainter, the warmth less. Don't —

[SFX: Ash's voice — cracking. Not loudly. A fracture, not a break. He stops mid-sentence. Silence where the rest of the word should be] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum — raw. The frequencies carrying everything the stopped sentence contained. Rune doesn't need the words. The bond delivered them] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's not angry at me. He's angry at the universe for making this feel like a sacrifice when it's the only thing he's ever wanted. I can feel it through the bond — the fury aimed not at the vow or the cost or the loss of godhood but at the framing. At the fact that choosing to be human, choosing to stay, choosing me — it got dressed up as a last resort. As the thing you do when every other option fails. He's furious because he wanted this to be a beginning, not a rescue.

[SFX: Rune — reaching. His hand finding Ash's forearm. The contact. The bond flaring at the touch — the frequencies merging at the point of skin, warm and immediate] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the touch stabilizing something. Not fixing it. Holding it] [under]

RUNE: Okay.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: Okay. I hear you.

[SFX: Ash — his other hand coming up. Covering Rune's hand on his arm. The grip tight. The heat from his palm radiating through Rune's fingers] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice drops. Not the formal register. Something underneath it — private, cracked, honest]

ASH: I am not doing this to save you. I am doing this because the alternative is a thousand more years of being the thing they made me. And I would rather be mortal in your kitchen burning coffee than divine and alone in the dark.

[SFX: bond-hum — a long, sustained note. Not a declaration of love. A declaration of self] [under]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's shaking. His hand over mine, gripping too tight, the heat of him almost painful. And through the bond I feel the terror underneath the resolve — not of the vow itself but of the permanence. Of choosing once and living with it forever. He has never chosen anything before. He was made, sealed, awakened, bound. Every chapter of his existence happened to him. And now, for the first time in a thousand years, he gets to decide. And the decision terrifies him more than the hunger ever did.

[SFX: Rune — stepping closer. Not a kiss. Not an embrace. Forehead against Ash's shoulder. The contact minimal, total. The bond humming through every point where they touch] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — settling. Not resolved. Held. The frequency of a decision already made and not yet spoken — late morning light, the window, tomorrow waiting] [under]

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: Not tonight.

ASH: No.

RUNE: Something this permanent deserves more than desperation.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: It deserves intention.

[SFX: the city through the window — traffic, distant, a siren somewhere. The ordinary sound of a world three days from ending, not knowing it] [under]

[Silence — 1500ms]

Ash watches from the kitchen doorway and doesn't say a word. Just a man standing at a window in the dark, telling the person beside him that what happens tomorrow isn't fate or obligation or survival calculus. It's him. Choosing. For the first time in a thousand years.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't say anything. I don't need to. Through the bond he can feel every word I'm not saying and every one of them means the same thing.

[SFX: bond-hum — the final note. Both frequencies aligned. Tomorrow's roof waiting above them, the city counting down below] [bed, fade-out]

RUNE (inner monologue): Tomorrow. The roof. The sky. And the words that seal a god into a man and a man into a life and the door shuts and the key melts and whatever he was before — the thousand years, the hunger, the infinite dark — becomes the price he paid for a kitchen and bad coffee and me. Tomorrow. Three days to solstice. And tonight the bond hums between us like a held breath, and he stands at the window, and I stand beside him, and neither of us moves, and that's enough.
