## 9. SFX FORMATTING, LAYERING, AND BLOAT

### [CRITICAL] SFX tags vastly exceed the 20-word maximum

The 20-word-per-tag limit is violated by nearly every SFX tag in all three episodes. The five longest:

| Episode | Line | Word count |
|---------|------|------------|
| Ep37 | 142 (Ash receiving) | **145 words** |
| Ep38 | 264 (bond-harmonic last note) | **143 words** |
| Ep37 | 208 (bond new frequency) | **98 words** |
| Ep36 | 246 (bond collision) | **93 words** |
| Ep37 | 144 (merged harmonic) | **89 words** |

These are not sound design directions. They are prose poems embedded in SFX brackets. A sound designer cannot execute "The love that built itself from obligation to habit to choice to certainty" (ep37 line 142). These passages function as narrative text -- internal monologue or authorial commentary wearing an SFX tag as a disguise.

**Recommendation:** Every SFX tag over 20 words must be split. The actable sound cue (what the designer builds) stays in the SFX tag. The narrative/emotional content either becomes inner monologue, a Beat notation with a brief emotional descriptor, or is cut. Example fix for ep37 line 142:

Before (145 words in SFX):
> [SFX: Ash -- receiving. Through the bond: the god-eater feeling a mortal man's accumulated grief. Every patient Rune lost...]

After:
> [SFX: Ash -- a low sound. The bond-harmonic shifting, absorbing, the frequency broadening] [under]
> RUNE (inner monologue): He feels it. All of it. Every patient I lost...

This preserves the content while making the SFX tag producible.

### [MAJOR] SFX density: 79 [spot]-tagged SFX cues across three episodes
- Ep36: 27
- Ep37: 25
- Ep38: 27

At roughly 25-minute episodes, this averages one spot SFX cue every 55-60 seconds. Combined with AMBIENT beds, [under] layers, Beats, and Silences, the sound design load is extremely dense. The ear needs air. Suggest identifying 3-4 SFX tags per episode that can be folded into adjacent cues or cut entirely. Priority cuts: SFX tags that describe visual information (facial expressions, looks, nods) that cannot be heard.

## 10. NON-ACTABLE DIRECTION TAGS

### [MAJOR] SFX tags containing emotional/psychological narration
Distinct from bloat, this is a directability problem. A sound designer reads these tags and asks: "What do I build?" Examples:

- Ep36 line 26: "The sound of a man looking at a page full of crossed-out equations and being honest about what's left" -- this is not a sound. It is a character description.
- Ep36 line 268: "Something brighter. Smaller. Hope. A divine being hoping" -- how does a designer make "hope" audible?
- Ep37 line 156: "Not damage. Expansion. The body's circuitry forced wider than it was built to carry." -- metaphysical description, not a sound cue.
- Ep38 line 136: "the unnameable quality that changes when a truth lands" -- explicitly unnameable, therefore unbuildable.

**Recommendation:** Each SFX tag should answer: "What sound does the designer layer, and at what level?" If the answer is "no specific sound," the content belongs in monologue or a director's note outside brackets.

## 16. EP38 TONE: GENUINE EXHALE VS. SENTIMENTAL

### [MODERATE] The final SFX tag (line 264) risks sentimentality

The 143-word bond-harmonic tag at ep38 line 264 catalogs: "The star charts on the ceiling. The plant on the windowsill. The two mugs in the sink." This is a list of cozy domestic details presented as sound design instruction. It reads as an author savoring the ending rather than a sound cue. The accumulation tips from "earned warmth" toward "sentimental inventory." The same issue appears in the closing inner monologue (lines 266-270): "The name I say when I wake up and the name I hear through the bond before I sleep and the name that means: I chose this." The repetition of "the name" three times in one sentence is a construction the series uses well elsewhere but here, at the very end, it risks feeling self-consciously poetic.

**Recommendation:** Trim the final SFX tag to the actable core: the bond-harmonic at its gentlest, the Meridian steady, the city outside. Let the inner monologue carry the emotion. And consider ending Rune's final inner monologue on "It's enough." -- the two-word echo of Ash's line. The current version adds "The bond hums. The city sleeps. The ground holds." in the SFX tag after the inner monologue ends, which is a strong three-beat closer. Keep that in the AMBIENT fade-out. Cut the inventory.
