# Episode 22: "Schism"

**One job:** The safe house attack reveals the scale of the summoner's operation — followers called the Kindling. Thessa's certainty fractures into need. And Kaya discovers the god-eater's original purpose was preservation, not destruction — reframing everything Ash is and everything they've been fighting.
**The moment:** "I was made to save gods. Not eat them." — The sentence that gives Ash a new origin and cracks a thousand years of shame.

{Scene 3 — The safe house. Later. Kaya and the old texts.}

[SFX: Thessa's silence — the kind that means she is hearing something she does not want to hear and cannot argue with] [under]

[SFX: Kaya — looking up from the texts. His reading glasses pushed up, his fingers on a passage he's been circling with his pen. The particular focus of an academic who has just had a thirty-year question answered] [spot]

[Silence — 2500ms]

KAYA: The earliest texts describe the god-eater as a vessel. A living repository. When a god died — naturally, from the fading of its sacred site, from the natural end of its lifecycle — the energy had to be managed. If it dissipated violently, it destabilized the Meridian. The god-eater was created to absorb that energy and hold it. Safely. Until it could be redistributed through the network.

[SFX: bond-hum — something happening. A resonance beneath the regular frequency. Ash's frequency doing something it has never done — deepening, broadening, as if responding to information that reaches below conscious thought into the architecture of what he is] [under]

KAYA: The god-eater was not a weapon. It was a — the nearest translation is caretaker. A spiritual shock absorber. A being designed to catch what falls so the system does not break.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: The god-eater was always meant to save the system. Not destroy it.

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Save. Not destroy. The word lands in the room and I watch it hit Ash the way I've watched medications hit patients in crisis — the delay, the silence while it travels from the ear to the place where it matters, and then the change. The visible, physical change.

[SFX: Ash's hand lifting from the map — slowly. His palm open. Looking at his own hand as though the ritual scars written across it have just been reread in a different language] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): He's looking at his scars. The binding marks that Kaya identified in week one — the marks that said "weapon," that said "leash," that said "god-eater." The same marks. But the word for what they mean just changed.

KAYA: The corruption came later. The god-keepers — our predecessors, in the early organization — they saw the god-eater's power and repurposed the ritual. The vessel became a weapon. The caretaker became a consumer. The original intention was buried and the modified version — the one that strips humanity, that creates the hunger as a feature rather than a flaw — became the standard rite.

THESSA (quietly): We perverted it.

KAYA: The order perverted it. Centuries ago. Before your time. Before mine. But yes. The god-keepers took a being made to save gods and turned it into a being that devours them. The hunger is not the design. It is the damage.

[SFX: Ash — standing. The chair scraping back. Not violent. The movement of someone who needs to be on their feet for what he's feeling] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the deeper resonance building. The frequency Rune has never heard before. Not agitation. Not the dissonance from the summoner's call. Something tectonic. The sound of a foundation being replaced beneath a standing structure] [under]

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: I was made to save gods. Not eat them.

[SFX: his voice — strange. Not the hostility of the shrine. Not the vulnerability of the kitchen. Not the controlled fury of the rooftop. Something Rune hasn't heard. Something that might be the sound a being makes when it learns it was not built for the thing it fears most about itself] [spot]

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Rune — standing. Crossing to Ash. His hand on Ash's arm. Not the wrist grip of the aqueduct, not the jaw-touch of the bathroom. The forearm. The simple, deliberate contact of one person grounding another] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — the deep resonance meeting Rune's frequency. The dissonance from the summoner's call finally breaking apart. Not because the argument is resolved. Because the ground beneath the argument has shifted. The god-eater is not what either of them thought. The shame that has been running beneath Ash since the shrine — the shame of what he is, the hunger, the nature — just lost its foundation] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): My hand on his arm. The bond carrying what my mouth can't say fast enough — that the hunger he's hated, the nature he's fought, the thing he refused to be in the aqueduct chamber when he chose to starve rather than feed — it was never the design. It was the damage done to the design. He was built to save. Someone broke the instructions.

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: Ash's arm — the muscle tensing and then releasing under Rune's hand. The physical surrender of someone letting new information reach the body, not just the mind] [spot]

KAYA: The hunger is the corrupted drive. The original impulse — to absorb, to hold, to redistribute — was transformed into consumption. The god-eater was never meant to destroy what it takes in. ASH: A vessel. Not a weapon.

KAYA: A vessel.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Ash looking at Rune — his arm still under Rune's hand. The eye contact that carries through the bond. The look of someone who has been told the thing they feared most about themselves was a lie, and the truth was something they could bear] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): He looks at me. And through the bond I feel something I haven't felt from him before. Not the want from the kitchen. Not the terror from the aqueduct. Not the dissonant withdrawal from the summoner's call. Something fragile and enormous and new. Hope. Terrified, raw, newborn hope. The kind that doesn't know how to stand yet. The kind that reaches for the nearest solid thing and holds on.

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — the frequencies realigning. Not the settled harmony of the aqueduct aftermath. Something different. Cleaner. As though a static that has been running since the pilot just cleared and the signal underneath is stronger than either of them knew] [under]

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: Thessa — who has been silent since her two-word admission. Her voice, when it comes, is different. Not the cold pronouncement. The voice of a woman rebuilding her convictions in real time] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: the room — the silence of five people and a handful of battered god-keepers processing a revelation that rewrites a thousand years of doctrine] [under]

[Silence — 2500ms]

MIKA (from the corner, where she has been listening while packing bandages): So. The magic vow that saves Rune also saves Ash also saves the whole system. And the bad guy's plan is the opposite of all of that.

[Beat — 400ms]

MIKA: Just making sure I have the summary.

[SFX: the almost-laugh — not from Ash this time. From the room. The collective exhale of people who are exhausted and overwhelmed and just heard the simplest possible version of a very complicated situation from the one person in the room who refuses to make anything more complicated than it needs to be] [under]

[SFX: Ash turning his head — looking at Rune beside him. The city behind them, the flickering streetlight casting his face in intervals of light and shadow] [spot]

[SFX: bond-hum — warm. The frequency steadying at its new depth. Two systems aligning around a revised understanding. Not the giddy incandescent of the first kiss. Not the desperate hold of the embrace. Something quieter and older and more certain] [under]
