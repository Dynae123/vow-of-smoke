# Episode 36: "The Third Way"

**One job:** Kaya finds the workaround. The vow's symbiotic principle applied to the siphon. The cost: Ash and Rune become permanent conduits. Both say yes without hesitating.
**The moment:** Kaya asks "I'm asking if you BOTH do." No pause. "Yes." / "Was there ever any doubt?"

---

{Scene 1 — Beneath the eastern shrine. Night. Day two, hours after the rooftop. Kaya's lab.}

[AMBIENT: the laboratory — deep night. The siphon's cycling whine constant, one frequency louder than last episode. Fewer people. The god-keeper researchers sent home. What remains: Kaya at the table, surrounded by texts and diagrams, working. The scratch of pen on paper. The hum of monitoring equipment. And underneath: the meridian-thrum, thinner than yesterday. The city's pulse losing pressure] [bed]

[SFX: Kaya — writing. Crossing out. Writing again. Pages turning. Books open in stacks around him. Old bindings cracking. His breathing audible. The measured cadence gone. What's left is the sound of a man doing mathematics against a deadline and losing] [spot]

[SFX: Sable — arriving. Not through the door. Through the air. The temperature dropping two degrees. The lights flickering once. The echo of a god entering a room carved from stone, their presence settling like snowfall] [spot]

KAYA: Sable. I did not expect —

SABLE: You have been here for nine hours. The Meridian carries your exhaustion. Even the dormant gods can feel the man who is trying to save them failing.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: I am not failing. I am eliminating options.

SABLE: How many remain.

[SFX: Kaya — the pen stopping. The sound of a man looking at a page full of crossed-out equations and being honest about what's left] [spot]

KAYA: None. The siphon's integration with the Meridian is total. Selin used the network's own architecture as the device's skeleton. Removing it is removing the skeleton. Every approach I've attempted — isolating the siphon, redirecting the drain to a dead node, overloading the mechanism — all roads lead to the same collapse.

[Silence — 1500ms]

SABLE: You are thinking of the siphon as a disease. Something to cut away.

KAYA: What else would it be.

[SFX: Sable — moving closer. Their presence near the table. The diagrams ruffling faintly as though a draft passed through, though the underground chamber has no wind] [spot]

SABLE: A wound can be fatal. Or it can become a mouth. The body does not reject a transplant that speaks its language.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: I don't follow.

SABLE: The siphon drains because it has nowhere to send what it takes. A one-way channel. The energy leaves the Meridian and has no path back. But the Vow of Smoke — what does the vow do?

[SFX: Kaya — the stillness. The pen rolling off the table and hitting the stone floor. He doesn't pick it up. The academic recalculating. The obsessive mind that has been staring at the wrong wall for nine hours turning toward the right one] [spot]

KAYA: The vow closes a circuit. It turns a parasitic bond into a symbiotic one. Energy flows both ways. The drain becomes an exchange.

SABLE: And if the same principle were applied not to the bond between two people, but to the bond between a device and a network?

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Kaya — standing. The chair scraping stone. His hands on the diagrams. Pulling one page out from under the pile. The siphon's schematic. His breathing changing. The academic on the edge of something] [spot]

KAYA: Close the circuit. Redirect the drain through the bond — through Ash and Rune — and back into the Meridian. The siphon draws energy out. The bond feeds it back. The loop sustains itself. The siphon becomes part of the system instead of a parasite on it.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: But the power. The volume of energy flowing through them continuously. Forever. They would become living conduits for the entire Meridian network. Anchored not just to each other but to every sacred site in the city.

SABLE: Yes.

KAYA: The bond would be permanent. Beyond the vow. Beyond anything I have encountered in the texts.

SABLE: Yes.

[SFX: Kaya — reaching for his phone. His hand shaking. Not from fear. From the particular tremor of a man who has spent nine hours in the dark and found a door and does not know what's behind it] [spot]

KAYA: I need to verify the mathematics. If the siphon's extraction rate exceeds the bond's capacity to redirect —

SABLE: The mathematics will hold. The bond was built to carry divine power. The vow made it efficient. This merely expands the load.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: You are certain.

SABLE: Certainty is a mortal habit. But the pattern is sound. The Meridian was built on symbiosis. The siphon broke that principle. Restoring it — through two people who have already demonstrated the principle works — is not invention. It is return.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Kaya — sitting back down. Pulling fresh paper. The pen retrieved from the floor. Writing. Faster now. The scratch of equations being born instead of dying. The monitoring equipment beeping steadily. The siphon cycling. And underneath: the meridian-thrum, weak but waiting] [spot]

{Scene 2 — Rune's apartment. Pre-dawn. Day three.}

[AMBIENT: apartment — the dark hours before morning. Radiator ticking. Street empty below. The quality of silence that only exists between 4 and 5 AM, when even the city sleeps. No alarm. No dispatch. Just the apartment and the two people in it who haven't slept] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — low. Strained. Two frequencies running parallel but not touching. The residue of the rooftop argument. Not broken. Guarded. The sound of two people in the same bed who can feel everything the other feels and are being very careful about what they let through] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Four AM. He's awake. I'm awake. We've been not-sleeping for three hours. His back to me. My back to him. Fourteen inches of mattress between us that might as well be the Meridian itself. I can feel him through the bond — his breathing, his temperature, the steady thrum of his heartbeat. And underneath that: the calm. Still there. The terrible, resolved calm of a man who has decided to die and is waiting for me to accept it.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I won't.

[SFX: Rune's phone — buzzing on the nightstand. The screen lighting up the ceiling. Kaya's pattern: two short, one long] [spot]

[SFX: Rune — reaching for it. The shift in the mattress. Ash turning. Both of them moving toward the phone because at 4 AM on the last day, a call from Kaya is either the worst news or the only good kind] [spot]

RUNE: Kaya.

KAYA: Come to the shrine. Both of you. I have something.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: Something good or something terrible.

KAYA: I am not sure those are different categories anymore. Come now.

[SFX: the call ending. The phone screen dimming. The apartment dark again. Two people in a bed, facing each other now, the fourteen inches closed to nothing] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

ASH: He found something.

RUNE: Yeah.

ASH: Rune. If it's another version of the same three options —

RUNE: Then we deal with it. Together. Not you deciding alone on a rooftop.

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: Ash — the breath. Not a sigh. Not agreement. The exhale of a man who is being asked to hope and doesn't know if he's allowed. Through the bond: the calm cracking. Underneath it, the thing he's been holding down for two days. Fear. Not of dying. Of wanting to live and being wrong to want it] [under]

ASH: Together.

[SFX: movement — two people getting out of bed. Feet on cold floor. Clothes in the dark. The choreography of urgency shared between people who know each other's rhythms. No lights turned on. They don't need them] [spot]

{Scene 3 — Beneath the eastern shrine. Day three. Dawn.}

[AMBIENT: the laboratory — transformed. Kaya's work spread across every surface. Diagrams on the walls. Equations circling the siphon schematic like annotations on a map. Sable in the corner, their presence a faint glow. Thessa standing, arms crossed, reading a page Kaya handed her. The monitoring equipment beeping. The siphon cycling — louder now. The third day] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Rune and Ash — arriving. Down the stairs. Into the room. The acoustic change as they enter: warmer, because Ash runs hot; tighter, because the siphon reacts to the god-eater's proximity with a pitch shift upward] [spot]

[SFX: Mika — already there. Coffee in hand. Two more on the table. She called in sick. The paramedic who decided that the world might need saving more than her shift needed covering. Her voice carrying the particular tension of someone who has been reading Kaya's notes for an hour and understood about sixty percent and the sixty percent was enough] [spot]

MIKA: Coffee. Sit. He's been up all night and he's going to explain it whether you're caffeinated or not.

[SFX: Rune — taking the coffee. His hand brushing Mika's. The contact brief but loaded. His sister. His grounding wire. Present] [spot]

RUNE: Thanks.

MIKA: Don't thank me, thank Sable. They found him face-down in a manuscript at 2 AM and apparently gods can make coffee.

SABLE: The water remembers how to be hot. The rest is measurement.

[SFX: Mika — the look. The one she gives statements that are either deeply profound or deeply strange and she can't tell which. She moves on. She always moves on] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: Kaya — at the central table. Standing. His sleeves rolled. Ink on his hands. His voice carrying the strained energy of a man who has not slept but has found something and the finding is holding him up where the caffeine can't] [spot]

KAYA: I have been approaching the siphon as a foreign body. Something to extract. Every approach failed because the siphon is not foreign — Selin built it from the Meridian's own architecture. Removing it is removing the infrastructure.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: Sable helped me see the alternative. The siphon drains because it is a one-way channel. Energy leaves the Meridian with no return path. But we have already proven that a one-way channel can be converted. The Vow of Smoke did exactly this — it closed the circuit between Ash and Rune, turning a parasitic drain into a symbiotic exchange.

[SFX: Ash — the stillness. The god-eater hearing the principle described. Through the bond: recognition. Something clicking into alignment] [spot]

ASH: Apply the vow's principle to the siphon.

KAYA: Yes. Close the circuit. Redirect the siphon's drain through the bond — through both of you — and back into the Meridian. The energy the siphon extracts flows through your shared circuit and returns to the network. The drain becomes a loop. The siphon stops being a parasite and becomes a circulation pump.

[Silence — 1500ms]

THESSA: The power involved. A god-eater absorbing the siphon's function directly would die from the volume. How is this different.

KAYA: Because the god-eater would not absorb it. He would channel it. The bond distributes the load between two people. And the return path sends the energy back into the network — they do not carry it permanently. They redirect it. The difference between holding a river and being the riverbed.

[Beat — 800ms]

THESSA: And the cost.

[SFX: Kaya — the pause. The professor choosing his words. His voice dropping into the register that means the answer is large and permanent] [spot]

KAYA: The power would flow through both of them. Continuously. The bond becomes the Meridian's central conduit. They would be connected to every sacred site in the city. They would feel the gods. The gods would feel them. The new nodes, the dormant sites, the seal on the First Root — all of it, running through their shared circuit.

[Beat — 400ms]

KAYA: And the bond between them would be permanent. Not the vow's permanence — the vow can theoretically be broken. This would be structural. Woven into the Meridian itself. The bond would be the network's architecture. Dissolving it would collapse the system.

MIKA: So they'd be — what. Plugged into the city. Forever.

KAYA: Living anchors. For the Meridian. For each other. For everything the network sustains.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Mika — setting down the coffee. The ceramic on wood. The sound of a woman processing the idea that her brother might become a permanent part of the city's divine infrastructure] [spot]

MIKA: Is it safe. For Rune.

KAYA: The bond is already stable. The vow ensured that. Expanding its function does not change the fundamental exchange — it increases the volume. Rune's bloodline sensitivity should be sufficient to handle the load. But I cannot guarantee there is no pain. The initial connection — opening the circuit to include the siphon and the full network — will be immense.

MIKA: That's not what I asked. Is my brother going to be okay.

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: I believe so. Yes.

[SFX: Mika — the nod. The exhale. The sound of a woman who needed to hear that specific sentence before she could let anything else in] [spot]

[SFX: Thessa — unfolding her arms. The Keeper processing. Her voice quiet. The pronouncement register replaced by something rarer: genuine assessment] [spot]

THESSA: The Kindling have dissolved. Selin's capture broke the coordination. Some surrendered. Some disappeared. The ideology collapsed when its founder was proven wrong. Movements built on one person's conviction do not survive that person's failure.

[Beat — 400ms]

THESSA: The external threat is ended. What remains is the siphon and the choice it demands.

[SFX: bond-harmonic — shifting. The strained parallel frequencies of the last two days beginning to move. Not toward each other yet. But toward the same point. The circuit, responding to the idea of what it might become] [under]

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I listen to Kaya's mathematics and Thessa's report and Mika's questions and I watch Ash standing across the table with ink on his hands from touching Kaya's diagrams and I do the math that matters. Not the divine engineering. The human engineering. The load-bearing calculation.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): He wouldn't die. That's the first number. The bond holds him. The bond holds me. The circuit redirects what would kill him alone and distributes it between us and sends it home. He lives. I live. The cost is that the bond becomes the city's infrastructure and the city becomes our bond and we carry every sacred site in the grid in our shared nervous system forever.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Forever. Bound to every god. Bound to the seal. Bound to the earth underneath the concrete. And bound to him. Permanently. Beyond the vow. Beyond choice. The kind of permanent where breaking it breaks the city.

[SFX: Rune — looking at Ash. The room full of people and the look private. Through the bond: the question arriving before the words form. Not will you. Are you sure. Because Rune already knows the answer. He knew it on the rooftop when Ash offered to die and Rune said no. He knew it in the wildflowers. He knew it the night he knelt in the ashes and the lock turned] [spot]

RUNE: You're asking if I want to be bound to him forever.

KAYA: I am asking if you both do. The circuit requires two anchors. It must be chosen freely by both. If either of you has reservations —

[SFX: the sentence — stopped. By the silence. By the look between Ash and Rune that the room can feel even though it has no sound. Sable watching. Thessa watching. Mika watching. Kaya, mid-sentence, watching. The laboratory and the siphon and the countdown and the Meridian waiting for two people to answer a question they answered months ago] [spot]

ASH: Yes.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: Was there ever any doubt?

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — the two frequencies that have been running parallel for two days colliding. Not gently. The sound of two signals finding each other and locking and the lock holding and the circuit — the old circuit, the vow's circuit — humming at a frequency the audience has never heard. Not louder. Clearer. The sound of a decision made by two people at the same time without discussion because the discussion happened in the ashes and in the undercrypt and on the rooftop and in the wildflowers and in every moment between] [under]

[SFX: Mika — the exhale. Quiet. Not relief. Something closer to recognition. The sound of a sister who has watched her brother choose this man over and over and is watching him do it one more time] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

KAYA: Then we begin preparations. The ritual must be conducted at the heart of the Meridian — the cathedral undercrypt. Where the network is strongest and the seal is deepest. I will need time to prepare the ritual sequence.

THESSA: The god-keepers will anchor the sacred sites. I will coordinate the network from the cathedral.

MIKA: And I'll be there. Monitoring Rune.

RUNE: Mika, you don't have to —

MIKA: I'll be there. Monitoring Rune. That wasn't a question.

[SFX: Rune — the breath that is half a laugh. The sound of a man who should know by now that his sister does not ask permission] [spot]

[Beat — 400ms]

SABLE: The sleeping gods will feel the shift when the circuit opens. They will lend what strength they have. Even the dormant ones. Even the new one in the north quarter.

[SFX: Sable — their voice carrying something the audience hasn't heard from them before. Not the gentle patience of a god who has been dying for six hundred years. Something brighter. Smaller. Hope. A divine being hoping] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: the siphon — cycling. Still running. Still counting down. But the room has changed. The desperation is gone. What's left is work to do and people to do it and a door that opened in the dark because a god and a professor sat in a cave all night and remembered that the answer was already there, built into the bond, built into the vow, built into the principle that started everything: you don't save a system by cutting it apart. You save it by closing the circuit] [bed]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — steady. Warm. Two frequencies aligned and humming. The sound of two people who said yes and meant it and have twelve hours to turn the yes into something the earth can hold] [under, fade-out]
