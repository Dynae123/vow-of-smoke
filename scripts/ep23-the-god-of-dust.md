# Episode 23: "The God of Dust"

**One job:** Sable appears — a dying god in human form — and offers Rune a choice: accept Sable's remaining power to slow the bond's drain, at the cost of Sable's final death. Rune refuses. Ash offers to take it himself. Sable refuses both and instead gives them something more valuable: the knowledge that the summoner is someone close, and a warning that the Meridian is holding back something worse than dead gods.
**The moment:** "Yes." — Ash's unhesitant offer to absorb more divine power and risk losing himself, to keep Rune alive. No calculation. No pause. Just the word.

[SFX: their footsteps crossing the lot — weeds brushing boots, concrete fragments crunching. The acoustic opens up as they move past the fence into the empty space] [bridge]

RUNE (inner monologue): The lot doesn't look sacred. It looks like what it is — a scar where a building used to stand. ASH: The god of forgotten places. Memory gods are the most fragile. They exist on accumulated human presence — centuries of people reading, studying, gathering. When the building came down, the presence thinned.

[SFX: a sound — not the meridian-thrum. Something softer. Dust settling in a pattern. Pages turning in an empty room. The impression of a library, remembered by the ground] [under]

[DIRECTION: The sound is subliminal. Not the vast cathedral acoustic of the aqueduct god. Smaller. Intimate. A god the size of a room, not a river]

Shelves. Paper. The particular quiet of people reading in the same room. The god of this place sounds like a library closing for the last time.

[DIRECTION: Sable's delivery is patient, gentle, unhurried. A being for whom centuries are a unit of measure. The thinness is real — this god is fading — but the authority is still present. Every sentence lands complete]

ASH (low, to Rune): A god. In human form.

SABLE: The human form costs me. But there are things that must be said with a mouth and heard with ears. The dust cannot do this work.

[SFX: bond-hum — a pulse. Ash's frequency. The sentence registering through the bond before Rune fully processes it] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Dying. I know this. Kaya told me. Ash told me. My own body tells me every time my hands shake or my nose bleeds into a tissue I crumple before anyone sees. But hearing it from a god — a being who has been watching people die for six centuries and can tell the difference between sick and terminal — hearing it from Sable lands different. Heavier.

SABLE: The bond draws from you. Steadily. The recent shifts — the deepening, the reciprocal frequency — have slowed the drain. But they have not stopped it. The anchor's body was not built to sustain a god-eater indefinitely. Not without the vow.

[SFX: Ash — stepping forward. The shadows sharpening at his feet] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice carries an edge — not hostile, protective. The formal register]

ASH: You know about the vow.

SABLE: The Vow of Smoke is older than the god-keepers. Older than the corruption of the ritual. The vow was part of the original design — the mechanism that made the bond sustainable. That it was forgotten is a tragedy. That it still exists is a mercy.

[Beat — 400ms]

SABLE: But the vow takes time to prepare. And the anchor does not have time.

[AMBIENT: same — the quiet holding. Sable's presence creating a pocket of stillness in the city noise] [bed]

[SFX: bond-hum — attentive. Both frequencies aligned, listening] [under]

SABLE: There is something left in me. Not much. Six centuries of accumulated presence — the memories of everyone who read in this space, who studied, who fell asleep in the stacks. It is enough to strengthen the anchor bond. To buy the time the vow needs.

[SFX: Sable's form flickering — the edges blurring, then refocusing. The cost of manifestation visible in the instability] [spot]

SABLE: The cost is simple. What remains of me. The transfer would complete what nine years of fading started. Not dormancy. Extinction. No trace in the Meridian. No possibility of return.

RUNE: I'm a paramedic. I save people. I don't sacrifice them.

[SFX: Sable — a sound. Not a laugh. Something older. The acoustic equivalent of dust settling on a smile] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: Ash — flat, immediate. No deliberation in the delivery] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash delivers this line without pause. Zero hesitation. The word arrives like a reflex]

ASH: No.

[SFX: temperature shift — Ash's fire response banking into something controlled and immovable] [spot]

ASH: You're not dying for him. Nobody else dies for him.

SABLE: That is not your choice, god-eater.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir — the shadows at his feet reaching toward Sable, then pulling back. Not aggressive. Instinctive] [under]

ASH: Then make it mine. Give the power to me. I'll hold it. I was built for it.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — a spike. Rune's frequency reacting to the sentence. The bond translating what Rune's mouth hasn't caught up to] [under]

Risk losing himself to the hunger that nearly ate him alive in the aqueduct. Risk the divine overwhelming the human. Risk everything. For me.

[SFX: Ash — no movement. No shadow response. Still as stone] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's "Yes" is quiet, certain, delivered like a fact about gravity]

ASH: Yes.

[SFX: bond-hum — something breaking open. Not breaking apart. The frequency of a word that means more than its single syllable. Rune's system registering the weight through the bond] [under]

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): Yes. One word. No qualifier. No condition. No calculation about what it costs or what the summoner might do with a more powerful god-eater or what Kaya would say. Just yes. The word a person says when they've already decided and the question is a formality. He would take on more of the thing he hates most about himself — the hunger, the power, the pull toward divinity — to buy me time. And the fact that he said it without pausing is the part that takes my knees out.

[SFX: Sable studying Ash — the dust orbiting them slowing, the patterns becoming deliberate. Reading something] [under]

SABLE: Ah. So that is how it is.

[Beat — 400ms]

SABLE: The vessel who chose to be human. And the human who holds the vessel's chain. And neither will let the other pay the price.

{Scene 4 — The lot. What Sable knows.}

[AMBIENT: same — but the quality shifts. Sable's presence strengthening as though the decision to stay a little longer sharpened the god's focus] [bed]

[SFX: Sable — moving to the edge of the foundation slab. Sitting. The gesture unexpectedly human — a god sitting on a ruin like a student on a bench] [spot]

SABLE: No power is given today. Not to either of you.

[SFX: Rune exhaling — the relief physical. Ash beside him, the shadows at his feet easing] [under]

SABLE: But there is something else you need. Something the old texts do not contain and the god-keepers have forgotten and the one who summoned you has been counting on no one remembering.

[SFX: bond-hum — attentive. Both frequencies leaning toward the god] [under]

SABLE: The Meridian is not just a network. It is not just a web of divine energy connecting the nodes. It is a cage.

[Beat — 800ms]

[DIRECTION: Sable's voice drops — not in volume but in register. The layered echo thins to a single strand. A god being precise about something dangerous]

SABLE: Beneath the city. Far below the foundations, below the sacred sites, below the bedrock itself. Something sleeps. The gods do not speak of it. We feel it. A weight. A pressure from below. The oldest among us remember when it stirred — before the Meridian was whole. Before the cage was locked.

[SFX: meridian-thrum — faint, rising from below the lot. Triggered by Sable's words as though the network itself is responding to the mention of its deepest function. Unstable, wavering] [under]

RUNE: What is it?

SABLE: The first texts called it the First Root. Not a god. Something that predates the gods. The raw, undifferentiated potential from which we eventually crystallized. Vast. Mindless. Hungry. The Meridian's weight keeps it dormant.

[SFX: ash-shadows-stir — deep, involuntary. Ash's body responding to the word before his mind does] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Ash's voice when he responds carries the archaic undertow — something in him recognizing what Sable describes]

ASH: I can feel it. I've felt it since the aqueduct. Something beneath the gods. Deeper. Like standing over a pit with no bottom.

[Silence — 1500ms]

SABLE: Every god that dies weakens the cage. The summoner's kill pattern is not merely destroying gods. It is unlocking the Meridian. And if enough nodes fail —

[SFX: meridian-thrum — the wavering frequency dropping, becoming irregular. The sound of a system under strain] [under]

RUNE: The cage opens.

SABLE: The cage opens. And what wakes will not care about summoners or god-eaters or vows. It will simply consume. Everything. The way roots consume the soil they grow through. Without malice. Without thought. Because that is its nature.

[SFX: bond-hum — tight, taut. The information settling into both of them through the bond] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The First Root. Something older than gods, sleeping under the city, held down by the Meridian. And the summoner — killing gods one by one — is pulling the cage apart from the inside. Not just destroying divine infrastructure. Unlocking something that makes a god-eater look like a house cat.

[Beat — 800ms]

RUNE: Does the summoner know? About the First Root?

SABLE: That is the question, is it not. Whether the one who tears open a cage knows what lives inside it. Or whether they only see the bars and think: these should not be here.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: Sable — their form flickering again. More pronounced. The edges dissolving, then reforming with effort] [spot]

SABLE: One more thing.

[SFX: Sable's eyes — a shift in the dust. The ancient weight behind the young face sharpening into focus] [spot]

SABLE: Not yet. There are rules older than names. Even dying, the laws that govern what a god may reveal still hold. But this: the summoner is close. Not distant. Not hidden across the city in a lair. Close to you. To the people you trust.

[SFX: bond-hum — a dissonant flicker. Brief. The residual echo of the summoner's phone call from ep21] [under]

RUNE: How close.

SABLE: Close enough that the betrayal, when it comes, will not feel like a surprise. It will feel like something you already knew and refused to see.

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Sable standing — the form thinning. Translucent at the edges. The dust beginning to disperse] [spot]

SABLE: You have a week. Perhaps less. The summoner moves faster now. The next god targeted — not the cathedral. That comes last. Something between. Something they need to clear the path.

[SFX: Sable's form — beginning to dissolve. Not dying. Withdrawing. The presence condensing back toward the crack in the foundation, the vine, the last trace of sacred ground] [spot]

[DIRECTION: Sable's final lines are thin but clear. Not weak — precise. A god conserving what remains]

SABLE: Speak the vow before it is too late. And when the cage breaks — because it will break, whether the summoner succeeds or fails, the damage is already too great — you will need the god-eater whole. Human and divine. Both parts. The original design.

[SFX: Sable's voice — fading. The reverb thinning to a single echo] [spot]

SABLE: The vessel who chooses. That is the only thing that can hold what stirs below.

[SFX: dust — settling. Slowly. The lot returning to its ordinary abandoned quiet. The presence withdrawing into the crack, into the vine, into the ground] [bridge]

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — the two of them standing in the empty lot. The god gone. The weight of what was said still in the air] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): Close. Close to the people we trust. Kaya, who has always held something back. Thessa, whose order is fractured from within. Mika, who doesn't know about the vow. RUNE (inner monologue): A week. Maybe less. The summoner moving faster. The First Root stirring beneath the Meridian like something turning over in its sleep. Sable dying by inches in a demolished library. And Ash said yes. One word, no hesitation, when a god asked if he'd risk losing himself to keep me alive. Yes. Like it wasn't even a question.

It's just a man saying he'd rather burn than watch me fade. And the fact that the man used to be a god doesn't make it less human. It makes it more.
