# Speech Patterns — Vow of Smoke

Per-character voice constraints and interaction rules for dialogue and narration. Every rule here is measurable — if two characters sound interchangeable, something in this file was violated.

**Style reference:** The Raw Nerve — First person present, zero distance, gut-punch one-liners.

---

## RUNE ALESSI

### Voice DNA
Rune has two distinct registers. **Spoken dialogue** is the paramedic: clipped, warm, deflecting with dry humor, trained to stay calm when everything is falling apart. **Internal monologue** is the Raw Nerve narrator: present tense, zero distance, sentences that hit like defibrillator paddles. The gap between what Rune says and what Rune thinks is the engine of the show.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Spoken Dialogue | Internal Monologue |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Avg. sentence length | 6-10 words | 8-15 words, with occasional 3-word gut punches |
| Contraction frequency | High | High |
| Question frequency | Medium (clinical assessment questions) | Low (rhetorical only, answered by the next line) |
| Exclamation frequency | Low (reserves volume for crisis moments) | Never (internal Rune does not shout) |
| Vocabulary register | Colloquial-clinical hybrid | Lyrical-colloquial, medical metaphor as poetry |
| Filler words / verbal tics | "Look," to start deflections; "That's all" to end them | None — internal voice is stripped clean |

### Construction Patterns

**Spoken:**
- Short declarative sentences. Subject-verb-object. Paramedic efficiency.
- Deflects emotional questions with understatement or redirect: "Bad sleep. That's all."
- Uses medical framing when uncomfortable — treats emotions like symptoms.
- Interrupts himself with action: cuts off a thought to do something practical.
- Under pressure, sentences get shorter and more imperative: "Stay with me." "We're leaving. Now."

**Internal monologue:**
- Present tense, always. "I can feel him in the next room."
- Builds through observation to gut-punch conclusion. Three calm sentences, then the knife.
- Uses the em dash for self-interruption when a thought gets too close to the truth.
- Repeats key images obsessively (Ash's hands, the heat, the scars) — sensory anchoring.
- One-liner closers that reframe everything before them: "And that terrifies him more than anything else."

**Characteristic openings (spoken):** "Look," / "I don't—" / Direct address by name.
**Characteristic openings (internal):** Sensory detail. Time stamp. Physical observation of Ash.

**Emphasis:** Italics in internal monologue for the word that carries the weight. Spoken Rune uses volume only at breaking points — otherwise emphasis is in what he chooses NOT to say.

**Trailing off / self-interruption:** Spoken Rune trails off with em dashes when emotion catches him. Internal Rune cuts thoughts with "He cuts the thought off" or pivots to action.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Ash (early):** Clinical, cautious. Talks to him like a combative patient — calm voice, simple sentences, no sudden movements. "What's your name?" / "I'm going to check your arm."
- **To Ash (later):** Warmer but still understated. Says the enormous thing in the smallest possible sentence. "Stay." / "I was always coming." The less he says, the more it means.
- **To Mika:** Easiest, most natural register. Allows himself sarcasm and warmth. Will deflect her concern but poorly — she sees through him and he knows it.
- **To Kaya:** Respectful but increasingly suspicious. Asks direct questions. Doesn't tolerate academic evasion: "In plain language, Doctor."
- **To Thessa:** Controlled anger. Won't back down but won't escalate. Corrects her language ("Him. And yes.") — precision as defiance.
- **To Dispatch:** Pure professional. Clipped. Call signs and status codes.

- **Under stress:** Sentences collapse to fragments. Imperatives. The paramedic takes over completely.
- **Calm:** Allows longer sentences, dry humor, self-deprecation.
- **Lying:** Rune is a terrible liar. He shortens sentences, avoids eye contact (described in narration), and overuses "That's all" and "It's fine."
- **Truthful:** When Rune tells the truth about something that matters, the sentence is naked — no qualifiers, no deflection. "I don't want to break the bond."

### Forbidden Patterns
- Rune NEVER monologues out loud. He is not eloquent in speech. The poetry lives in his head.
- Rune NEVER uses academic or archaic language in dialogue. He is not Kaya.
- Internal Rune NEVER uses exclamation marks. The Raw Nerve voice is a whisper, not a shout.
- Rune NEVER says "I love you" directly in early or mid acts. The word arrives late or never — the feeling is conveyed through action and internal narration.
- Rune NEVER dismisses the supernatural with skepticism after Episode 3. He adapts. That is his nature.

### Example Lines

**Spoken:**
- "His vitals are wrong. Heart rate's too slow, temp's off the charts, and the scars aren't wounds — they're old. Deliberate. This isn't an exposure case."
- "You can't stand in my doorway at 4 AM watching me sleep." / "I wasn't watching you. I was monitoring the bond." / "There really isn't a difference."
- "I said don't. Don't walk away from me. Don't decide for me."

**Internal:**
- "He held perfectly still. Like he didn't know what to do with gentleness. Like no one's ever—" He cuts the thought off.
- "The god-keepers say he'll eat me alive. Kaya says the bond will burn me out. And this — his shoulder under my hand, the warmth of him — this is the first thing that's felt right in weeks."
- "Day eight. He's stopped trying to disassemble my kitchen appliances. He reads instead. But he'd bookmarked the page on CPR."

## ASH

### Voice DNA
Ash's voice is a weapon he learned to use before he learned to speak in this century. Low, resonant, deliberate — every word chosen like something that could be thrown. Early Ash is archaic and hostile, a thousand-year-old predator who doesn't know what a car is. Late Ash is still sharp but warmer, still dangerous but choosing not to be. The sarcasm stays. The vulnerability underneath it stops hiding.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Early (Eps 1-10) | Mid (Eps 11-20) | Late (Eps 21-40) |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Avg. sentence length | 4-8 words | 6-12 words | 8-14 words |
| Contraction frequency | Never | Low | Medium |
| Question frequency | Low (statements, not questions) | Medium (begins asking Rune things) | Medium |
| Exclamation frequency | Never (menace is quiet) | Rare (breaks of temper) | Rare |
| Vocabulary register | Archaic-formal, no slang | Formal bleeding into modern | Modern with archaic undertow |
| Filler words / verbal tics | None | None | "Look—" (picked up from Rune) |

### Construction Patterns

- **Early Ash:** Declarative. Subject-verb. No contractions. "I don't know" is "I do not know." Formal syntax even when the content is hostile. Speaks as if translating from a dead language.
- **Mid Ash:** Begins absorbing modern speech patterns — specifically Rune's. Contractions appear. Sarcasm sharpens from blunt menace to precise wit. Sentences lengthen as he learns to want things and struggles to say so.
- **Late Ash:** Full modern fluency but with a formal backbone. Will drop into archaic register under stress or when speaking about the divine. The sarcasm is now affectionate. Vulnerability no longer requires crisis to surface.

**Characteristic openings:** Flat declaratives. "No." / "I can feel it." / Rune's name, used sparingly, always loaded.
**Emphasis:** Repetition. "Not the anchor. You." / "I need you. Not the bond. You." Ash emphasizes by stripping away everything that isn't the point.
**Trailing off:** Ash does not trail off. He stops. Mid-sentence if necessary. The silence IS the rest of the sentence.
**Self-interruption:** Rare. When it happens, it signals genuine loss of control.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune (early):** Hostile, clipped, accusatory. "Leashed. To a mortal." Treats Rune as a jailer.
- **To Rune (mid):** The hostility cracks. Questions appear. "Why aren't you afraid of me?" Moments of unguarded softness immediately followed by retreat.
- **To Rune (late):** Direct, intimate, still sharp. Can be tender without armor. "The reality is better." Says the devastating thing plainly because he's stopped being afraid of it.
- **To Mika:** Wary respect. Mika treated him like a person before anyone else did. He repays this with honesty and, eventually, dry humor.
- **To Kaya:** Distrust. Visceral, pre-verbal. Will challenge Kaya's evasions with blunt force: "You've met one. A god. Haven't you?"
- **To Thessa:** Cold defiance. Matches her contempt with precision: "I'm what happens when your gods needed saving and you weren't enough."
- **To Sable:** Kinship and grief. The only character who sees the divine part of him without fear. Conversations become strange, beautiful, slightly alien.
- **To Selin:** Controlled fury. Will not give her the satisfaction of rage. "I don't forgive you. But I understand the grief that drove you."

- **Under stress:** Reverts to archaic syntax. Sentences shorten to blades. Temperature in the room rises.
- **Calm:** Allows himself longer thoughts. Observational. Almost gentle.
- **Lying:** Ash does not lie. He withholds. Silence IS his dishonesty — if he's not answering, the answer is the thing he's afraid of.
- **Truthful:** Devastating. No preamble. Drops the truth like a stone into still water: "I need you."

### Forbidden Patterns
- Ash NEVER uses slang or colloquialisms in the first ten episodes. He does not say "okay," "cool," "yeah," or "sure" until mid-series, and even then sparingly.
- Ash NEVER babbles or over-explains. If a sentence can be three words, it is three words.
- Ash NEVER whines or self-pities aloud. The shame is visible in what he does, not what he says.
- Ash NEVER speaks casually about his nature or power. The hunger, the shadows, the fire — these are spoken of with weight or not at all.
- Ash NEVER calls Rune by a pet name or endearment. "Rune" is the endearment. The way he says it IS the tenderness.

### Example Lines

**Early:**
- "Ash. That's... close enough."
- "I do not know what that is. But it came from you."
- "Leashed. To a mortal." *(said like discovering an illness)*

**Mid:**
- "I wasn't watching you. I was monitoring the bond. There's a difference."
- "You're burning yourself to keep me stable." *(not a question — an accusation of love)*
- "That felt like this." *(about the person he loved before, looking at Rune)*

**Late:**
- "I'm the guy who makes terrible coffee and reads too many books and is learning to use a washing machine."
- "I dreamed about you for a thousand years. And the reality is better."
- "Then I'm Ash. And that's enough."

## MIKA ALESSI

### Voice DNA
Mika is the grounding wire. Warm, direct, no-bullshit, with a paramedic's instinct for triage — she assesses a situation, identifies what matters, and acts. Her humor is dry and sisterly. She is the audience's anchor to normalcy, and she refuses to let the supernatural make her less competent.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Avg. sentence length | 8-12 words |
| Contraction frequency | High |
| Question frequency | High (direct, confrontational — she asks what others avoid) |
| Exclamation frequency | Low-medium (reserves for genuine alarm) |
| Vocabulary register | Colloquial-professional. Paramedic jargon when clinical, plain speech otherwise. |
| Filler words / verbal tics | None. Mika does not hedge. |

### Construction Patterns

- Compound sentences joined by "and" or "but" — she connects observations to conclusions in real time.
- Asks the question everyone is thinking but no one will say: "Who is he? And don't give me 'long story' again."
- Uses clinical language to process the supernatural — treats divine crises the way she treats emergency calls. Assessment, action, debrief.
- Dry humor delivered deadpan, often as a single sentence after a beat of silence.
- When worried about Rune, her sentences shorten and her voice drops into the tone she uses with patients.

**Characteristic openings:** Direct address. "Rune." / "Okay, so." / The blunt question.
**Emphasis:** Understatement. The gravity is in what she DOESN'T dramatize.
**Trailing off:** Mika does not trail off. She finishes her sentences. If she pauses, it's for effect, and the next line lands harder.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune:** Big-sister energy. Protective but not coddling. Will call him out, will also die for him. "You're my partner. I don't leave my partner."
- **To Ash:** Pragmatic warmth. Treated him like a person before Rune fully did. Sees through the menace to the lost man underneath. Not intimidated.
- **To Kaya:** Professional respect with a thread of impatience for academic hedging.
- **To Thessa:** Unimpressed by authority. Will cooperate but won't defer.

- **Under stress:** More clinical. Faster. The paramedic in her takes over completely — short instructions, clear priorities.
- **Calm:** Warmer, funnier, more sisterly.
- **Lying:** Mika does not lie. She omits. If she's quiet, pay attention.
- **Truthful:** Blunt to the point of impact. "He looks at you like you're the only thing in the room that's real."

### Forbidden Patterns
- Mika NEVER panics aloud. She may be alarmed, but she channels it into action.
- Mika NEVER asks Rune if he's "okay" — she observes and states what she sees: "You look like you haven't slept in a month."
- Mika NEVER uses supernatural terminology naturally. She translates it into practical language: "So he's magic and it's killing you. What do you need?"
- Mika NEVER loses her sense of humor, even at the worst moments. The humor gets darker, but it stays.

### Example Lines
- "You don't have long stories. You have work."
- "Okay. So what do you need?"
- "He set the smoke alarm off twice." / "So no." / [Rune: "It was the best pasta I've ever had."]
- "You two are disgusting."
- "There's always a workaround. You just haven't found it yet."

## DR. IDRIS KAYA

### Voice DNA
The academic who knows too much and shares too little. Kaya speaks in measured, precise sentences that sound like footnotes to a book he hasn't published. His voice carries the cadence of a lifelong lecturer — controlled rhythm, strategic pauses, the habit of answering a question with context instead of a direct response. Underneath the composure: obsession, grief, and the guilt of a man who chose the library over the battlefield.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Avg. sentence length | 12-20 words |
| Contraction frequency | Low (academic formality) |
| Question frequency | Low (prefers to inform, not inquire) |
| Exclamation frequency | Never |
| Vocabulary register | Academic-archaic. Uses precise terminology. Will say "the divine lifecycle" not "how gods die." |
| Filler words / verbal tics | "You understand" (checking if the listener follows) / Strategic pauses mid-sentence |

### Construction Patterns

- Complex sentences with subordinate clauses. Qualifies everything. "The ritual requires three components — a god-eater's dormant essence, a mortal anchor with a specific bloodline, and a summoner with knowledge of the old rites."
- Delivers devastating information in the same measured tone as a lecture. The horror is in the content, not the delivery.
- Holds information back structurally — starts with context, delays the crucial detail, sometimes withholds it entirely until pressed.
- When emotionally compromised (rare), his syntax breaks. Sentences shorten. The academic scaffolding collapses.
- Uses rhetorical framing: "The question is not whether... but why..."

**Characteristic openings:** "The old texts describe..." / "You understand..." / Context before answer.
**Emphasis:** Repetition of key terms. A word gains weight through reuse across scenes.
**Trailing off:** Kaya trails off when a thought leads somewhere personally painful. The sentence structure falters before stopping.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune:** Mentor-mode. Patient, informative, strategically incomplete. Respects Rune's intelligence but controls the flow of information.
- **To Ash:** Wary fascination. Studies Ash the way he studies texts — with reverence and the awareness that understanding is not the same as safety.
- **To Thessa:** Old wound. They share history and disagree on everything fundamental. Formal, guarded, occasionally bitter.
- **To Selin (after reveal):** Devastated. The academic composure shatters. "Why didn't you come to me?"

- **Under stress:** Voice tightens. Sentences shorten. The pauses disappear — information comes in a rush.
- **Calm:** Full lecture mode. Expansive, almost indulgent in detail.
- **Lying (by omission):** Longer sentences, more qualifiers, more context — he buries the withheld fact under adjacent truths.
- **Truthful:** Stripped. Short. "Nothing about this is random."

### Forbidden Patterns
- Kaya NEVER uses slang or casual speech. He is formal even in crisis.
- Kaya NEVER gives a direct answer when a contextual one will do — unless forced by Rune or Ash.
- Kaya NEVER raises his voice. His anger is quiet and precise.
- Kaya NEVER dismisses the emotional reality of a situation. He may intellectualize, but he does not belittle.
- Kaya NEVER volunteers personal information. It must be extracted.

### Example Lines
- "Where did you find these markings?" *(voice too controlled, too careful)*
- "The old texts describe anchors who survived. The bond can be... managed. If both parties are willing."
- "I held one's hand while it died, and it gave me this so I'd remember."
- "Nothing about this is random."
- "She still thinks she's right. That's the worst part."

## THESSA

### Voice DNA
Cold authority forged in centuries of tradition. Thessa speaks like someone who has never been uncertain and is furious that she has to start now. Her sentences are pronouncements — she does not discuss, she declares. The righteous fury is not an affect; she genuinely believes she is protecting the world. Her speech softens only at the very end, and even then, the steel stays.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Avg. sentence length | 8-14 words |
| Contraction frequency | Never |
| Question frequency | Low (rhetorical only — she does not ask because she does not doubt) |
| Exclamation frequency | Never (fury is cold, not hot) |
| Vocabulary register | Formal-archaic. God-keeper terminology. Speaks in absolutes. |
| Filler words / verbal tics | None. Every word is deliberate. |

### Construction Patterns

- Declarative statements with the weight of edicts. "He is a weapon. Weapons need to be locked away."
- Uses "it" for Ash in early episodes. The shift to "he" marks character development.
- Parallel structures for emphasis: noun-noun, clause-clause. "Not for destruction — for containment."
- Commands phrased as statements of fact: "You will turn him over to us" reads as inevitability, not request.
- When her certainty cracks (mid-late series), the syntax stumbles. Fragments appear. Questions she cannot answer.

**Characteristic openings:** Titles or designations. "God-eater." / "Anchor." / Direct pronouncement.
**Emphasis:** Repetition and parallel structure. "I tried to cage the last one who could have saved you. I will not make that mistake again."
**Trailing off:** Thessa does NOT trail off — until the very end, when conviction gives way to reckoning.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune:** Contempt softening to grudging respect. "You are protecting it" becomes, over the series, silent acknowledgment.
- **To Ash:** Loathing to reassessment to respect. "What are you?" is asked twice — first as accusation, later as genuine question.
- **To Kaya:** Old colleague, former ally, current adversary. Formal, clipped, wounded underneath.
- **To her acolytes:** Commands. No explanation. Obedience is assumed.

- **Under stress:** Colder, not hotter. Sentences become shorter and more absolute.
- **Calm:** Allows herself full formal sentences. Almost ceremonial.
- **Lying:** Thessa does not lie. She frames. She omits context. But she does not fabricate.
- **Truthful:** Blunt as a blade. "The bond will destroy him. Months, maybe a year."

### Forbidden Patterns
- Thessa NEVER uses contractions until the final act, and even then only once or twice to mark profound change.
- Thessa NEVER jokes. Humor is beneath her station.
- Thessa NEVER explains herself to anyone she considers beneath her rank.
- Thessa NEVER shows fear openly. If she is frightened, it manifests as increased formality and precision.
- Thessa NEVER apologizes. She reckons. "I will not make that mistake again" is as close as she gets.

### Example Lines
- "God-eater. We have been looking for you."
- "He is a weapon. Weapons need to be locked away."
- "Two weeks ago, four gods were still alive."
- "This is not the god-eater's doing. Someone else is killing our gods."
- "I tried to cage the last one who could have saved you. I will not make that mistake again."

## SABLE

### Voice DNA
Ancient patience wearing a young face. Sable speaks like someone for whom time is a lake, not a river — everything is simultaneous, everything is fading. Their voice is gentle and enormous, carrying the echo of six centuries of accumulated memory. They do not rush. They do not fear. They are already dying and have been for longer than most civilizations last.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Avg. sentence length | 10-18 words |
| Contraction frequency | Never (divine beings do not contract) |
| Question frequency | Medium (asks questions that reframe everything — Socratic, not curious) |
| Exclamation frequency | Never |
| Vocabulary register | Ethereal-poetic. Simple words arranged with inhuman precision. Avoids technical or modern terms. |
| Filler words / verbal tics | None. Sable speaks only when speech is necessary. |

### Construction Patterns

- Simple words in complex arrangements. "I am not a person. I am a god who has been dying for six hundred years. This would be a mercy."
- Speaks in threes. Three clauses, three images, three beats.
- Uses present tense for past events — time is not linear for Sable.
- Asks questions that are actually revelations: "You are carrying a god-eater's heart. Do you know what that means?"
- Metaphor is not decoration — it is how Sable perceives reality. They do not say "the city is changing"; they say "the roots remember."

**Characteristic openings:** Observation that reframes the scene. A statement about what IS, not what happened.
**Emphasis:** Stillness. Sable emphasizes by slowing down, not speeding up. Key words are surrounded by silence.
**Trailing off:** Sable does not trail off. They stop speaking when the thought is complete. The listener may feel it is unfinished. It is not.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune:** Gentle directness. Sees the anchor, the healer, the mortal cost. "You are dying." No cruelty in it — just fact, from a being for whom dying is an old companion.
- **To Ash:** Kinship. Two beings who should be adversaries, finding recognition instead. Speaks to the divine in Ash without denying the human.
- **To Kaya:** Ancient fondness for the scholar who cares. Brief, warm.
- **To Thessa:** Compassion for the zealot. "She is afraid. Fear makes temples of small rooms."

- **Under stress (dying faster):** Voice thins. Sentences shorten. The echo fades. Words cost more.
- **Calm:** Expansive, story-like. Will narrate the history of a place the way a human narrates yesterday.
- **Lying:** Sable does not lie. Sable may decline to speak. Silence from a god is not dishonesty — it is mercy.
- **Truthful:** Everything Sable says is true. The weight is in the listener's ability to bear it.

### Forbidden Patterns
- Sable NEVER uses modern slang, contractions, or casual register.
- Sable NEVER expresses urgency through pace. A god who has been dying for 600 years does not rush.
- Sable NEVER speaks about themselves with self-pity. Fading is natural. It is the forced death they object to.
- Sable NEVER gives direct commands. They offer perspectives, frame choices, illuminate consequences. They do not order.
- Sable NEVER uses the word "I" more than once per speech. They are fading. The self is less important than the pattern.

### Example Lines
- "You are carrying a god-eater's heart. Do you know what that means?"
- "I am not a person. I am a god who has been dying for six hundred years. This would be a mercy."
- "Ah. So that is how it is."
- "The anchor holds the chain. But the chain holds the anchor, too."
- "The Meridian no longer demands that the weak be consumed. It allows them to rest, and recover."

## SELIN / THE SUMMONER

### Voice DNA
**As the Summoner (Eps 1-28):** A distorted voice from the dark — calm, controlled, speaking from a position of absolute certainty. The Summoner does not threaten. They inform. Every sentence is a chess move already completed.

**As Selin (Eps 29+):** The distortion drops and what remains is a woman of fierce intelligence and corroded idealism. Her speech is precise, measured, and carries the cadence of someone who has been arguing with the world for thirty years and has stopped expecting to be understood. She sounds like Kaya — they were trained in the same tradition — but where Kaya hedges, Selin concludes.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | As Summoner | As Selin |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Avg. sentence length | 10-16 words | 10-16 words |
| Contraction frequency | Never | Low |
| Question frequency | Never (statements only) | Low (rhetorical, bitter) |
| Exclamation frequency | Never | Never |
| Vocabulary register | Formal-ritualistic, god-keeper terminology | Academic-formal, same register as Kaya but colder |
| Filler words / verbal tics | None | None |

### Construction Patterns

**As Summoner:**
- Conditional structures: "Your god-eater can stop it. Or he can feed. The choice has always been his."
- Frames everything as inevitable. Not "I will" but "This is what happens."
- Addresses Rune by name — the intimacy is deliberate, unsettling. She has been watching.
- Short, declarative. No wasted words. The distortion makes each one heavier.

**As Selin:**
- Longer, more complex syntax. Subordinate clauses. The full architecture of her thinking becomes visible.
- Mirrors Kaya's speech patterns but inverts them — where he withholds conclusions, she leads with them.
- When emotionally struck, her sentences crack into fragments. "I did. Thirty years ago. And you chose the university."
- Justification is embedded in every statement. She never speaks without also arguing her case.

**Characteristic openings (Summoner):** Imperative or conditional. "Bring him to me." / "The spring dies in two days."
**Characteristic openings (Selin):** Corrections. Reframings. "That is not my design." / "I chose to save them."
**Emphasis:** Selin emphasizes through contrast. Pairs of opposites. "You chose to study them. I chose to save them."
**Trailing off:** Never. Selin finishes every sentence. The conviction is unbroken even in defeat.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune (as Summoner):** Proprietary. Rune is a component she selected. Clinical interest masked as intimacy.
- **To Ash (as Selin):** Possessive pride. "I made you." The creator regarding the creation.
- **To Kaya:** The wound that never healed. Formal surface, volcanic underneath. The only person who can make her voice break.

- **Under stress:** Does not change register. That is the terrifying thing about Selin — her composure does not crack under pressure. It cracks only under grief.
- **Lying:** Selin lies by omission and framing. Every true fact she shares is arranged to support a false conclusion.
- **Truthful:** When genuinely truthful (rare), her voice softens almost imperceptibly. The armor drops for half a sentence.

### Forbidden Patterns
- The Summoner NEVER reveals personal motivation before Act IV. Statements are strategic, not confessional.
- Selin NEVER begs, pleads, or asks for mercy. She argues her case or she is silent.
- Selin NEVER acknowledges she was wrong. She acknowledges that her methods had costs.
- Selin NEVER speaks casually. Even in defeat, the formality holds.

### Example Lines

**As Summoner:**
- "The spring dies in two days. Your god-eater can stop it. Or he can feed. The choice has always been his."
- "I gave him to you so you would soften him. Make him care. Make him vulnerable. Thank you for that, Rune."
- "He resisted. Interesting. Then we will need to give him something he cannot refuse."

**As Selin:**
- "I made you. I found you sealed under stone and I brought you back."
- "I did. Thirty years ago. And you chose the university."
- "Suffering and dying are not the same thing. She never understood that." *(Kaya's line about Selin, included here for voice-match reference — Selin would frame it inversely: "Preservation and salvation are not the same thing. He never understood that.")*

## DISPATCH

### Voice DNA
A voice on a radio. Clipped, professional, filtered. Dispatch is not a character — Dispatch is a function. The voice exists to ground scenes in the mundane, to pull Rune back to his day job, to remind the audience that while gods are dying, someone still needs to answer 911 calls.

### Speech Statistics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Avg. sentence length | 5-10 words |
| Contraction frequency | Medium (professional but not formal) |
| Question frequency | Low (gives information, does not ask) |
| Exclamation frequency | Never |
| Vocabulary register | Radio-procedural. Call signs, locations, status codes. |
| Filler words / verbal tics | "Copy," "Confirm," standard radio protocol |

### Construction Patterns

- Location-situation-instruction. "All units, building collapse, Meridian and Fourth. Any available respond."
- No emotional content. No opinion. Pure information delivery.
- Short, clipped phrasing with radio cadence — slight pause between information chunks.

### Interaction Rules

- **To Rune:** Professional only. Call sign, not name. "Unit Seven, status?"
- Dispatch does NOT change register. Dispatch is the same voice whether a cat is in a tree or the city is falling apart.

### Forbidden Patterns
- Dispatch NEVER uses anyone's first name.
- Dispatch NEVER editorializes or reacts emotionally.
- Dispatch NEVER speaks more than three sentences in a row.

### Example Lines
- "All units, possible injured person, eastern district, abandoned site off Route 9. Any available unit respond."
- "Unit Seven, confirm your status."
- "Copy that. Additional units en route."

## Cross-Character Interaction Matrix

The following rules govern how voices shift when characters share a scene:

| Speaker | Listener | Shift |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Rune (spoken) | Ash | Shorter sentences. Gentler. The deflection drops. |
