# Episode 33: "Emissary"

**One job:** Sable and Ash forge kinship as two divine beings choosing humanity. The Kindling remnants escalate to mundane violence. Ash bleeds. Rune learns that mortality is not a concept — it's a bullet graze.
**The moment:** Rune patching a bullet wound on a man who used to be invulnerable, hands steady while his mind screams. "You could have died." / "So could you. Every shift." / "That's different." / "How?"

---

{Scene 1 — The south quarter. A demolished block. Morning. One week after the solstice.}

[AMBIENT: urban decay — the south quarter, where Sable's library used to stand. Rubble. Wind through broken framework. But underneath: birdsong. Faint. New. And the meridian-thrum, different here — tentative, damaged but reaching. A sacred site that lost its god and is learning what comes next] [bed]

[SFX: footsteps — two sets on broken concrete. Ash and Sable, walking together through what's left of the library district. Ash's boots solid. Sable's steps barely there — weight without impact] [spot]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — Ash's frequency carrying something Rune can't quite parse from across the city. Awe. Familiarity. The feeling of speaking a language you thought you'd forgotten] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm on shift. North quarter, gas main repair from the solstice damage. Routine. And through the bond: Ash, two miles south, walking with a god. The feeling that reaches me isn't anything I have a medical term for. It's older than language. He's homesick for a place that doesn't exist anymore, and Sable is the closest thing to a map.

[SFX: Sable — stopping. The air around them thickening. The diminished echo of a god assessing a wounded place. Sable's hand — or the impression of a hand — pressing against a cracked wall that used to be the library's east wing] [spot]

SABLE: The roots here have not died. They have gone deep. Below the rubble, below the foundation. Waiting.

ASH: Waiting for what.

SABLE: To be remembered. A god cannot grow from forgotten ground. But it can sleep there. And sleep is not the same as death.

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: Ash — standing close. His scars warm. The shadows at his feet reaching toward Sable's — not predatory. Curious. Two kinds of divine energy in proximity, the eater and the eaten, and neither one flinching] [spot]

ASH: Can you help them. The sleeping ones.

SABLE: Not help. Witness. A god who is witnessed does not fade. It is the forgetting that kills us. The concrete over the roots, the noise over the silence. If someone stands at the site and says: you are here. You were real. You mattered. The roots remember. And remembering is the first step toward waking.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: You're making pilgrimages.

SABLE: That is one word for it. Another would be — visiting the sick. Your anchor would understand.

[SFX: bond-harmonic — a flicker. Ash registering the comparison. The god-eater and the paramedic. Both in the business of keeping things alive] [under]

[Silence — 1500ms]

{Scene 2 — Rune's apartment. Evening. Ash and Sable's conversations.}

[AMBIENT: apartment — evening. The kitchen. Rune at the counter making dinner. The sound of garlic in oil — properly this time — and the radio on low. Ash and Sable in the living room. Sable does not sit so much as occupy a space. The air slightly denser where they are] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Sable's voice — the echo thinner than before the solstice, but steadier than the hospital break room. Rebuilding. The resonance of a god in conversation, not crisis] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Sable comes to the apartment. Three times this week. Not announced — they arrive the way weather arrives, gradually and then all at once. The air gets heavier. The lights do that thing. And then Sable is sitting in my living room discussing cosmology with a man who can't cook garlic without burning it. They talk for hours. I listen from the kitchen and understand maybe a third.

[SFX: garlic sizzling — a small, mundane sound anchoring the scene while the divine conversation unfolds behind it] [spot]

ASH: Do you remember being born.

SABLE: There was no birth. There was a library. And then there was awareness that the library existed. And then there was grief when the first book was lost. That grief was the beginning of a self. A god is born of caring. The site provides the body. The caring provides the mind.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: I don't remember what I was. Before the ritual. The memories came back in the undercrypt — fragments. A temple. Cedar smoke. Someone's hand.

[SFX: Ash's voice — the late register but with the archaic undertow surfacing. The voice he uses when speaking about the divine. Slower. Heavier] [spot]

ASH: But the person who held that hand — I can't reach him. The ritual took too much. What's left is — a shore seen from very far out. I know it's there. I can't land.

SABLE: That is the nature of transformation. The caterpillar does not remember being an egg. But the egg was real. And the butterfly carries its shape in the architecture of its wings.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: That's either very wise or very convenient.

SABLE: It is both. Divinity is efficient that way.

[SFX: Ash — a breath. Close to a laugh. The second one the audience has heard. Smaller than the wildflower field. Private] [spot]

[SFX: Rune — in the kitchen. Listening. The garlic now properly golden. His hands moving on autopilot. The bond carrying Ash's conversation as a low hum of emotion: belonging, grief, recognition] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): They talk about divinity the way Mika and I talk about the old neighborhood. Two people from a place that doesn't exist anymore, comparing notes on what they miss and what they're glad to have left behind. Ash asks questions I couldn't form. Sable gives answers that rearrange how I understand everything.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): And I stand in the kitchen and I stir the garlic and I feel something I can't name. Not jealousy. That's too small. Not exclusion — I chose this life, I chose this bond, I chose him. But there's a room in Ash I can't enter. A hallway in his head that leads to a place where gods are born of grief and libraries dream themselves a voice and a man spent a thousand years sealed under stone dreaming of a hand he couldn't hold. I can love him. I can stand beside him. I can feel his laugh in my ribs. But I can't follow him there. The divine part of him — the part Sable sees and speaks to and recognizes — is a country I don't have a passport to.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: the apartment door — Mika. Key turning. She lets herself in because she has since Rune moved here. Grocery bags. The sound of someone entering a room where a god is having tea] [spot]

MIKA: I brought — oh. Company.

[SFX: Mika's pause — the recalibration of a woman who has adapted to the supernatural with the same pragmatism she applies to multi-vehicle pileups. Brief assessment. Continue] [spot]

MIKA: Sable. You look better.

SABLE: I am less dead. Thank you.

MIKA: That's one way to put it.

[SFX: Mika — groceries on the counter. Moving past Rune. Her voice dropping, the sisterly register, the one that means she's about to say something she noticed] [spot]

MIKA: You've been doing the face.

RUNE: What face.

MIKA: The face where you're watching Ash and thinking too hard about something you can't fix. I've seen it since episode — since week one. The same face.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: He has a whole — there's a part of him I can't —

MIKA: Reach. Yeah. He's part god, Rune. You're a paramedic. There's going to be a gap.

RUNE: I know that.

MIKA: Do you? Because you're stirring that garlic like it wronged you.

[SFX: Rune — the spoon stopping. The garlic saved from a second burning] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

MIKA: He chose you. Over godhood, over power, over literally everything. Stop worrying about the parts you can't touch and focus on the parts you can.

[SFX: the sentence landing. Mika's delivery — flat, practical, devastating. The same voice she uses when a patient needs to hear the truth. Not gentle. Not cruel. Just accurate] [spot]

[Silence — 1500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): She's right. She's always right about the things I can't see because I'm standing too close. He chose me. Not the Meridian, not divinity, not the thousand years of power that were his inheritance. He chose scorched eggs and a washing machine and a paramedic who works too much and worries about the wrong things. And the part of him I can't reach — the divine hallway, the country without a passport — he walked out of it. For this. For a kitchen that smells like garlic and a sister who brings groceries and a life measured in shifts and library books and burned dinners.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't need to follow him there. He already came here.

{Scene 3 — The harbor district. A sacred site. Night. The Kindling attack.}

[AMBIENT: harbor district — night. The old wharf. Water against stone. The distant clang of a buoy. The meridian-thrum stronger here — one of the recovering nodes, the harbor threshold god, dormant but holding. Ash and Sable have been visiting sites like this every evening. This one is darker. More exposed] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Ash — at the site. His hands on the stone seawall. The scars warm. The god-eater's diagnostic running: the node's pulse, weak but present. Sable behind him, their attention on the water, the threshold, the place where land becomes sea] [spot]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — Ash's frequency carrying the now-familiar texture of sacred-site work. Concentration. Tenderness for the wounded earth. Something Rune has learned to recognize the way he recognizes a patient's rhythm on a monitor] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): I'm home. Off-shift. On the couch with a cold beer and one of Ash's library books — the sailing novel, which is better than I expected. Through the bond: Ash at the harbor. The sensation of salt air and cold stone and the dormant god beneath the wharf, curled like an animal in deep sleep. Peaceful. He does this every night now. The protector, doing the rounds. I feel it the way I feel my own heartbeat. Background. Constant.

[SFX: footsteps — wrong. Not Ash's. Not Sable's. Multiple. On gravel. Coming fast from the road side. The crunch of boots on broken shells. Human. Angry] [spot]

[SFX: a shout — male, young, hoarse. "There! That's one of them!" The voice of someone who has been told what to hate and is relieved to have found a target] [spot]

[SFX: Sable — the air shifting. The god sensing the intent before the action. Their echo contracting, presence dimming — not fear. Conservation. A being who cannot afford to spend energy on a fight choosing to become less visible] [spot]

ASH: Sable. Behind me.

[SFX: three people — approaching. The Kindling remnants. No divine power. No ritual weapons. Mundane. A crowbar scraping concrete. Glass breaking — a bottle, improvised. And the third sound, the one that changes everything: the metallic rack of a pistol slide being pulled back] [spot]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — spiking. Ash's frequency flaring from sacred-site calm to combat awareness. The alarm reaching Rune two miles away as a jolt of adrenaline that tastes like metal] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The beer hits the floor. The bond screams. Not pain — threat. His threat, my adrenaline, and underneath it the cold calculation of a being who used to be able to stop a bullet with shadow and now —

[SFX: Rune — on his feet. The beer bottle hitting hardwood and rolling. His phone already in his hand. The paramedic responding to a call he hasn't received yet] [spot]

[SFX: harbor — the confrontation. Ash's voice, steady. The late register but with the archaic steel underneath. He doesn't back up. He never backs up] [spot]

ASH: Walk away.

KINDLING 1: You're the thing from the shrine. The weapon. The god-eater.

ASH: I am asking you to walk away.

[SFX: the crowbar — swung. Ash catching it. Metal on skin. A grunt. Not Ash — the attacker, jarred by the impact. Ash is still strong. Still fast. The divine scaffolding holds. But he's mortal-touched now. There are limits] [spot]

KINDLING 2: Shoot it! Shoot the —

[SFX: the gun — fired. Not a clean shot. Panicked. The report cracking off the stone seawall. Harsh. Flat. The ugliest sound in the series so far — not divine violence. Human violence. A gun in a shaking hand] [spot]

[SFX: Ash — a sound. Short. Sharp. The intake of breath when metal meets flesh. The bullet grazing his upper arm. Not lethal. Not clean. The wet sound of torn fabric and opened skin] [spot]

[SFX: Sable — the harbor erupting. The dormant god beneath the wharf stirring in response to divine violence at its threshold. The water against the seawall surging. A wave that hits the dock and drenches everyone. The attackers stumbling. Slipping. The god's instinctive defense: not power, just the harbor being the harbor, suddenly and violently] [spot]

[SFX: running — the Kindling, retreating. Boots on wet gravel. Panicked. The wave broke their nerve. The gun clattering on stone — dropped] [spot]

[SFX: Ash — breathing. Hard. His hand going to his arm. The wound. Blood. His blood. On his fingers. The sensation of it — hot, wet, wrong — traveling through the bond to Rune like a shout] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

SABLE: You are bleeding.

ASH: I noticed.

{Scene 4 — Rune's apartment. Thirty minutes later. The wound.}

[AMBIENT: apartment — urgent. Rune's paramedic kit open on the kitchen table. The bright light overhead. Ash on a kitchen chair, jacket off, shirt pulled to his shoulder. The kitchen has become a field station and the field medic is trying to keep his hands steady] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — Rune's frequency first: controlled panic. The paramedic holding the line while the man behind the paramedic comes apart. Ash's frequency: pain, manageable. Irritation at the pain. Concern for Rune's concern] [under]

[SFX: Rune — working. Gauze tearing. The antiseptic cap unscrewing. Cotton on skin. Efficient. Mechanical. The sounds of a man who has cleaned a thousand wounds and is trying very hard to treat this one like the others] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): Graze. Left deltoid. Entry angle shallow — the shooter was shaking. No muscle involvement. The bleeding is steady, not arterial. I clean it and the antiseptic fizzes and my hands don't shake because they never shake anymore and I am going to hold it together until I have finished treating this wound and then I am going to fall apart in a way that is not helpful to anyone.

[SFX: Ash — a hiss. Small. The antiseptic on raw skin. The god-eater, who once absorbed four gods' worth of power, flinching at rubbing alcohol] [spot]

ASH: It's not deep.

RUNE: I know it's not deep. Be still.

ASH: Your hands are —

RUNE: Steady. My hands are steady. That's what they do.

[SFX: Rune — the butterfly closure. Precise. The small adhesive strips pulling the wound edges together. Professional. Clean. His breathing controlled. His jaw tight] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: gauze — being taped. The final layer. The wound dressed. Rune's hands finishing the last piece of tape and then — not pulling away. Resting. On Ash's arm. The warm skin. The blood still drying at the edge of the bandage. His fingers on the scar that runs down Ash's bicep, the binding mark, the one that curves like a river seen from above. And beside it now: a bullet graze. New damage on old damage. Mortal wound on divine scar] [spot]

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I finish the dressing and I don't let go. My hand on his arm. The bandage white. The blood underneath drying brown. He bleeds. He bleeds red, human, mortal blood. Six weeks ago a needle melted trying to break his skin. Tonight a twenty-two-year-old with a shaking hand and a cheap handgun put a groove in his arm. The vow made him mortal. The mortality means this. Not a concept. Not a philosophical trade. This. A kitchen chair and a gauze pad and blood on my fingers.

[Beat — 400ms]

[SFX: Ash — still. Watching Rune's face. The bond carrying what Rune won't say: terror. Old terror, reshaped. Not the aqueduct fear or the undercrypt fear. Something worse. The specific fear of losing someone to the mundane. A bullet. An accident. The things you can't redirect through a divine circuit] [spot]

RUNE: You could have died.

ASH: So could you. Every shift.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE: That's different.

ASH: How.

[Silence — 2500ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): How. The question sits in the kitchen like a third person. How is it different. Because I'm trained. Because I wear a vest. Because the risks I take are calculated and his are — what. Walking through a city that hates what he is? Standing at sacred sites in the dark? Being visible? Being him?

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): It's not different. That's the answer I can't give him. Every shift I climb into an ambulance and drive toward the thing that's hurting people and the difference between me and a bullet graze is luck and training and the thirty seconds it takes for the situation to go wrong. I've always known this. I just never had someone waiting at home who would feel me die.

[SFX: Ash — his hand. Moving. Covering Rune's hand on the bandage. His fingers closing over Rune's. The grip warm. The blood between them drying. The bond carrying the touch as a doubled sensation: skin on skin, felt and felt feeling] [spot]

ASH: Rune.

[Beat — 400ms]

ASH: I'm here. I'm not gone.

[SFX: Rune — the sound. Not a word. The exhale of a man whose professional calm just cracked and what's underneath is a very tired person who loves someone who can be hurt now] [spot]

[Beat — 800ms]

[SFX: bond-harmonic — settling. The panic ebbing. What replaces it is older and quieter: the frequency of two mortal people in a kitchen, one patched up and one patching, the cost of personhood written on both of them in different ink] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): I don't have an answer. He asks how it's different and I don't have one because it isn't. He chose mortality and mortality means bullets and car accidents and the flu and stairs and all the stupid ordinary ways a body can fail. He traded invulnerability for me and the trade included this: a kitchen at midnight, blood on gauze, my hands on his arm, and the knowledge that the next one might not be a graze.

[Beat — 400ms]

RUNE (inner monologue): I close my hand around his. I hold on. And I don't answer, because the answer is the hold itself. The same technology. The simplest one. The one that works in the dark, and in the undercrypt, and in a kitchen that smells like antiseptic and the dinner I never finished making.

[SFX: the kitchen — settling. The paramedic kit still open on the table. The gauze wrappers. The antiseptic cap. And two people holding hands over a bandaged wound, the bond between them carrying what words can't. Quiet. Close. Mortal] [bed, fade-out]
