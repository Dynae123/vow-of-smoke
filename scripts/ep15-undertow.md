# Episode 15: "Undertow"

**One job:** The spring battle. Ash faces the dying god and the hunger roars. The summoner speaks. Rune anchors him — not with control, with everything he is. Ash turns away from the god and chooses. The god speaks to Rune before it dissolves. In the aftermath, Ash takes Rune's hand in front of everyone.
**The moment:** "I am not your weapon." Ash's eyes clearing. The god-eater looking at all that power and letting it go.

{Scene 3 — The spring chamber. The choice.}

[AMBIENT: chamber — ritual chanting, divine water, the conflicting sounds of the god screaming silently and Ash's power surging] [bed]

[SFX: Rune grabbing Ash's arm — both hands, forearm to bicep, full grip. Fabric and skin and the bond blazing] [spot]

RUNE: ASH.

[SFX: bond-hum-surge — massive, deliberate, sustained. Rune pushing through the connection] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Everything Rune is, routed through the bond. The paramedic. The anchor. The man who holds people together when they're falling apart]

RUNE (inner monologue): I grab his arm and I push. Not a wall. Not a cage. I don't try to contain him — I tried that at the sinkhole and it worked but this is bigger, the power is stronger, the hunger is a tide and I am not a seawall. So I don't push against. I push through. Everything I am. Every patient I've held steady. Every hand I've gripped in the back of an ambulance when the monitors were screaming and the patient's eyes were going glassy and I said *stay with me* and meant it with my whole body. The part of me that chose this work because I could not bear to let people go alone.

[SFX: Rune's breathing stuttering. A cough. A hard swallow, a choked grimace] [spot]
[DIRECTION: The drain is worse than before. Rune's hearing drops — the chanting, the water, all of it pulling away into near-silence. Pins-and-needles erupt from his wrists to his shoulders. His legs are threatening to fold. But his hands do not let go]

RUNE (inner monologue): It costs. The sound drops out — the chanting, the water, all of it receding into a ringing silence like the moment after an explosion. Pins-and-needles swarm up my arms, metallic cold spreading from my palms into my chest. My knees buckle and I lock them. Through the bond I send the only thing that matters: *you don't have to be what they made you.*

[SFX: bond tone — two frequencies fighting for dominance. One low, vast, pulling. One higher, tighter, holding] [under]
[DIRECTION: The hunger massive and ancient. Rune's signal smaller, human, stubborn]

[Silence — 2500ms]

[SFX: Ash's breathing changing — the primal rhythm breaking. A raw sound, guttural, half gasp. The shadows pulling back, receding] [spot]

[SFX: bond tone shifting — hunger frequency dropping, replaced by a warmer, human register] [under]
[DIRECTION: Ash's voice shifts — the raw, guttural quality breaking into something human, present, his own]

ASH (raw, wrecked): I am not your weapon.

[DIRECTION: Said to the summoner. Said to the chamber. Said to the thousand-year-old ritual that made him. Said to himself]

[SFX: the ritual fracturing — chanting faltering, breaking rhythm. Carved channels flickering, the drawn light stuttering] [spot]
[DIRECTION: The chanting falters — scattered gasps, bodies shifting on stone]

THE SUMMONER (distorted): Disappointing. But not unexpected.

[SFX: the summoner's presence withdrawing — the projection cutting out. A void where the voice was] [spot]

[SFX: Ash turning — away from the god, toward the ritualists. Shadows erupting outward, fire answering. Heat blasting through the chamber] [spot]
[DIRECTION: Not the hunger — the anger, directed, purposeful]

[SFX: chamber shaking — stone dust falling from the ceiling. Water churning. The ritualists scrambling — boots on stone, panicked, robes catching, implements dropping] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The ritual breaks. The hooded figures scatter like startled birds — running for the side passages, abandoning their positions. Ash's shadows lash out and the fire follows and the chamber is suddenly full of heat and noise and chaos.

[SFX: god-keeper acolytes engaging from the side tunnels — footsteps, shouted commands, echoing pursuit] [under]

[SFX: stone chains cracking — the binding fracturing. Stone splitting. One chain. Two. Three] [spot]

RUNE (inner monologue): The stone chains on the god crack and fall. One by one. The carved bindings shattering as the ritual's structure collapses. And the god — the spring god — stands free. But it is not free. It is fading.

{Scene 4 — The spring chamber. The god speaks.}

[AMBIENT: chamber — aftermath. The chanting gone. Water still flowing but dimmer. The god's light fading. Distant sounds of pursuit in the tunnels. Stone settling] [bed, crossfade]

[SFX: Ash on his knees — heavy impact. Breathing hard. The shadows around him spent, exhausted. Bond-hum-settled — the hunger gone quiet, replaced by depletion] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The chamber settles. The ritualists are gone — scattered into the tunnel system where the god-keepers are waiting. Ash is on his knees and so am I and my hand is still on his arm because I don't know how to let go and my body is telling me in several languages that I've spent something I didn't have.

[SFX: the spring god — its light dimming. The water sound thinning. The glow from the source pulling inward, contracting] [under]

RUNE (inner monologue): The god is dying. Freed from the chains but too far gone — too much power already drawn out. The water-figure is smaller now, dimmer, the shifting light condensing into something almost solid. Almost human. A face. Eyes that are not eyes — just the impression of looking.

[Silence — 1500ms]

[SFX: the god's light flickering — a wavering pulse, dimming] [spot]

SPRING GOD: You know what he is. You do not yet know what you are becoming.

RUNE (inner monologue): The god dissolves. The spring goes dark. The water keeps flowing — it's still water, still a spring, still doing what springs do. But the light is gone. The presence is gone. The thing that made this place sacred for ten thousand years is gone and what's left is a cave with a stream and the silence where a god used to be.

[SFX: water. Stone. Dripping. The ordinary sounds of an ordinary cave] [bed]

ASH (barely audible): I did not feed.

RUNE: No. You didn't.

[Beat — 800ms]

ASH: It died anyway.

RUNE: Yeah.

ASH: Was it enough? That I did not consume it? It died regardless. The summoner's ritual had already drawn too much. My refusal changed nothing for the god.

[SFX: Rune's hand tightening on Ash's arm — firm grip, knuckles pressing] [spot]

RUNE: It changed something for you.

[SFX: bond-hum-warm — quiet, exhausted, but present] [under]
